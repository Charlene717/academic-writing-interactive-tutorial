故事開場

場景：Daniel 與三明治論文的兩難

SCENE · TAIPEI 2025

從「我該寫傳統 5 章嗎？」到「我的三篇論文各回答不同子問題」

博士第四年的 Daniel 正在規劃他的論文。三年來他發了三篇 first-author paper：第一篇在 Bioinformatics（單細胞批次效應校正方法）、第二篇在 Nature Communications（用該方法分析肝癌單細胞圖譜）、第三篇剛被 Cell Reports Methods minor revision 接受（把方法擴展到空間轉錄體）。

他卡在一個問題：要寫成傳統 5 章論文（Ch1 緒論、Ch2 文獻、Ch3 方法、Ch4 結果、Ch5 討論），還是把三篇 paper 當成 Ch2–Ch4，再加 General Intro / General Discussion 的三明治論文？傳統格式對台灣口委比較熟悉，但他得把三篇 paper「拆開重組」成單一敘事——光想到就頭痛。

他敲開指導教授 陳老師的門。陳老師沒回答對錯，只說：「選擇能讓你的讀者在 30 分鐘內理解你貢獻的結構。口委要寫推薦信、未來學弟妹要拿來當文獻——他們不會逐字讀完 200 頁，他們會跳著讀。問自己：哪種架構讓他們最快找到你的貢獻？」

Daniel 回房間想了一晚。他發現三篇 paper 其實各自回答一個子問題：(1) 如何校正批次效應？(2) 校正後肝癌異質性長什麼樣？(3) 方法能否從單細胞推廣到空間？這三個子問題彼此關聯但獨立——硬塞進傳統 5 章會讓 Results 變成三個沒交集的小山。隔天他告訴陳老師：「我選 paper-based。General Intro 串接邏輯、General Discussion 寫整體貢獻、每個 paper 當一章。」陳老師點頭：「對。現在去查系上規定、找近 3 屆畢業生的 paper-based 論文，把目次抄下來當模板。」

Fourth-year PhD student Daniel is planning his thesis. Over three years he's published three first-author papers: one in Bioinformatics (a single-cell batch-correction method), one in Nature Communications (applying the method to a liver-cancer atlas), and one just received minor revision at Cell Reports Methods (extending the method to spatial transcriptomics).

He's stuck on a question: write a traditional 5-chapter thesis (Ch1 Intro, Ch2 LitRev, Ch3 Methods, Ch4 Results, Ch5 Discussion), or use the three papers as Ch2–Ch4 wrapped by a General Introduction and General Discussion — the "sandwich" thesis? Traditional format is familiar to Taiwanese committees, but he'd have to dismantle and reassemble all three papers into a single linear story. Just thinking about it gives him a headache.

He knocks on his advisor Prof. Chen's door. Chen doesn't answer right/wrong. He just says: "Pick the structure that lets your readers understand your contribution in 30 minutes. Your committee writes recommendation letters, future students will mine your thesis as background — none of them will read all 200 pages word-by-word. They will skim. Ask yourself: which structure lets them locate your contribution fastest?"

Daniel thinks all night. He realizes his three papers each answer a distinct sub-question: (1) How do we correct batch effects? (2) What does liver-cancer heterogeneity look like after correction? (3) Can the method extend from single-cell to spatial? The three sub-questions are related but independent — forcing them into a traditional 5-chapter mold would turn Results into three disjoint islands. The next morning he tells Chen: "Paper-based. General Intro stitches the logic, General Discussion synthesizes the overall contribution, each paper becomes a chapter." Chen nods: "Right. Now go check the department's rules and copy the TOCs from the last 3 paper-based theses in our group as your template."

核心詞彙

12 個關鍵術語（雙版本）

每個詞有兩個版本：🧒「給小孩看」用比喻、📐「給研究者看」給定義。

Each term has two versions: 🧒 "for kids" uses a metaphor; 📐 "for researchers" gives the formal definition.

IMRaD
IMRaD 結構
把論文拆成 4 段：為什麼做？怎麼做？發現了什麼？這代表什麼？——像漢堡有四層。
Split a paper into 4 sections: Why? How? What? So-what? — like a hamburger with four layers.
Introduction-Methods-Results-and-Discussion；Sollaci & Pereira 2004 J Med Libr Assoc 統計 >95% 生醫期刊採用；起源於 19 世紀末實驗報告體例。
Introduction-Methods-Results-and-Discussion. Sollaci & Pereira 2004 (J Med Libr Assoc) found >95% of biomedical journals use it; rooted in late-19th-century experimental-report conventions.
Hourglass structure
沙漏結構
論文上下寬、中間窄——緒論從大背景慢慢縮小到你的問題，討論再從你的結果擴回大世界。
A paper is wide-narrow-wide — Intro funnels from big background to your question; Discussion fans back out to the big world.
議題寬度 (topical width) 隨章節變化：Intro 寬→窄、Methods/Results 窄、Discussion 窄→寬。Hill 1965、Swales 1990 CARS model 形式化。
Topical width across sections: Intro wide→narrow, Methods/Results narrow, Discussion narrow→wide. Formalized by Hill 1965 and Swales 1990 (CARS model).
Sandwich thesis
三明治論文
論文像三明治：上下兩片麵包 (General Intro + General Discussion)，中間夾你發表過的 paper 當餡。
A thesis like a sandwich: General Intro and General Discussion are the bread, your published papers are the filling.
Paper-based thesis 的別稱；以 published / submitted manuscripts 構成中段章節，外加綜合性 General Intro 與 General Discussion 串接。
Synonym for paper-based thesis; middle chapters are published / submitted manuscripts, framed by a synthesizing General Intro and General Discussion.
Paper-based thesis
論文集式學位論文
把已發表的論文當成章節組合成的學位論文。
A thesis assembled from already-published papers as its chapters.
Cumulative / article-based / publication-based thesis；含 3-5 篇 first-author paper + General Intro/Discussion；歐盟 PhD 主流，台灣部分理工博班接受。
Cumulative / article-based / publication-based thesis; typically 3-5 first-author papers + General Intro/Discussion. EU PhD mainstream; accepted by some Taiwanese STEM PhD programs.
Traditional 5-chapter
傳統 5 章式
最常見的學位論文寫法：緒論、文獻、方法、結果、討論——一個故事從頭講到尾。
The most common thesis layout: Intro, LitRev, Methods, Results, Discussion — one story start to finish.
Ch1 Introduction、Ch2 Literature Review、Ch3 Methods、Ch4 Results、Ch5 Discussion (incl. Conclusion)。亞洲生醫碩士主流。
Ch1 Introduction, Ch2 Literature Review, Ch3 Methods, Ch4 Results, Ch5 Discussion (incl. Conclusion). Mainstream for Asian biomedical master's degrees.
Monograph thesis
專論式學位論文
一本「書」式的論文：一個大主題、一條主軸、從頭寫到尾。
A "book"-style thesis: one big theme, one through-line, written from start to finish.
傳統 5/6 章式的廣義稱呼；單一敘事、由作者本人完整撰寫；對比 cumulative thesis。人文社科 PhD 多採此形式。
Umbrella term for traditional 5/6-chapter format; single narrative authored entirely by the candidate; contrasts with cumulative thesis. Standard in humanities/social-science PhDs.
Cumulative thesis
累積式學位論文
paper-based 的德語區用語——「累積」就是把幾篇 paper 累起來變一本書。
German-language term for paper-based — "cumulative" means stacking several papers into one volume.
Kumulative Dissertation；德、奧、瑞、北歐 PhD 慣用語；通常要求 3 篇以上 peer-reviewed publication 或 submitted manuscript。
Kumulative Dissertation; used in Germany, Austria, Switzerland, Nordic countries; typically requires 3+ peer-reviewed or submitted manuscripts.
Doctoral vs Master's thesis
博士 vs 碩士論文
碩士：證明你「會做研究」；博士：證明你「對領域有原創、實質貢獻」。後者厚 3-5 倍。
Master's: prove you "can do research." PhD: prove "substantial original contribution to the field." PhDs are 3-5× thicker.
碩士 30-50k 字 / 60-120 頁；博士 80-200k 字 / 150-300 頁。博士須通過 viva/defense；碩士部分國家僅口試。
Master's: 30-50k words / 60-120 pages; PhD: 80-200k words / 150-300 pages. PhD requires viva/defense; master's varies by country.
Defense (viva)
口試 / 答辯
把論文成果在 3-5 位口委面前報告 + 接受質詢——通過才算畢業。
Present your thesis to 3-5 committee members + answer questions — passing means graduation.
Oral defense / viva voce；通常 60-180 分鐘；委員可要求 minor / major revision；英制 UK viva 較嚴格，可能要求 resubmission。
Oral defense / viva voce; usually 60-180 minutes; committee may require minor / major revisions; UK viva is notably stricter, possibly requiring resubmission.
Front matter
前頁件
論文正文前的所有頁面：封面、目次、致謝、摘要——讀者翻開的第一印象。
Everything before Ch1: title page, TOC, acknowledgements, abstract — your reader's first impression.
Title page、Declaration of originality、Abstract (zh+en)、Acknowledgements、Table of Contents、List of Figures/Tables、Abbreviations、Glossary。
Title page, Declaration of originality, Abstract (zh+en), Acknowledgements, Table of Contents, List of Figures/Tables, Abbreviations, Glossary.
Back matter
後頁件
論文最後的補充材料：附錄、參考文獻、補充資料——通常最厚但讀者最少看。
Supporting material at the end: appendices, references, supplements — usually the thickest part but least read.
References、Appendices (full protocols, raw data tables)、Supplementary Materials、Author publications list、Curriculum Vitae (部分學校要求)。
References, Appendices (full protocols, raw data tables), Supplementary Materials, Author publications list, Curriculum Vitae (required by some institutions).
Abstract conventions
摘要規範
摘要有兩種：結構化（分 Background/Methods/Results/Conclusions 標題）與非結構化（一段連續文字）。
Two abstract styles: structured (labeled Background/Methods/Results/Conclusions) and unstructured (single paragraph).
Structured abstract 由 Ad Hoc Working Group 1987 Ann Intern Med 提出，現為臨床期刊主流；unstructured 仍多見於基礎科學期刊；通常 150-300 字。
Structured abstract proposed by Ad Hoc Working Group 1987 (Ann Intern Med); now mainstream in clinical journals; unstructured still common in basic-science journals; usually 150-300 words.

🍳
生活化比喻

IMRaD 像料理 5 步驟：Introduction 是菜單 (why — 告訴客人為何點這道菜)、Methods 是食譜 (how — 別人能照做)、Results 是上菜 (what — 端出實際成品)、Discussion 是評論家評論 (so what — 這道菜在整個料理界的意義)。少一個環節，整桌都垮。

IMRaD is like a five-step meal: Introduction is the menu (why — telling diners why to order this dish), Methods is the recipe (how — reproducible by others), Results is the serving (what — the actual plated dish), and Discussion is the critic's review (so what — meaning in the wider culinary world). Drop any one and the whole table collapses.

逐步操作

從零開始建論文骨架的 8 步

Σ 可執行 SOP
Σ₁
選定架構：5 章傳統式 vs 6 章延伸式 vs paper-based 三明治。看你有沒有第一作者論文、學校規定、研究是否單一主題。
Pick the structure: 5-chapter traditional vs 6-chapter extended vs paper-based sandwich. Decision drivers: do you have first-author papers, school mandate, single vs multiple subprojects.
Σ₂
寫一句 Introduction 目的：「本研究的核心問題是 ____；本研究的貢獻是 ____。」這一句先寫好，整篇都會有方向。
Draft a one-sentence Intro purpose: "The central question of this study is ____; the contribution is ____." Nail this first and the whole thesis gains direction.
Σ₃
畫 Methods 骨架：列出樣本、實驗、分析、統計、軟體版本——條列就好。先求完整不求優美。
Sketch Methods skeleton: list samples, experiments, analyses, statistics, software versions — bullet form. Aim for completeness, not elegance.
Σ₄
規劃 Results：圖一張一段。先列出 5–8 張主圖各對應什麼發現，再為每張圖寫一個 topic sentence。
Plan Results figure-by-figure: list 5–8 main figures and the finding each carries, then draft one topic sentence per figure.
Σ₅
列 Discussion 6 段大綱：(1) 核心發現 summary、(2) 與文獻對比、(3) 機制解釋、(4) 臨床/領域意義、(5) limitation、(6) 未來方向。每段一個主題。
Outline Discussion in 6 paragraphs: (1) core finding summary, (2) comparison with literature, (3) mechanistic interpretation, (4) clinical / field implications, (5) limitations, (6) future directions. One topic per paragraph.
Σ₆
把參考文獻映射到章節：用 Zotero/EndNote 開 4 個 collection（Intro/Methods/Results/Disc），把每篇文獻先丟到「它會被用到的那一節」。寫作時就不會搜不到。
Map references to sections: in Zotero/EndNote create four collections (Intro/Methods/Results/Disc) and drop each ref into the section where it will be cited. Writing then never stalls on hunting refs.
Σ₇
Abstract 留到最後寫：所有節都完成後再回頭壓縮 abstract（250 字）。先寫 abstract 通常會卡，因為你還沒釐清「我到底發現什麼」。
Write the abstract last: only compress the abstract (~250 words) after everything else is done. Drafting it first usually stalls because you haven't yet crystallised "what did I actually find".
Σ₈
IMRaD 連貫性自檢：Intro 提的 gap、Methods 的對應實驗、Results 的對應數據、Discussion 的對應結論——4 個位置必須「一條線」對得起來。對不上就是斷裂。
Cross-check IMRaD coherence: the gap in Intro, the matching experiment in Methods, the supporting data in Results, and the matching conclusion in Discussion — these four anchors must trace a single straight line. Anything misaligned is a rupture.
開放思考

三題反思：結構不是教條

議題 1
Paper-based 三明治論文 vs 5 章傳統式——哪一種對學術職涯更有利？
Paper-based sandwich thesis vs 5-chapter traditional — which is better for an academic career?
支持 paper-based：畢業即有發表記錄、申請 postdoc 直接拿出來、各章已通過同儕審查。支持傳統式：敘事完整、亞洲口委更熟悉、避免章節風格不一致；對只有 1 篇論文者更安全。
For paper-based: graduation = publication record, instantly usable for postdoc applications, each chapter peer-reviewed. For traditional: cohesive narrative, more familiar to Asian committees, avoids inter-chapter style mismatch; safer if you only have one paper.
議題 2
在 AI 摘要與結構化 metadata 的時代，我們是否應該放棄 IMRaD，改用階層式 XML 標籤？
In the age of AI summarization and structured metadata, should we abandon IMRaD for hierarchical XML tagging?
支持 XML：機器更易解析、可組合不同段落、適合 modular publishing (eLife、F1000)。支持 IMRaD：人類讀者仍佔大多數、敘事邏輯是論證的根本、XML 反而讓段落變零碎缺乏推理。
For XML: machine-parsable, recomposable across articles, fits modular publishing (eLife, F1000). For IMRaD: humans still dominate, narrative logic is the core of argument, XML fragments paragraphs and strips reasoning.
議題 3
「沙漏 (hourglass)」結構——是優雅還是僵化？
The "hourglass" structure — beautiful or rigid?
優雅論：讓讀者用同一張地圖讀任何論文，認知負擔最低。僵化論：創新的研究（如方法論、推測性、田野觀察）被迫扭曲成 hourglass，反而失去原本的力量。
Elegance view: readers use the same mental map for any paper — minimal cognitive load. Rigidity view: innovative work (methods papers, speculative work, field observations) gets forced into the hourglass shape and loses its native power.
案例聚焦

兩個現實案例：模範與警鐘

🏆
模範 MODEL

Watson & Crick, 1953, Nature: "A Structure for Deoxyribose Nucleic Acid"。整篇僅約 900 字、完美 IMRaD：Intro 1 段（前人模型問題）→ Methods 內嵌（X-ray + 模型搭建）→ Results 1 段（雙螺旋幾何）→ Discussion 1 段（含著名的「It has not escaped our notice...」遺傳機制暗示）。70 年來仍是「短而震撼」的典範。

Watson & Crick, 1953, Nature: "A Structure for Deoxyribose Nucleic Acid". ~900 words, textbook IMRaD: 1-paragraph Intro (problems with prior models) → embedded Methods (X-ray + model-building) → 1-paragraph Results (double-helix geometry) → 1-paragraph Discussion (including the famous "It has not escaped our notice..." nod to the genetic mechanism). Still the gold standard for "short and seismic" 70 years on.

⚠️
警鐘 WARNING

2010 年代基因組學論文：30+ 補充章節。為了「完整呈現」，許多大型 omics 論文把 IMRaD 主文壓到 3000 字，但 Supplementary 拉到 30–60 個檔案、上百頁。讀者反而抓不到核心發現，IMRaD 的「閱讀地圖」優勢被官僚化破壞——TCGA、ENCODE 早期論文皆是此例。教訓：補充材料是為「可重現」服務，不是傾倒「沒地方放的內容」的垃圾桶。

2010s genomics papers with 30+ supplementary files. To "be complete," many large omics papers compressed the IMRaD body to ~3000 words but ballooned the Supplement to 30–60 files and hundreds of pages. Readers lost track of the core finding; IMRaD's reading-map advantage was bureaucratized away — early TCGA and ENCODE papers exemplify this. Lesson: supplements serve reproducibility, not as a dumping ground for "stuff with nowhere else to go."

常見陷阱

六個結構級陷阱

1. Methods 滲進 Results

Results 段落寫「為了測 X，我們使用 Y...」——這屬於 Methods。Results 只該寫「X 的結果是 N=...」。
A Results paragraph says "To test X, we used Y..." — that belongs in Methods. Results should only state "the X result was N=...".

2. Results 滲進 Discussion

Results 出現「This suggests / This implies」即犯規。任何推論、機制、與文獻對比都該搬到 Discussion。
"This suggests / This implies" in Results is a violation. All inference, mechanism, and literature comparison belongs in Discussion.

3. 沒有「一段一張圖」結構

Results 寫成一大塊長文，讀者抓不到「這段在講哪張圖」。每張主圖應該對應一個獨立段落，topic sentence 直接點名圖號。
Results becomes one long blob and readers cannot tell which paragraph covers which figure. Each main figure should map to a dedicated paragraph whose topic sentence names the figure.

4. 先寫 Abstract

很多人從 abstract 開寫，結果發現主文發現跟 abstract 不一致。Abstract 應該最後寫——它是全文的索引，不是大綱。
Many start drafting from the abstract and then find the body diverges. The abstract should be written last — it indexes the paper, it doesn't outline it.

5. Discussion 過度宣稱

「本研究證明 X 致病。」其實樣本只 12 例、且只是 correlation。把 "demonstrates" 改成 "is consistent with"、"suggests"，並加 limitation 段。
"Our study demonstrates that X causes disease." — but n=12 and only a correlation. Switch "demonstrates" to "is consistent with" / "suggests" and add a limitations paragraph.

6. Paper-based 缺整合章

三明治論文裡的 General Intro / General Discussion 寫得太空，章節間「拼貼感」嚴重。整合章應該明確說「三篇論文如何回答同一個大問題」。
In sandwich theses the General Intro / General Discussion is left thin and the chapters feel pasted together. The integration chapter must explicitly argue "how the three papers answer one big question."
正反對照

一句摘要：壞 vs 好

❌ 反例

「我們對 TNBC 樣本做了一些實驗與分析。」

"We did some experiments and analysis on TNBC samples."

沒方法、沒對象、沒結果、沒貢獻——IMRaD 四節全失蹤。

No method, no subjects, no result, no contribution — all four IMRaD sections are absent.

✅ 正例

「我們對 18 對化療前/後 TNBC 樣本執行 paired scRNA-seq (10x v3) (Methods)；鑑定出 3 個化療抗性相關狀態 (Results)；並討論 EMT-high 子群在抗性機制中的角色 (Discussion)。」

"We performed paired scRNA-seq (10x v3) on 18 pre/post-chemotherapy TNBC samples (Methods); identified 3 chemoresistance-associated states (Results); and discussed the role of the EMT-high subpopulation in resistance mechanisms (Discussion)."

一句話對應 IMRaD 三節各一個錨點——讀者立刻知道整篇論文長什麼樣。

One sentence anchors three IMRaD sections — the reader immediately knows the paper's shape.

自我提問

送印前的 6 個問題

1. 每一節 (I/M/R/D) 是否只負責一個角色？有沒有「Results 在解釋、Discussion 在補數據」的越界？
1. Does each IMRaD section have one—and only one—role? Any "Results explaining / Discussion adding new numbers" boundary violations?
2. 整篇是不是「沙漏」？Intro 從寬到窄、Discussion 從窄到寬，看得出來嗎？

進階補充

2024–2026 結構潮流

趨勢 AMethods / Code / Data 三節獨立化

越來越多期刊 (Nature Methods、Cell Systems、eLife) 開始要求「Code Availability」與「Data Availability」成為與 Methods 同層的獨立 section。某些期刊甚至把 Methods 拆成「Experimental Procedures」+「Computational Methods」+「Statistical Analysis」三節。背景：FAIR 原則 (Wilkinson 2016)、NIH Data Management & Sharing Policy 2023 上路。

Increasingly journals (Nature Methods, Cell Systems, eLife) require "Code Availability" and "Data Availability" as standalone sections alongside Methods. Some split Methods into "Experimental Procedures" + "Computational Methods" + "Statistical Analysis." Drivers: FAIR principles (Wilkinson 2016) and the 2023 NIH Data Management & Sharing Policy.

趨勢 BModular publishing: Cell + Cell Reports 雙投

Cell Press 從 2023 起鼓勵把同一研究切成 main paper (Cell) + companion resource paper (Cell Reports / Cell Reports Methods)。Main paper 維持 IMRaD 並聚焦核心生物發現；companion paper 專門放方法細節、benchmark、軟體。挑戰：要重新切 IMRaD，main paper Methods 要極簡（500 字以內）。

Since 2023 Cell Press encourages splitting one study into a main paper (Cell) + a companion resource paper (Cell Reports / Cell Reports Methods). Main paper stays IMRaD and focuses on biology; companion paper carries method details, benchmarks, and software. Catch: requires re-slicing IMRaD — main-paper Methods must be ultra-lean (under 500 words).

趨勢 Comics 論文的 pipeline 該放哪？

大型 omics 研究的 pipeline (Snakemake / Nextflow)、container (Docker / Apptainer) 與 workflow DAG 圖，現代慣例是放在 Methods 末段 + 圖一張 + GitHub/Zenodo + DOI。不把它寫進 Results；不單獨成節（除非期刊明文要求）。Strategy：主文一句「Full pipeline at github.com/lab/repo (Zenodo DOI: ...)」即可。

For large omics papers, the pipeline (Snakemake / Nextflow), container (Docker / Apptainer) and workflow DAG figure now belong in the last Methods paragraph + one figure + GitHub/Zenodo + DOI. Not in Results; not a standalone section (unless mandated). Strategy: one Methods line — "Full pipeline at github.com/lab/repo (Zenodo DOI: ...)" — suffices.

概覽

為什麼結構這麼重要？

IMRaD（Introduction–Methods–Results–and–Discussion）是現代生醫期刊論文的「通用合約」：作者照這個順序寫，讀者就知道在哪裡找什麼。讀者掃 Introduction 找「為什麼做這個研究」、跳到 Methods 確認「實驗設計可不可信」、看 Results 抓「具體數字」、再讀 Discussion 確認「這對我有什麼意義」。

這個合約讓讀者 30 秒內就能定位到他關心的段落——這對被引用、被同儕審查、被期刊接受都至關重要。學位論文則衍生出更多變體，因為國家、學科、學位等級不同，組織方式也不一樣。

IMRaD (Introduction–Methods–Results–and–Discussion) is the universal contract of modern biomedical journal writing: if you write in this order, the reader knows exactly where to look for what. Readers scan the Introduction for "why this study," jump to Methods to verify "is the design credible," read Results for "the actual numbers," and finally Discussion for "what this means for me."

This contract lets any reader locate the paragraph they care about in 30 seconds — crucial for citation, peer review, and journal acceptance. Theses have more variants because country, discipline, and degree level all influence how chapters are organized.

💡
核心原則：結構不是裝飾，是讓讀者「讀得快、信得過、引得到」的工具。寫之前先問：我的讀者會用什麼順序提問？答案就是你的章節順序。 Core principle: Structure is not decoration — it's the tool that makes readers read faster, trust more, and cite easier. Before writing, ask: in what order would my reader ask their questions? That order is your chapter order.
IMRaD 逐節

一、IMRaD 四節各自回答什麼問題？

IMRaD 不是隨意排序——它對應讀者腦中的四個問題。記住「沙漏 (hourglass)」形狀：Introduction 從寬到窄（領域→ gap →本研究問題）；Methods 與 Results 保持窄（只談你做了什麼、發現什麼）；Discussion 從窄到寬（你的結果→與文獻對比→領域意義）。

IMRaD isn't arbitrary — it mirrors four questions in the reader's head. Remember the hourglass: Introduction goes wide → narrow (field → gap → your question); Methods and Results stay narrow (only what you did and found); Discussion goes narrow → wide (your result → vs literature → field implications).

I

Introduction （緒論）

回答：「為什麼要做這個研究？」
定位領域 → 已知什麼 → 還不知道什麼 (gap) → 本研究問題 / 假設。形狀：寬 → 窄。

Answers: "Why this study?"
Position the field → what's known → what's missing (gap) → your question / hypothesis. Shape: wide → narrow.

M

Methods （方法）

回答：「你怎麼做的？我能不能複製？」
樣本、材料、實驗設計、統計、軟體版本——每一步都要讓另一個實驗室能照著做。形狀：窄而精確。

Answers: "How did you do it? Could I reproduce it?"
Samples, materials, design, statistics, software versions — each step must let another lab repeat it. Shape: narrow and precise.

R

Results （結果）

回答：「你發現了什麼？」
只陳述事實與數字（含圖表），不解釋、不推論。順序與 Methods 對應，每段先 topic sentence 再給數據。形狀：窄。

Answers: "What did you find?"
State facts and numbers (with figures/tables) — no interpretation, no speculation. Order should mirror Methods; each paragraph leads with a topic sentence, then data. Shape: narrow.

aD

(and) Discussion （討論）

回答：「這代表什麼？跟別人比起來如何？接下來呢？」
Key finding → 與文獻對比 → 機制解釋 → limitation → 未來方向 / 意義。形狀：窄 → 寬。

Answers: "What does it mean? How does it compare? What's next?"
Key finding → vs literature → mechanism → limitations → future directions / implications. Shape: narrow → wide.

⚠️
常見違規：(1) 在 Results 寫「This suggests…」（推論屬於 Discussion）；(2) 在 Discussion 第一次出現新數字（應該都已在 Results）；(3) Methods 寫成日記體（「我們先試了 A 不行，又試了 B…」）。三者都會讓審稿人皺眉。 Common violations: (1) writing "This suggests…" in Results (interpretation belongs in Discussion); (2) introducing new numbers in Discussion (they should all already be in Results); (3) writing Methods like a lab diary ("We first tried A but it failed, then tried B…"). All three irritate reviewers.
學位論文變體

Journal articles almost universally use IMRaD, but theses (master's / PhD) come in three main flavors: traditional 5-chapter, extended 6-chapter, and paper-based "sandwich" thesis. Picking the wrong one confuses your committee and makes future submission painful.

篇幅與深度

三、PhD vs 碩士 vs 期刊論文

🎓

碩士論文 Master's

字數：約 30k–50k 字（中文常見 60–120 頁）
深度：展現「會做研究」
讀者：口委 3–5 人
壽命：少數人下載；除非後續轉為期刊論文，影響力有限

Words: ~30k–50k (60–120 pages)
Depth: show you "can do research"
Audience: 3–5 committee members
Lifetime: few downloads — limited impact unless converted to a paper

👨‍🎓

博士論文 PhD

字數：約 80k–200k 字（生醫常見 150–300 頁）
深度：對領域有「實質、原創貢獻」
讀者：口委 + 同領域博士生
壽命：機構庫永久收錄；常被同領域引為背景

Words: ~80k–200k (150–300 pages)
Depth: "substantial original contribution" to the field
Audience: committee + future PhD students
Lifetime: permanently archived; often cited as background

📄

期刊論文 Journal

字數：3k–6k 字（不含圖表/補充材料）
深度：單一明確發現 + 充分證據
讀者：全球同領域研究者
壽命：數十年可被引；引用次數定義學術影響

Words: 3k–6k (excluding figures & supplements)
Depth: one clear finding with full evidence
Audience: the entire global subfield
Lifetime: citable for decades; citation count defines impact

⚠️
關鍵差異：學位論文要「展示思考過程」（包含 negative results、嘗試過的死胡同），期刊論文要「壓縮成最有說服力的故事」。同一個研究改寫成這兩種文體時，不能直接複製貼上——通常字數要砍一半以上、補實驗、補圖。 Key difference: A thesis must "show your thinking" (negative results, dead ends, etc.); a paper must be "compressed into the most persuasive story." Converting one to the other is not copy-paste — usually you cut more than half the words, add experiments, and rebuild figures.

四、學位論文架構決策樹

🌳 3 分鐘決定你該用哪一種架構

Q1:

Q2:

你的研究是「圍繞一個大問題的單一專題」嗎？→ 是 → 用 5 章（碩士）或 6 章（博士，凸顯結論）傳統式。
Q4:
你的研究是「3+ 個彼此弱關聯、各自獨立完成的子計畫」嗎？→ 是 → 用 paper-based，General Intro/Discussion 負責串接邏輯。
Q5:
還是不確定？→ 找主導指導教授 + 同實驗室近 3 屆畢業生各問一次，他們的偏好權重大於書本。
Q1:
Do you already have 1+ first-author paper (published / submitted / in review)? → Yes → Strongly consider paper-based — each paper becomes a chapter, maximally efficient.
Q2:
Does the institution or department mandate a format? → Yes → Follow the rule (most TW/Asian biomed prefer 5-chapter; some EU PhDs mandate paper-based).
Q3:
Is your research a single project around one big question? → Yes → Use 5-chapter (master's) or 6-chapter (PhD, with stand-alone conclusion).
Q4:

Q5:
Still unsure? → Ask your main advisor + last 3 graduates from your lab — their preference outweighs any textbook.
