故事開場

場景：Sofia 用 CARS 救活被 Cell 退稿的緒論

SCENE · ZOOM CALL 2024

從「五句負評」到「進入同儕審查」的一週

博後 Sofia 把她第一篇主導的 scRNA-seq TNBC 論文投到 Cell。三週後 desk-reject 通知出現在信箱裡，附了一段編輯前內審的回饋：「Introduction does not state a gap; previous literature is mentioned but not engaged with; the aim is hidden in the last sentence of paragraph 4; the first sentence reads like a textbook; we do not see why this study is necessary now.」共 5 條評論——而論文連送外審都沒到。

她在 Zoom 上把緒論逐段念給她在 EMBL 的 mentor 聽。Mentor 只問一句：「你的緒論裡有沒有一句話以 'However' 或 'remains' 開頭？」Sofia 翻了三遍——沒有。整篇緒論是 4 段「文獻摘要式」的羅列，第三段把 Wu 2022、Bassez 2023、Chen 2024 都引了，但只是並列「他們做了 X、他們發現 Y、他們報告 Z」，沒有任何一句把這些研究放在一起比較並指出共通的限制。

Mentor 把 Swales 1990 的 CARS 模型寄給她。Sofia 用週末重寫——第三段現在明確寫著：「However, three studies (Wu 2022, Bassez 2023, Chen 2024) all profiled pre-treatment samples; the post-chemotherapy landscape remains uncharacterized.」這一句話把三篇文獻整合進一個明確的 gap，並把 Move 2 完整佔位。她也把 aim 從第 4 段最後一句搬到段首，並在段尾加了一句「We show that…」預告主要結果。

修改版隔週投出。四天內收到「out for peer review」通知——這次連 desk reject 都沒踩到。Mentor 在 Slack 留言：「內容沒變，是結構變了。CARS 不是寫作技巧，是論證的骨架。」

Postdoc Sofia submitted her first lead-author scRNA-seq TNBC paper to Cell. Three weeks later a desk-reject landed in her inbox with a pre-review editorial note: "Introduction does not state a gap; previous literature is mentioned but not engaged with; the aim is hidden in the last sentence of paragraph 4; the first sentence reads like a textbook; we do not see why this study is necessary now." Five comments — and the paper never even reached external review.

On Zoom she read the Introduction paragraph by paragraph to her EMBL mentor. The mentor asked one question: "Is there a single sentence in your Introduction that opens with 'However' or 'remains'?" Sofia scrolled three times. There wasn't one. The whole Intro was four "literature-summary" paragraphs in a row; paragraph 3 cited Wu 2022, Bassez 2023, and Chen 2024 — but only as "they did X, they found Y, they reported Z." Nothing put those three side-by-side to compare them and surface a shared limitation.

The mentor emailed Sofia the Swales 1990 CARS model. Over the weekend she rewrote. Paragraph 3 now reads: "However, three studies (Wu 2022, Bassez 2023, Chen 2024) all profiled pre-treatment samples; the post-chemotherapy landscape remains uncharacterized." One sentence — three citations integrated into one explicit gap — Move 2 fully occupied. She also pulled the aim from the last sentence of paragraph 4 up to the top of that paragraph, and added a "We show that…" preview at the end.

Resubmitted the next week. Four days later: "out for peer review." No desk-reject this time. Her mentor's Slack message: "The content didn't change. The structure did. CARS is not a writing trick — it's the skeleton of the argument."

核心詞彙

12 個關鍵術語（雙版本）

每個詞有兩個版本：🧒「給小孩看」用比喻、📐「給研究者看」給定義。

Each term has two versions: 🧒 "for kids" uses a metaphor; 📐 "for researchers" gives the formal definition.

CARS model
CARS 模型
寫緒論就像在森林裡插旗子佔地：先說這座山很重要、再說這塊地還沒人佔、最後說「我來佔」。
Writing an Intro is like planting a flag in a forest: say the mountain matters, say this patch is unclaimed, then say "I claim it."
Create A Research Space；Swales 1990《Genre Analysis》以 110 篇 STEM 緒論歸納出三個論證 Move (Territory → Niche → Occupying)。是 EAP 與寫作中心最常教的模型。
Create A Research Space; Swales 1990 (Genre Analysis) induced three argumentative Moves (Territory → Niche → Occupying) from 110 STEM introductions. Standard model in EAP and writing centres.
Move 1 — Territory
Move 1 — 建立領域
「這座山很大、很重要、很多人爬過」——讓讀者覺得這個領域值得花時間讀。
"This mountain is big, important, many have climbed it" — convince the reader the field is worth their time.
3 個子步驟：(a) claiming centrality、(b) topic generalisation、(c) reviewing prior research。對應論文段落 1-2，引用密度由低 (1-3) 到高 (5-15)。
Three sub-steps: (a) claiming centrality, (b) topic generalisation, (c) reviewing prior research. Maps to paragraphs 1-2; citation density rises from low (1-3) to high (5-15).
Move 2 — Niche
Move 2 — 建立利基
「但這座山有一塊地沒人去過、或大家走的路其實有錯」——指出缺口或矛盾。
"But there's a patch nobody has explored — or the paths people take are wrong" — name the gap or contradiction.
4 種子步驟擇一：(a) counter-claim、(b) gap、(c) question-raising、(d) continuing tradition。必出現 however / despite / yet / remains。最常被忘記的 Move，也是被退稿主因。
Choose one of four sub-steps: (a) counter-claim, (b) gap, (c) question-raising, (d) continuing tradition. Must contain however / despite / yet / remains. The most-forgotten Move and the most common rejection trigger.
Move 3 — Occupying
Move 3 — 佔領利基
「我來佔這塊地，做這幾件事」——把自己的工作大聲講清楚。
"I'm claiming this patch, here's what I'll do" — declare your work loudly and clearly.
子步驟：(a) outlining purpose、(b) listing RQ/hypotheses、(c) announcing principal findings、(d) indicating structure。對應段落 4，零引用（除方法 paper 例外）。
Sub-steps: (a) outlining purpose, (b) listing RQ/hypotheses, (c) announcing principal findings, (d) indicating structure. Maps to paragraph 4; zero citations (method-paper exception aside).
Funnel structure
漏斗結構
像沙漏：上面很寬（領域），中間變窄（已知），下面再窄（gap），最下面就是你自己的研究——一格比一格小。
Like an hourglass: wide on top (field), narrowing (known), narrower (gap), narrowest (your study) — each level smaller than the last.
3-4 段結構：(P1) broad importance、(P2) what's known、(P3) gap、(P4) our study。每段「scope」逐步收窄；對應 Hyland 2024 的「topic-narrowing trajectory」。
3-4 paragraph structure: (P1) broad importance, (P2) what's known, (P3) gap, (P4) our study. Each paragraph narrows in scope; matches Hyland 2024's "topic-narrowing trajectory."
Gap statement
Gap 陳述
「這件事還沒人做過」、「兩派意見打架沒解」——一句很具體、有比較對象的話。
"Nobody has done this yet" or "two camps disagree and nobody has settled it" — one specific sentence with named comparisons.
合格 gap 需要 3 元素：(1) 引述既有研究、(2) 明確 contrast (however/yet)、(3) 量化或限定 (cell type / cohort / setting)。「few studies」「more research needed」屬不合格 gap。
A valid gap needs 3 ingredients: (1) cite prior work, (2) explicit contrast (however/yet), (3) quantify or scope (cell type / cohort / setting). "Few studies" or "more research needed" are non-gaps.
Counter-claim
反證
「之前大家以為 A，但其實 B」——一個翻轉的小爆點。
"Everyone thought A — actually it's B" — a small reversal.
Swales Move 2(a)；學界最有說服力的 gap 形式，但需要直接證據與引用作支撐。常見句型：「Contrary to the prevailing view that…」「Recent evidence challenges this assumption…」。
Swales Move 2(a); the most persuasive gap form, but requires direct evidence and citations to support. Common phrasing: "Contrary to the prevailing view that…", "Recent evidence challenges this assumption…".
Citation density
引用密度
每段塞了幾個 (Author, year)——太少看起來不熟領域，太多像在抄文獻回顧。
How many (Author, year) tags each paragraph carries — too few looks unread, too many looks like a lit review.
期刊論文 (~3000 字本文) 緒論建議：P1 (1-3)、P2 (5-15)、P3 (3-8)、P4 (0)。Thesis 章節可放大 2-3 倍。> 40 篇通常代表論證凝聚不足。
For a journal article (~3000-word body): P1 (1-3), P2 (5-15), P3 (3-8), P4 (0). Thesis chapters scale 2-3×. > 40 refs usually means the argument hasn't been condensed enough.
Aim statement (in Intro)
緒論中的研究目標
緒論最後那一句「我要做什麼」——一定要在最後一段、最好是第一句。
The "what we did" sentence at the end of the Intro — must be in the last paragraph, ideally the first sentence of it.
與研究問題章的 Aim 同義，但位置嚴格：Move 3 第一句或第二句。動詞用 characterize / quantify / compare / identify / show，避免 study / discuss / investigate。
Same concept as the Research Question chapter's Aim, but the position is strict: first or second sentence of Move 3. Verbs: characterize / quantify / compare / identify / show. Avoid study / discuss / investigate.
Lay reader test
外行讀者測試
把緒論給不是這領域的朋友看——如果他讀完不知道你要做什麼，就還沒寫好。
Hand the Intro to a friend outside your field — if they don't know what you did after reading, it's not done.
PLOS 2004 創刊時的明確要求：「the broad readership of working scientists」。對應「3-paragraph rule」——前三段對相鄰領域的研究者應可讀。
PLOS 2004 launch criterion: "the broad readership of working scientists." Maps to the "3-paragraph rule" — first three paragraphs should be readable to adjacent-field researchers.
Importance statement
重要性陳述
「為什麼這件事值得做？」——通常用一個流病數字、一個臨床痛點、或一個方法瓶頸來開頭。
"Why is this worth doing?" — usually opens with an epidemiology number, a clinical pain point, or a methodological bottleneck.
Move 1(a) claiming centrality 的具體實現；高 IF 期刊偏好量化的 importance（「affects X million; mortality Y%」）而非定性宣稱（「is important」）。
Concrete realisation of Move 1(a) claiming centrality; high-IF journals prefer quantified importance ("affects X million; Y% mortality") over qualitative claims ("is important").
Hook (first sentence)
引子（第一句）
論文的黃金第一句——讀者只給你 5 秒。第一句沒抓到他，後面寫得再好也沒用。
The golden first sentence. Readers give you 5 seconds. Miss the hook and the rest is wasted.
eye-tracking 研究 (Eyal-Wronski 2021 J Doc) 顯示，editors 平均 5 秒判斷是否續讀；Cell Press 2023 數據顯示 70% 退稿在第一段內決定。
Eye-tracking (Eyal-Wronski 2021 J Doc) shows editors decide to keep reading within ~5 seconds; Cell Press 2023 internal data: 70% of rejections are decided within paragraph 1.

🥁
生活化比喻

Introduction 是一個漏斗。從整個研究領域 (廣) 漸窄到「你那一句研究問題」(尖)。每一段都比上一段更窄、更聚焦、更靠近你的研究。漏不到底——讀者就「掉到漏斗旁邊」，讀完不知道你到底要做什麼。漏太快——讀者跟不上、覺得邏輯斷裂。寫緒論就是練習這個「平順收窄」的技藝。

The Introduction is a funnel. It narrows from the whole research field (wide) down to your single research question (tip). Every paragraph must be narrower, more focused, closer to your study than the one before. If it doesn't reach the bottom, readers fall off the side and finish unsure what you actually did. If it narrows too fast, readers can't follow and feel the logic breaks. Writing the Intro is practicing this smooth narrowing.

逐步操作

從 CARS Move 1 到完整 Introduction 的 8 步

Σ 可執行 SOP
Σ₁
Para 1 — 開廣的重要性：用一個具體數字開頭（疾病負擔、患病率、年新發數）。不要從教科書定義開頭。1–3 篇流病/共識文獻足矣。
Para 1 — Open with broad importance: lead with a concrete statistic (disease burden, prevalence, annual incidence). Do not open with a textbook definition. 1–3 epidemiology / consensus citations are enough.
Σ₂
Para 2 — 引述既有文獻：用 5–15 篇代表性文獻說明領域目前的共識與進展。寫成故事（時序、技術演進、發現累積），不要寫成清單。
Para 2 — Cite the known literature: use 5–15 representative refs to summarise current consensus and progress. Write it as a story (timeline, methodological evolution, accumulating findings) — never a list.
Σ₃
Para 3 — 明確指出 gap：必須出現轉折詞「however / yet / despite / remains unclear」其中之一。沒有這個轉折，gap 就不存在。
Para 3 — Identify the gap: one of "however / yet / despite / remains unclear" must appear. Without that pivot the gap is not on the page.
Σ₄
Para 3 — 說明 gap 為何重要：不能只說「沒人做過」就停。要說「沒人做過因此導致 X 臨床/科學後果無法解決」。讓 gap 有重量。
Para 3 — Explain why the gap matters: don't stop at "no one has done this." Say "no one has done this, therefore clinical/scientific consequence X cannot be addressed." Give the gap weight.
Σ₅
Para 4 — 宣告研究目的：「Here we ____」或「本研究旨在 ____」一句到底，動詞要具體（profile、quantify、compare、identify），不要用 discuss/explore/study 等空泛詞。
Para 4 — Announce the purpose: "Here we ____" / "This study aims to ____" — one tight sentence with a concrete verb (profile, quantify, compare, identify). Avoid vague verbs like discuss/explore/study.
Σ₆
Para 4 — 假設 (要可被推翻)：「We hypothesised that ____。」假設必須可驗證、可證偽（Popperian falsifiability）。「X 與 Y 有關」太弱；「在 N=12 中，X 表達量 ≥1.5 倍時 Y 顯著上升 (p<0.05)」才夠。
Para 4 — Hypothesis (must be falsifiable): "We hypothesised that ____." Must be testable and refutable (Popperian falsifiability). "X is related to Y" is too weak; "in N=12, when X expression ≥1.5×, Y rises significantly (p<0.05)" is concrete enough.
Σ₇
Para 4 — 方法 1–2 句預告：「To this end, we performed ____」說明你用什麼技術、什麼樣本、什麼 N。不引文獻（除非方法原始論文）。
Para 4 — Preview the approach in 1–2 sentences: "To this end, we performed ____" — state the technique, sample, and N. No citations (except a single method-origin paper if essential).
Σ₈
最終通讀：每段最後一句是否「開啟下一段的話題」？Para 1 末→Para 2 頭、Para 2 末→Para 3 頭。如果每段結尾都「自己結束自己」，整篇就會卡頓不連貫。
Final read-through: does each paragraph's last sentence open the next paragraph's topic? Para 1 end→Para 2 start, Para 2 end→Para 3 start. If every paragraph closes itself off, the Intro reads choppy and disconnected.
開放思考

三題反思：緒論寫作沒有標準答案

議題 1
用一個流病統計開頭，還是用一個臨床故事 / anecdote 開頭？
Open with a statistic, or with a clinical anecdote?
支持統計：客觀、可被引、無偏；biomedical journal 慣例。支持 anecdote：讀者記憶較強、適合 perspective / commentary、可在 Nature/Science 高影響期刊試行；但風險高、容易被認為「不嚴謹」。
For statistics: objective, citable, unbiased — biomedical-journal default. For anecdote: more memorable, suits perspective / commentary, can work in Nature/Science; but high risk — easily judged "unrigorous."
議題 2
「Many studies have shown...」這種開頭——是偷懶還是有時必要？
"Many studies have shown..." — lazy, or sometimes warranted?
偷懶論：「many」不精確、引一堆文獻反而稀釋焦點、審稿人看了會皺眉。必要論：在文獻太多的領域 (如 p53、IL-6) 確實沒辦法一一列舉，「many」加 2–3 個 representative refs 是合理妥協。
Lazy side: "many" is imprecise, citation pile-ups dilute focus, and reviewers wince. Warranted side: in saturated fields (p53, IL-6) you literally cannot list every study — "many" plus 2–3 representative refs is a reasonable compromise.
議題 3
假設驅動 (hypothesis-driven) vs 假設產生 (hypothesis-generating) 緒論——兩者都正當嗎？
Hypothesis-driven vs hypothesis-generating introductions — are both valid?
支持兩者皆可：omics 探索性研究本來就是 hypothesis-generating；硬要套 hypothesis-driven 是假裝。傳統派：沒有假設等於沒有研究問題、研究會失焦；應該至少寫成「我們提問：____？」的問題形式。
Both-valid view: omics exploratory work is intrinsically hypothesis-generating; forcing it into hypothesis-driven framing is pretend-rigor. Traditionalist view: no hypothesis = no research question = unfocused study; at minimum frame it as "We asked: ____?"
案例聚焦

兩個現實案例：模範與警鐘

🏆
模範 MODEL

Sabeti Lab, 2018, Cell: Lassa 病毒大規模演化分析。緒論 4 段完美 CARS：Para 1 以「Lassa 病毒在西非每年造成 30 萬感染、5,000 死亡」開頭（具體數字、不是「Lassa is dangerous」）；Para 2 引用 12 篇代表性病毒基因組研究、講演化追蹤的進展；Para 3 明確指出「然而 (however) 過去研究皆來自 <100 個基因組，無法解析跨國傳播路線」；Para 4「Here we sequenced 220 Lassa genomes...」一氣呵成。整篇 ~600 字，是 CARS 模型實作的教科書級範本。

Sabeti Lab, 2018, Cell: large-scale Lassa virus evolution. A textbook 4-paragraph CARS Introduction: Para 1 opens "Lassa virus causes ~300,000 infections and 5,000 deaths annually in West Africa" (concrete numbers, not "Lassa is dangerous"); Para 2 cites 12 representative viral-genomics studies tracing evolution; Para 3 says explicitly "however, previous studies sampled <100 genomes, leaving cross-border transmission unresolved"; Para 4 "Here we sequenced 220 Lassa genomes..." flows seamlessly. ~600 words total — a textbook CARS implementation.

⚠️
警鐘 WARNING

2019 年某 TNBC 投稿論文（已 desk-reject）。第一句：「Cancer is a serious disease that affects many people around the world.」第二段繼續廣泛談癌症與化療歷史，毫無 TNBC 聚焦；第三段唯一引用 3 篇文獻、都是 2010 年以前；第四段「This study examined TNBC and chemotherapy.」沒有假設、沒有方法。Editor 兩小時內 desk-reject。問題：每一段都「太廣」、漏斗從未收窄。教訓：第一句、Gap 句、Aim 句三個位置，任何一個出問題都會被視為「沒準備好」。

A 2019 TNBC manuscript (desk-rejected). First sentence: "Cancer is a serious disease that affects many people around the world." Para 2 continued broadly on cancer and chemotherapy history with no TNBC focus; Para 3 cited only 3 papers, all pre-2010; Para 4 said "This study examined TNBC and chemotherapy." — no hypothesis, no methods. Desk-rejected by the editor within two hours. Problem: every paragraph was too broad and the funnel never narrowed. Lesson: of the three critical slots — opening sentence, Gap sentence, Aim sentence — failure at any one reads as "not ready."

常見陷阱

六個緒論寫作陷阱

1. 開頭太廣

「Cancer is a serious disease...」「Many proteins play important roles...」這些句子放在任何論文都成立——也就是說，放在哪裡都沒用。改用具體數字開頭。
"Cancer is a serious disease..." / "Many proteins play important roles..." — these fit anywhere, which means they help nowhere. Open with a concrete statistic instead.

2. 沒有 however / yet 轉折

整篇緒論找不到「however / yet / despite / remains unclear」——表示沒有 gap，也就沒有研究存在的理由。讀者問「為什麼要做這個？」你答不出來。
No "however / yet / despite / remains unclear" anywhere — meaning no gap, meaning no reason for the study. The reader asks "why this study?" and you have no answer.

3. 假設遺失

Para 4 只寫「我們做了 X」、沒寫「我們假設 Y」。少了假設，整個研究像在「賭一賭看看」——這對 reviewers 是大忌。
Para 4 says only "we did X" with no "we hypothesised Y." Without a hypothesis the work looks like "let's see what happens" — a red flag for reviewers.

4. 引超過 40 篇文獻

期刊緒論引文獻 > 40 篇，等於把 Lit Review 章節塞進緒論，gap 反而被稀釋。應該把詳盡回顧搬到 thesis 的 Lit Review 章，期刊論文緒論引 10–25 篇即可。
A journal Intro with >40 refs squeezes a Lit Review into the Introduction and dilutes the gap. Move the exhaustive review to the thesis Lit Review chapter; keep journal Intros at 10–25 refs.

5. 最後一段沒對應 CARS Move 3

最後一段應該明確「占領 niche」——宣告目的、假設、方法、預告結果。如果寫成「文獻又一次回顧」，等於前面 3 段都白寫。
The last paragraph must explicitly "occupy the niche" — announce purpose, hypothesis, approach, preview result. If it turns into another literature recap, the first three paragraphs were wasted.

6. Aim 與 Discussion 結論不一致

Intro 說「我們要做 A」，Discussion 卻說「我們發現 B」——A、B 不是同一回事。這是 reviewer 一眼就看穿的缺陷。寫完後請同時把 Aim 句與 Discussion 第一句並列比較。
Intro says "we set out to do A," Discussion says "we found B" — A and B are different things. Reviewers spot this instantly. After drafting, place the Aim sentence next to the first Discussion sentence and compare them side by side.
正反對照

緒論整段：壞 vs 好

❌ 反例

「癌症是一種影響很多人的嚴重疾病。少有研究探討 TNBC。本研究探討 TNBC。」

"Cancer is a serious disease that affects many people. Few studies have looked at TNBC. This study examined TNBC."

沒數字、沒文獻、沒 gap 轉折、沒假設、沒方法——所有 CARS 元素全部缺席。

No numbers, no refs, no gap pivot, no hypothesis, no methods — every CARS element absent.

✅ 正例

「三陰性乳癌 (TNBC) 佔乳癌病例 15–20%，卻缺乏標靶治療 (Foulkes 2010)。三篇研究 (Wu 2021、Bassez 2021、Liu 2023) 在 TNBC 中做過治療前 scRNA-seq，然而 (however)化療後的轉錄景觀至今仍未被刻畫。本研究對 12 對病人切片執行 scRNA-seq，以鑑定化療誘發的細胞狀態轉移。」

"Triple-negative breast cancer accounts for 15–20% of breast cancer cases but lacks targeted therapies (Foulkes 2010). Three studies (Wu 2021, Bassez 2021, Liu 2023) profiled pre-treatment scRNA-seq in TNBC, but the post-chemotherapy transcriptional landscape remains uncharacterized. Here we applied scRNA-seq to 12 paired biopsies to identify chemotherapy-induced cell-state transitions."

具體數字 + 3 篇代表性文獻 + however 轉折 + 明確 aim——一段完整 CARS。

Concrete numbers + 3 representative refs + "however" pivot + explicit aim — a complete CARS paragraph.

進階補充

緒論寫作的前沿議題

進階 ACARS Model 2024 更新版

Swales 與 Feak (2024 版 Academic Writing for Graduate Students) 對 1990 原始 CARS 做了三項修訂：(1) Move 2 的「counter-claim」應該避免直接批評既有作者（學術社群更小、容易得罪人）、改為強調「補充而非推翻」；(2) Move 3 新增 sub-step「pre-empt potential criticism」——主動預告 limitation 反而會增加 reviewer 信任；(3) AI 工具如 Elicit、Scite 可幫助快速 scan 領域內 gap，但 Swales 警告：「AI 找到的 gap 可能不是真 gap，要回頭核實」。

The 2024 edition of Swales & Feak's Academic Writing for Graduate Students revised the original 1990 CARS in three ways: (1) the Move-2 "counter-claim" sub-step should avoid direct criticism of named authors (smaller academic communities make this risky) — emphasise "complementing not overturning"; (2) Move 3 adds a new sub-step "pre-empt potential criticism" — flagging limitations proactively builds reviewer trust; (3) AI tools like Elicit and Scite can speed up gap scanning, but Swales warns "an AI-found gap may not be a real gap — verify."

進階 BBertin 等 2023 對科學緒論修辭的研究

Bertin 等 (2023, Journal of Informetrics) 用 NLP 分析了 200 萬篇 PubMed 緒論，發現：(1) 過去 30 年「however」出現頻率下降，被「nevertheless / nonetheless / yet」取代；(2) 緒論平均長度從 1990 年的 ~420 字增加到 2020 年的 ~680 字（引用數膨脹）；(3) 高被引論文的緒論在首段引用密度顯著較低——印證「先建立宏觀重要性、再引文獻」的修辭策略有效。

Bertin et al. (2023, Journal of Informetrics) used NLP on 2 million PubMed introductions and found: (1) "however" frequency has declined over 30 years, replaced by "nevertheless / nonetheless / yet"; (2) average Intro length grew from ~420 words (1990) to ~680 words (2020), driven by citation inflation; (3) highly-cited papers show significantly lower citation density in Para 1 — confirming the "establish macro importance before citing" rhetoric works.

進階 CAI 輔助緒論寫作的風險

2025 年 Nature 與 Science 都要求作者揭露 AI 使用方式。緒論用 LLM 草擬的三大風險：(1) 幻覺引用——LLM 會編造看似真實的 DOI、Author 年份；(2) 句式雷同——某些 LLM 偏好「In recent years」「has emerged as」等套句、reviewer 一看就知道；(3) 失去個人 voice——緒論是研究者「為自己工作辯護」的舞台，LLM 化的緒論讀起來像 textbook 摘要，沒有立場。建議：用 AI 找文獻、寫 outline，但最後一稿手寫。

From 2025, both Nature and Science require authors to disclose AI use. Three risks of LLM-drafted introductions: (1) hallucinated citations — LLMs invent plausible-looking DOIs and author-year pairs; (2) stylistic mirroring — certain LLMs over-use "In recent years," "has emerged as," and reviewers spot them instantly; (3) loss of voice — the Intro is where the researcher argues for their work; LLM-generated text reads like a textbook abstract with no stance. Recommendation: use AI for literature scouting and outlining, but hand-write the final draft.

概覽

為什麼緒論最難寫？

緒論 (Introduction) 是論文裡最難寫的一段。它必須在短短 3–4 個段落內，同時說服一位陌生人兩件事：(1) 你研究的問題真的重要，且 (2) 這個問題還沒有人回答。多數的「desk reject」與「reject after review」決定，正是在這裡定生死。

編輯與審稿人讀緒論的方式很功利：第一段問「這領域我為什麼要在意？」、中段問「文獻有讀夠嗎？」、最後一段問「所以你到底要做什麼、有何貢獻？」三個問題只要漏一個，就會被視為「沒準備好」。

The Introduction is the toughest section of a paper. In just 3–4 paragraphs it must convince a stranger of two things at once: (1) the problem genuinely matters, and (2) nobody has answered it yet. Most desk rejects and post-review rejects are decided right here.

Editors and reviewers read introductions instrumentally: paragraph 1 asks "why should I care about this field?", the middle asks "have they read enough?", and the last asks "so what exactly are you going to do, and what's the contribution?" Miss any one and the paper looks unprepared.

💡
核心原則：緒論不是「文獻回顧」的縮影，而是一場有方向的論證——從廣到窄、從共識到 gap、從 gap 到你要做的事。沒有方向感的緒論等於沒有緒論。 Core principle: The Introduction is not a mini literature review — it is a directed argument: broad to narrow, consensus to gap, gap to your contribution. An Introduction without that arrow is no Introduction at all.
CARS 模型

一、Swales 的 CARS 模型：三個 Move

應用語言學家 John Swales 觀察了上百篇 STEM 期刊論文後提出 CARS (Create A Research Space) 模型：作者在緒論裡其實在「為自己的研究爭一塊空間」。整個緒論可以拆成三個有先後順序的「Move」。

Applied linguist John Swales analysed hundreds of STEM articles and proposed the CARS (Create A Research Space) model: authors in the Introduction are essentially carving out a space for their own work. The entire introduction breaks into three ordered "Moves".

1

Move 1 — 建立領域

告訴讀者你的研究所在的領域很重要，並概述既有研究。常見子步驟：(a) claiming centrality（這個議題很關鍵）、(b) topic generalisation（給出已知共識）、(c) reviewing prior research（簡述代表性文獻）。

Tell the reader the field matters, and outline existing research. Sub-steps: (a) claiming centrality, (b) topic generalisation (state consensus), (c) reviewing prior research (cite representative work).

2

Move 2 — 建立利基

指出既有文獻的不足或矛盾，把 gap 挖出來。Swales 列了 4 種子步驟：(a) counter-claim（推翻先前主張）、(b) gap（指出未被探討的問題）、(c) question-raising（提出新問題）、(d) continuing tradition（延續未完成的工作）。四選一即可，但一定要有。

Surface the limits or contradictions of existing work and dig out the gap. Swales lists four sub-options: (a) counter-claim, (b) gap (identify unexplored issue), (c) question-raising (pose a new question), (d) continuing a tradition (extend unfinished work). Pick one — but you must have one.

3

Move 3 — 佔領利基

把你要做的事直接擺出來。子步驟：(a) outlining purpose（宣告本研究目的）、(b) listing research questions/hypotheses、(c) announcing principal findings 或方法、(d) indicating structure（簡述後續章節）。最後一段就是讀者最在意的「contribution」段。

Lay out what you are doing. Sub-steps: (a) outlining purpose, (b) listing research questions/hypotheses, (c) announcing principal findings or methods, (d) indicating structure. This last paragraph is the "contribution" paragraph reviewers most care about.

⚠️
順序不能亂：很多研究生把 Move 3 (我要做什麼) 放到第一段、Move 1 放到中間，讀起來像在自我介紹。順序錯了，論證的邏輯感就消失了。
Order matters: students often put Move 3 (what I'm doing) in paragraph 1 and Move 1 in the middle — it reads like a self-introduction. Get the order wrong and the argumentative logic vanishes.
漏斗結構

二、把 CARS 落地成「漏斗段落」

CARS 是抽象架構，實際寫的時候建議用 3–4 個段落的漏斗：每段越往下越窄、越具體，最後一段直接落到你的研究本身。

CARS is abstract; in practice write the introduction as a 3–4 paragraph funnel — each paragraph narrower and more specific than the last, ending squarely on your own study.

🌍

Para 1 — 廣的重要性

從疾病負擔、生物學意義或臨床現實切入。可引 1–3 篇代表性流病/共識文獻。避免從教科書定義開頭。

Open with disease burden, biological significance or clinical reality. Cite 1–3 representative epidemiology / consensus papers. Don't start from a textbook definition.

📚

Para 2 — 已知什麼

建立 context：過去 5–10 年的代表性研究、主要發現、技術演進。引用密度最高的一段（5–15 篇）。寫成「故事」而非清單。

Build context — representative studies of the last 5–10 years, key findings, methodological evolution. This is the citation-densest paragraph (5–15 refs). Write it as a story, not a list.

🕳️

Para 3 — 還不知道什麼 (gap)

明確指出 gap：反證、矛盾、未涵蓋的族群、樣本偏誤、缺乏外部驗證、技術限制。一定要出現「however / despite / yet / remains unclear」這類轉折詞。

Spell out the gap: counter-evidence, contradictions, missing populations, sampling bias, lack of external validation, technical limits. There must be a "however / despite / yet / remains unclear" turn here.

🎯

Para 4 — 我們做了什麼

1–2 句宣告 aim、1 句假設、1 句方法摘要、1 句預告主要結果或貢獻。這段不引文獻——它是你的工作。

One or two sentences for the aim, one for the hypothesis, one for the methods, one previewing main result/contribution. No citations in this paragraph — it is your work.

範例對照

三、好壞句子對照

❌ 開頭句 — 教科書式

「癌症是一種影響許多人的嚴重疾病。」
(沒有數字、沒有指出研究範圍、跟你的論文沒關係。)

"Cancer is a serious disease that affects many people."
(No numbers, no scope, no link to your paper.)

✅ 開頭句 — 聚焦版

「三陰性乳癌 (TNBC) 佔所有乳癌的 15–20%，缺乏標靶治療選擇，5 年存活率不到 77%。」

"Triple-negative breast cancer (TNBC) accounts for 15–20% of breast cancer cases but lacks targeted therapies, with 5-year survival below 77%."

❌ Gap 句 — 偷懶式

「目前少有研究探討此議題。」
(沒引文獻、沒說「少」是少到什麼程度、不知道哪裡缺。)

"Few studies have looked at this."
(No citations, no quantification of "few", no specifics on what is missing.)

✅ Gap 句 — 明確版

「目前已有 3 篇研究 (Smith 2022、Lin 2023、Chen 2024) 在 TNBC 中執行 scRNA-seq，但全部使用治療前切片，化療後的轉錄景觀至今仍未被刻畫。」

"Three studies (Smith 2022, Lin 2023, Chen 2024) profiled scRNA-seq in TNBC, but all used pre-treatment biopsies — the post-chemotherapy transcriptional landscape remains uncharacterized."

❌ Aim 句 — 空泛式

「本研究將討論 TNBC 與化療反應。」
(動詞 "discuss" 太弱、沒方法、沒對象、沒可驗證的目標。)

"This study will discuss TNBC and chemotherapy response."
(Verb "discuss" is weak; no method, no subjects, no verifiable target.)

✅ Aim 句 — 具體版

「我們對 12 對化療前/後 TNBC 切片執行 scRNA-seq (10x v3)，以鑑定化療誘發的細胞狀態轉移 (cell-state transitions)。」

"We applied scRNA-seq (10x v3) to 12 paired pre/post-chemotherapy TNBC biopsies to identify chemotherapy-induced cell-state transitions."

🌳 送投稿前的 5 個自我檢查

Q1:

Q1:
Is the first sentence a textbook definition? → Yes → Too broad — cut it; open with an epidemiology number or clinical fact instead.
Q2:
Did you cite fewer than 5 papers? → Yes → Not enough context — add representative refs to Moves 1 and 2.
Q3:

Q4:
No "however / despite / yet / remains unclear" anywhere? → Yes → Your gap isn't on the page — meaning you have no gap.
Q5:
Last paragraph doesn't state aim or hypothesis? → Yes → Reader leaves without knowing what you did — the introduction fails.

範本

六、可填空使用的句型範本

# 1. 流病 / 重要性開頭
[Disease] affects approximately [N] million people worldwide,
with a [X]-year mortality rate of [Y]%.

# 2. 領域共識
It is now widely accepted that [established finding]
plays a central role in [biological process / disease].

# 3. 既有研究綜述
Recent studies have demonstrated that [mechanism / phenotype]
contributes to [outcome] in [population / system]
(Author et al. 20XX; Author et al. 20XX).

# 4. 技術趨勢開頭
The advent of [technology, e.g. scRNA-seq, spatial transcriptomics]
has enabled [capability] at unprecedented resolution.

# 5. 臨床需求開頭
Despite advances in [treatment], [outcome problem]
remains a major clinical challenge in [patient group].
# 1. 「However + 對比」型
However, [prior work] has focused exclusively on [narrow scope],
leaving [broader scope] largely uncharacterized.

# 2. 「Despite + 不足」型
Despite these advances, the mechanism / impact / role
of [X] in [Y] remains unclear / controversial / poorly defined.

# 3. 「矛盾證據」型
While [Author 20XX] reported [finding A],
[Author 20XX] observed the opposite in [setting] —
this discrepancy has yet to be reconciled.

# 4. 「樣本/族群缺口」型
To date, no study has examined [X] in [underrepresented population],
limiting the generalizability of current findings.

# 5. 「方法限制」型
Existing [method]-based analyses lack the resolution / sensitivity / scale
required to detect [target], leaving [question] open.
# 1. 標準 aim 句
Here, we [verb: characterize / quantify / compare / identify]
[X] in [population / system]
using [method].

# 2. 帶假設的 aim
We hypothesized that [predicted relationship],
and tested this by [experimental approach]
in [N] samples / participants.

# 3. 多目標 aim
This study aimed to (i) [verb] [X];
(ii) [verb] [Y];
and (iii) [verb] [Z].

# 4. 結果預告 (Nature/Cell 偏好)
We show that [finding 1], identify [finding 2],
and demonstrate that [finding 3],
providing [contribution / implication].

# 5. 探索性研究 aim
As a hypothesis-generating study, we sought to profile / map / survey
[X] in [Y] and to identify candidate [Z]
for future validation.
⛔
千萬別寫的開頭：「Since the dawn of medicine…」「In recent years, X has become an important topic…」「With the rapid development of technology…」——全部是空話，沒有資訊量，編輯讀到第二句就走神。 Never open with: "Since the dawn of medicine…", "In recent years, X has become an important topic…", "With the rapid development of technology…" — all are content-free filler. Editors disengage by sentence two.

歷史小檔案：應用語言學家 John Swales 在 1980 年代分析了 158 篇科學論文的緒論，發現 ~95% 都遵循同一個三步驟修辭結構，於 1990 年正式提出 CARS (Create A Research Space) 模型。35 年來它一直是學術寫作教學的主流框架，從生醫到工程、社會科學皆有採用。即便在 2024 年的更新版裡，三個 Move 的核心結構（Territory → Niche → Occupy）仍未動搖——這是少數能跨越學科、跨越時代的修辭觀察。
History note: Applied linguist John Swales analysed 158 scientific-paper introductions in the 1980s and found ~95% followed the same three-step rhetorical structure, which he formalised as the CARS (Create A Research Space) model in 1990. For 35+ years it has been the dominant framework taught in academic writing — across biomedical, engineering, and social sciences. Even in the 2024 update, the three Moves (Territory → Niche → Occupy) remain intact — one of the rare rhetorical observations that holds across disciplines and decades.
