故事開場

場景：1 小時標題改寫，省下 6 個月

SCENE · 2024 · TAIPEI

從「A study of breast cancer subtypes」到 desk-reject 再到 Cell Reports

博士後 Andy 把累積 3 年的 TNBC 單細胞數據投給 Cell Reports。標題寫「A study of breast cancer subtypes」，摘要 240 字裡塞滿背景、沒寫一個數字、Conclusion 用「will revolutionize cancer treatment」。送出 24 小時後 desk-reject——editor 在拒信只寫一句「Title and abstract do not communicate the contribution.」

Andy 不知道怎麼修，找了在 NEJM 發過兩篇的學姊 Sandy 看。Sandy 沒讀正文，只花 1 小時改了三件事：(1) 標題改成「Single-cell profiling identifies three chemoresistance-associated cell states in triple-negative breast cancer」——具體 verb + 數字 + 領域，(2) Abstract 用 1+2+3+1 公式重寫，Results 加入 12× / HR 2.4 (95% CI 1.5-3.8)，(3) 關鍵字補上 MeSH terms (drug resistance neoplasm, breast neoplasms) 避開標題已有字。

同一個正文、同一份 figure，重投 6 週後 sent to review，3 個月後 minor revision 接受。Andy 後來算過：那 1 小時的標題-摘要改寫，省下了至少 6 個月 (跳到 mid-IF 期刊重投的時間 + 補實驗壓力)。

Postdoc Andy submits 3 years of TNBC single-cell data to Cell Reports. Title: "A study of breast cancer subtypes." Abstract: 240 words of background, zero numbers, Conclusion claims "will revolutionize cancer treatment." Desk-rejected in 24 hours — the editor's only line: "Title and abstract do not communicate the contribution."

Stuck, Andy asks senior labmate Sandy (two NEJM papers) for help. Sandy doesn't read the body — just spends 1 hour fixing three things: (1) Title rewritten to "Single-cell profiling identifies three chemoresistance-associated cell states in triple-negative breast cancer" — specific verb + number + domain; (2) Abstract rebuilt on the 1+2+3+1 formula with 12-fold / HR 2.4 (95% CI 1.5-3.8) in Results; (3) Keywords swapped in MeSH terms (drug resistance neoplasm, breast neoplasms) avoiding title duplicates.

Same body, same figures. Six weeks later: sent to review. Three months later: minor revision accepted. Andy later did the math — that 1-hour rewrite saved at least 6 months (the time to retreat to a mid-IF journal + the pressure to add more experiments).

核心詞彙

14 個關鍵術語（雙版本）

每個詞有兩個版本：🧒「給小孩看」用比喻、📐「給研究者看」給定義。

Each term has two versions: 🧒 "for kids" uses a metaphor; 📐 "for researchers" gives the formal definition.

Abstract
摘要
整篇論文的「電影預告片」——150-350 字內讓人決定要不要看正片。
A movie trailer for the paper — 150-350 words to decide if you'll watch the feature.
論文的獨立摘要段；PubMed、Google Scholar 索引主要對象。長度依期刊 150-350 字不等。
Standalone synopsis of the paper; the primary unit indexed by PubMed and Google Scholar. Length 150-350 words per journal.
Structured abstract
結構式摘要
有小標題的摘要：Background / Methods / Results / Conclusions 各佔一段。
Abstract with sub-headings: Background / Methods / Results / Conclusions, each a block.
ICMJE 1987 推動；NEJM、Lancet、JAMA、BMJ、BMC 系列、PLOS Medicine 採用；常見 250-350 字。
Promoted by ICMJE 1987; used by NEJM, Lancet, JAMA, BMJ, BMC family, PLOS Medicine; typically 250-350 words.
Unstructured abstract
非結構式摘要
一整段連貫敘事，沒有小標。
A single flowing paragraph, no sub-headings.
Nature、Cell、Science、PNAS 系列偏好；通常 150-200 字；強調敘事流暢度。
Preferred by Nature, Cell, Science, PNAS families; ~150-200 words; emphasizes narrative flow.
Title
標題
高速公路上的廣告看板——讀者只有 1.5 秒掃過。
A highway billboard — drivers have 1.5 seconds to look.
一句完整句或名詞片語，多數期刊限制 ≤150 字元；PubMed 超過會截斷顯示。
A complete sentence or noun phrase; most journals cap at ≤150 chars; PubMed truncates display beyond this.
Keywords
關鍵字
論文的「標籤」——告訴搜尋引擎你的論文是關於什麼。
Paper's "tags" — telling search engines what you're about.
大多期刊要求 4-7 個；應補充標題未涵蓋的同義詞 / 上位詞 / 應用領域。
Most journals require 4-7; should add synonyms / parent terms / application areas not in the title.
MeSH terms
MeSH 醫學主題詞
PubMed 用的「官方詞典」——用對的詞才會被找到。
PubMed's "official dictionary" — use the right words to be found.
NLM 1971 推出，Medical Subject Headings；階層式詞庫；2025 年含 ~31000 主詞 + 87000 entry terms。
NLM's Medical Subject Headings (1971); hierarchical thesaurus; ~31k main terms + 87k entry terms as of 2025.
Discoverability (SEO)
可被搜尋性
就算你寫得很好，搜尋引擎找不到，等於沒寫。
If search can't find it, you didn't write it.
Academic SEO：標題、摘要前 250 字、關鍵字共同決定 PubMed / Google Scholar 排名 (Beel 2010)。
Academic SEO: title, first 250 words of abstract, and keywords jointly drive PubMed / Google Scholar ranking (Beel 2010).
Lay abstract
通俗摘要
寫給「沒念過你領域的人」看的摘要版本——阿嬤也能懂。
An abstract written so a non-specialist (or grandma) understands.
非技術語言、避免行話、Flesch-Kincaid 等級 ≤ 12；Cochrane、NIHR、PLOS 等系統要求。
Non-technical language, no jargon, Flesch-Kincaid ≤ 12; required by Cochrane, NIHR, PLOS systems.
Plain Language Summary
通俗語言摘要 (PLS)
比 lay abstract 更短、更白話——常隨論文一起公開。
Even shorter & plainer than a lay abstract — published alongside the paper.
Cochrane 強制 (≤400 字 / 8 級閱讀)；EU Horizon 2020 與 NIHR 2025 起新增 PLS 強制要求。
Cochrane mandates (≤400 words / grade 8 reading); EU Horizon 2020 & NIHR (from 2025) require PLS submission.
IMRaD-condensed
IMRaD 壓縮版
把整篇論文的「四個房間」壓進一個摘要段。
All four rooms of the paper crammed into one abstract block.
Introduction → Methods → Results → Discussion 對應 Background → Methods → Results → Conclusion 段。
Introduction → Methods → Results → Discussion mapped onto Background → Methods → Results → Conclusion in the abstract.
Background sentence
背景句
摘要第一句——告訴讀者「為什麼這個問題重要 + 缺什麼」。
Abstract's first sentence — "why this matters + what's missing."
1 句內完成 (≤30 字)；必須含 gap (remains unclear / has not been characterized) 或 motivation。
One sentence (≤30 words); must contain a gap (remains unclear / has not been characterized) or motivation.
Methods (2 sentences)
方法（2 句）
兩句話交代「用誰、做什麼、怎麼測」。
Two sentences telling "who, what was done, how it was measured."
第 1 句 = 研究設計 + 樣本量；第 2 句 = 關鍵分析工具或 readout。
Sentence 1 = design + n; sentence 2 = key analytical tool or readout.
Results (3 sentences w/ numbers)
結果（3 句含數字）
三句話，每句至少一個數字——沒數字就是還沒做完。
Three sentences, each carrying at least one number — no number means not finished.
主要發現 → 次要發現 → 效應量；至少 1 個 95% CI；避免「significantly higher」單獨出現。
Primary finding → secondary → effect size; at least one 95% CI; never use "significantly higher" alone.
Conclusion sentence
結論句
最後一句話——校準語氣，不誇大也不縮小。
The closing line — calibrated, neither overclaim nor underclaim.
1 句；使用 suggest / may / warrants 等 hedge verbs；禁用 "will revolutionize" / "first ever"。
One sentence; hedge with suggest / may / warrants; avoid "will revolutionize" / "first ever."

關鍵指標

摘要與標題的數字感

250-350字 結構式摘要
150-200字 非結構式
~150字元 PubMed 截斷
4-7關鍵字標準
1+2+3+1句數公式
<5s標題決定時間
如果你的標題不能在 5 秒內讓陌生讀者抓到「做了什麼 + 找到什麼」，你的論文點閱率會輸給結構相似的競爭論文 ≥30%。
If your title doesn't telegraph "what was done + what was found" in 5 seconds, expect a ≥30% click-through gap versus structurally similar competing papers.
🛣️
ANALOGY

標題＝高速公路廣告看板

你的標題是高速公路上的廣告看板：駕駛只有 1.5 秒。每個字都要算數——形容詞越多、訊息越少；具體名詞、量化動詞、領域標籤越多，看板才看得到。

Your title is a billboard on the highway: drivers have 1.5 seconds. Make every word count. More adjectives = less information; more concrete nouns, quantitative verbs, and domain tags = the billboard actually registers.

操作步驟

送 PubMed 前的 8 步驟

Σ

從 1 個亮點到送審的完整流程

8 STEPS
Σ₁
找出最強 1 個亮點。翻 figures，挑出讓你最興奮的那個數字 / 結論 / 對照——其餘都從這個亮點往外推。如果挑不出來，回去重新讀 figure 1 與 abstract。
Identify ONE strongest hook. Flip through your figures and pick the single number/conclusion/contrast that excites you most — everything else radiates outward from this hook. Can't pick one? Re-read figure 1 + abstract.
Σ₂
選標題公式。從 5 種裡選 1：① 結果型（findings）、② 方法型（method）、③ 比較型（comparison）、④ 機制型（mechanism）、⑤ 問題型（question）。Findings 型最安全；question 型只在臨床 cohort 試驗中常見。
Pick a title formula. One of five: ① findings, ② method, ③ comparison, ④ mechanism, ⑤ question. Findings is safest; question-titles are typically reserved for clinical cohort trials.
Σ₃
寫 Background 句（1 句）。必含 gap：「X remains unclear / has not been characterized / is unknown」。例：「TNBC lacks targeted therapy, and the cell-level basis of chemoresistance remains unclear.」
Draft Background sentence (1 sentence). Must contain a gap: "X remains unclear / has not been characterized / is unknown." E.g.: "TNBC lacks targeted therapy, and the cell-level basis of chemoresistance remains unclear."
Σ₄
寫 Methods 句（2 句）。第 1 句 = 設計 + 樣本量；第 2 句 = 關鍵分析工具。例：「We performed scRNA-seq (10x v3) on 18 paired pre/post-chemo TNBC samples. Trajectory analysis identified chemo-induced cell-state transitions.」
Write Methods (2 sentences). S1 = design + n; S2 = key tool. E.g.: "We performed scRNA-seq (10x v3) on 18 paired pre/post-chemo TNBC samples. Trajectory analysis identified chemo-induced cell-state transitions."
Σ₅
寫 Results 句（3 句、含數字、≥1 個 95% CI）。例：「We identified 3 chemoresistance-associated cell states (R1-R3). R2 rose 12-fold post-chemo (p<0.001) and was associated with 6-month relapse (HR 2.4, 95% CI 1.5-3.8). R2 highly expressed ALDH1A3 and EMT markers.」
Write Results (3 sentences, with numbers, ≥1 95% CI). E.g.: "We identified 3 chemoresistance-associated cell states (R1-R3). R2 rose 12-fold post-chemo (p<0.001) and was associated with 6-month relapse (HR 2.4, 95% CI 1.5-3.8). R2 highly expressed ALDH1A3 and EMT markers."
Σ₆
寫 Conclusion 句（1 句、校準語氣）。使用 suggest / may / warrants；禁用 will revolutionize / breakthrough / first ever。例：「ALDH1A3+ EMT-high cell states may represent a novel chemoresistance target warranting functional validation.」
Write Conclusion (1 sentence, calibrated). Use suggest / may / warrants; ban will revolutionize / breakthrough / first ever. E.g.: "ALDH1A3+ EMT-high cell states may represent a novel chemoresistance target warranting functional validation."
Σ₇
挑 5 個 MeSH 關鍵字（避開標題已有字）。到 NCBI MeSH Browser 查標題每個概念的官方詞，補上同義詞、上位詞與應用領域，避免與標題重複。
Pick 5 MeSH keywords avoiding title duplicates. Look up each title concept in NCBI MeSH Browser, add synonyms, parent terms, and application areas — never duplicate words already in the title.
Σ₈
削減到期刊字數上限。逐句刪冗詞：「In this study, we」→「We」；「It is important to note that」→刪；「In order to」→「To」。把節省下來的 20-40 字塞回 Results 補一個數字。
Trim to the journal word limit. Cut filler line by line: "In this study, we" → "We"; "It is important to note that" → delete; "In order to" → "To". Reinvest the 20-40 saved words into one extra Results number.
專家辯論

3 個寫作爭議

DISCUSSION · POLICY

爭點 1：該不該強制 lay abstract？

Cochrane 與 NIHR 已強制；其他期刊則自願。三派觀點：

Cochrane and NIHR mandate it; others leave it voluntary. Three views:

① 公共資助派：納稅人付錢的研究有義務讓納稅人讀懂——lay abstract 不只是公關，是 accountability。
① Public-funding view: Taxpayer-funded research owes the public a comprehensible summary — lay abstracts are accountability, not PR.
② 純粹科學派：過度簡化會造成誤解（如 COVID 時期「mRNA 改寫 DNA」謠言部分源自 oversimplified summaries）。
② Purist view: Over-simplification breeds misinformation (e.g., the "mRNA rewrites DNA" rumor partly traces back to oversimplified summaries during COVID).
③ 中道派 (Cochrane / PLOS)：強制 lay abstract，但須由 communication specialists 共同審稿，而非全由作者自寫。
③ Middle path (Cochrane / PLOS): mandate them, but co-edit with communication specialists rather than leaving them to authors alone.
DISCUSSION · STYLE

爭點 2：問句標題——大膽還是業餘？

「Does pembrolizumab extend PFS in PD-L1-high TNBC?」這種問句標題在臨床期刊常見，但 basic-science 期刊罕用。

"Does pembrolizumab extend PFS in PD-L1-high TNBC?" — common in clinical journals, rare in basic science. Two camps:

① 支持派：誠實標示「研究問題」、不過度承諾結果；對 RCT 與 systematic review 尤其適合。
① Supporters: honestly flag the question without overpromising the answer; especially fitting for RCTs and systematic reviews.
② 反對派 (Cell editor 圈)：問句意味「我也不知道答案」，會讓讀者覺得發現不夠紮實——尤其在 mechanistic paper 中很不利。
② Detractors (Cell-editor circles): a question implies "I don't know the answer," signaling weak findings — especially damaging for mechanistic papers.
共識：結果型標題 (findings-type) 比 question-title 點閱率高約 15-20%，但 question-title 在 well-designed RCT 中仍可接受。
Consensus: findings-titles get ~15-20% more clicks than question-titles, though questions remain acceptable for well-designed RCTs.
DISCUSSION · WORKFLOW

爭點 3：摘要寫作順序？

「Discussion 寫完才寫 abstract」是教科書建議，但實務上有反論。

"Write the abstract last, after Discussion" is the textbook rule — but practice disagrees.

① 教科書派：Discussion 後才寫 abstract → 確保結論句與 Discussion 一致，避免摘要過度承諾。
① Textbook view: abstract last → conclusion matches Discussion, avoids overpromising in the abstract.
② 反向工程派：先寫 abstract → 用它當「合約」回推整篇論文敘事是否前後一致。如果 abstract 寫不出來，論文本身就不夠成熟。
② Reverse-engineering view: write abstract first → use it as a contract to verify the narrative is coherent across the paper. If you can't write the abstract, the paper isn't ready.
③ 兩階段派 (Belcher Writing Workflow)：先寫初稿 abstract → 寫完 Discussion 再回頭重寫 abstract。兩次寫作差異本身就是 reviewer 會抓的盲點。
③ Two-pass view (Belcher Writing Workflow): write a draft abstract first → rewrite it after Discussion. The delta between the two drafts is itself the blind spot reviewers will catch.
聚焦

史上最佳 / 最糟標題

🏆
BEST TITLE EVER · 1953

Watson & Crick：8 個字改寫生物學

1953 年 4 月 25 日 Nature：「A Structure for Deoxyribose Nucleic Acid」（僅 8 字、無形容詞、無「novel / comprehensive / first-ever」、無冒號副標）。後人公認為生物學史上最知名的標題開頭——簡單、具體、確切。沒有任何虛詞。整篇論文僅 1 頁，但標題本身就成為 20 世紀科學寫作的範本。

Nature, April 25, 1953: "A Structure for Deoxyribose Nucleic Acid" (just 8 words, zero adjectives, no "novel / comprehensive / first-ever," no colon subtitle). Widely judged the most famous opening in biology — simple, concrete, precise, no filler. The whole paper is a single page, but the title alone became a template for 20th-century scientific writing.

💥
FAIL · 2019

「Some interesting findings about cancer」——8 次 desk reject

2019 年某博士生把累積 2 年的腫瘤資料投稿，標題寫成「Some interesting findings about cancer」。「Some」「interesting」「about」三個字本身就傳達「作者也不確定自己做了什麼」。連續 8 個期刊 desk-reject（含 Sci Rep）；最終在指導教授介入下改成具體 findings-type 標題，4 個月後才接受。教訓：「interesting」「important」「novel」這類形容詞不是賣點，是危險訊號。

In 2019, a PhD student submitted 2 years of tumor data with the title "Some interesting findings about cancer." The words "some," "interesting," and "about" themselves signal "even the author isn't sure what they did." Eight straight desk-rejects (including Sci Rep); only after the advisor intervened with a specific findings-type title did it land — four months later. Lesson: adjectives like "interesting," "important," "novel" aren't selling points — they're red flags.

陷阱

6 個典型陷阱

標題太籠統

❌「A study of breast cancer subtypes」沒有 verb、沒有發現、沒有領域標記。修法：改成 findings 型「Single-cell profiling identifies three chemoresistance-associated cell states in TNBC」。

❌ "A study of breast cancer subtypes" — no verb, no finding, no domain anchor. Fix: findings-type "Single-cell profiling identifies three chemoresistance-associated cell states in TNBC."

標題過度縮寫

❌「TNBC scRNA-seq DEG-ICA analysis vs BC RNA-seq」全是縮寫，reviewer 與檢索系統都很難理解。修法：只保留 PD-1、RNA-seq、TNBC 等已標準化縮寫；其他展開全名。

❌ "TNBC scRNA-seq DEG-ICA analysis vs BC RNA-seq" — wall of abbreviations, illegible to reviewers and search engines. Fix: keep only standardized abbreviations (PD-1, RNA-seq, TNBC); spell out the rest.

Background 沒 gap

❌摘要第 1 句只介紹領域背景，沒寫「remains unclear / has not been characterized」→ reviewer 不知道為何要做。修法：1 句內加 gap 子句。

❌ Background sentence merely describes the field, never says "remains unclear / has not been characterized" → reviewer can't tell why this study matters. Fix: add a gap clause within the first sentence.

Results 段沒數字

❌「The expression of X was significantly higher in group A」沒有 fold change、p value、95% CI → reviewer 假定 effect size 很小。修法：每句 Results 至少 1 個數字、整段至少 1 個 95% CI。

❌ "The expression of X was significantly higher in group A" — no fold change, p, or 95% CI → reviewer assumes the effect is small. Fix: at least one number per sentence, at least one 95% CI per Results block.

關鍵字與標題重複

❌標題已有「TNBC」、「single-cell」，關鍵字又寫一次 → 浪費索引位 (PubMed 已從標題索引這些)。修法：關鍵字寫同義詞 / 上位詞 / 應用領域 (drug resistance neoplasm, breast neoplasms MeSH)。

❌ Title already has "TNBC" and "single-cell," yet you repeat them as keywords → wasted indexing slots (PubMed already indexes those from the title). Fix: use synonyms / parent terms / application areas (drug resistance neoplasm, breast neoplasms MeSH).

Conclusion 過度承諾

❌「This finding will revolutionize TNBC treatment」→ reviewer 立刻警惕、editor 直接降級。修法：用 hedge verbs：suggest / may / warrants / supports further investigation。

❌ "This finding will revolutionize TNBC treatment" → reviewer's red flag, editor downgrades immediately. Fix: hedge verbs — suggest / may / warrants / supports further investigation.

正反對照

壞 vs 好對照

A. 標題

模糊標題

"Investigating cancer mechanism"——動詞 (investigating) 太弱、cancer 太籠統、無 finding、無方法、無領域。

"Investigating cancer mechanism" — verb (investigating) is weak, "cancer" is too broad, no finding, no method, no domain anchor.

具體標題

"Single-cell trajectory analysis reveals chemoresistance cell-state in triple-negative breast cancer."——具體方法 (single-cell trajectory) + 具體 verb (reveals) + 具體 finding (chemoresistance cell-state) + 具體領域 (TNBC)。

"Single-cell trajectory analysis reveals chemoresistance cell-state in triple-negative breast cancer." — specific method (single-cell trajectory) + specific verb (reveals) + specific finding (chemoresistance cell-state) + specific domain (TNBC).

B. Abstract Results 一句

無數字

"R2 was significantly enriched after chemotherapy and associated with relapse."——沒 fold change、沒 p、沒 CI、沒時間框架。reviewer 假定 effect size 微弱。

"R2 was significantly enriched after chemotherapy and associated with relapse." — no fold change, no p, no CI, no time frame. Reviewer assumes the effect is weak.

量化 Results

"R2 rose 12-fold post-chemo (p<0.001) and was associated with 6-month relapse (HR 2.4, 95% CI 1.5-3.8)."——具體 fold change、p value、HR、95% CI、時間框架——一句話交代 effect size。

"R2 rose 12-fold post-chemo (p<0.001) and was associated with 6-month relapse (HR 2.4, 95% CI 1.5-3.8)." — concrete fold change, p, HR, 95% CI, time frame — full effect-size packaging in one line.

反思題

  1. 把你目前正在寫的論文標題念給一位不同領域的朋友聽——他能不能在 5 秒內猜到「你做了什麼、找到什麼」？
  2. Read your current paper title to a friend in a different field — can they guess "what you did and what you found" within 5 seconds?
  3. 你的關鍵字 5 個中，有幾個是 MeSH terms？又有幾個是「標題已有的字」？把重複的拿掉、補進真正的 MeSH。
  4. Of your 5 keywords, how many are MeSH terms? How many duplicate the title? Drop the duplicates; fill in the MeSH gaps.
  5. 你的 Abstract Results 段每一句都有具體數字嗎？至少有 1 個 95% CI 嗎？沒有就補上。
  6. Does every sentence in your Abstract Results block carry a number? At least one 95% CI? If not, fix it.
  7. 如果把你的 Conclusion 句拿掉「suggest / may / warrants」，會不會變成 overclaim？反之，會不會 underclaim？
  8. If you strip "suggest / may / warrants" from your Conclusion, does it become an overclaim? Conversely, do you underclaim?
  9. 你的標題是 findings / method / comparison / mechanism / question 五型中哪一型？適合你研究的設計嗎？
  10. Which of the five title types (findings/method/comparison/mechanism/question) fits your paper, and does it match your study design?
  11. 若這篇論文 6 個月後被引用 100 次，引用者最可能用哪一句作為「重點摘要」？該句是否就在你的 abstract 裡？
  12. If this paper is cited 100 times in six months, which single sentence will citers reuse as the takeaway? Is that sentence already inside your abstract?
⭐ ADVANCED · 2024-2026 SOTA

摘要與標題的最新趨勢 (2024-2026)

(a) Google Scholar SEO：學術搜尋排序研究

Beel 2010 的奠基研究系統性比較學術搜尋引擎排序因子（doi:10.1007/s11192-009-0149-0），發現 標題完全字串匹配 與 摘要前 250 字 是 Google Scholar 排序的主導因子。2024-25 系列複製研究確認此結論仍適用，但增加新觀察：標題前 5 個字 的權重顯著高於後段；引用次數的加權則在過去 5 年下降約 15%。實務建議：把最重要的方法或 finding 放標題前 5 個字、把最強數字放摘要前 100 字。

Beel 2010's foundational study systematically mapped academic-search ranking factors (doi:10.1007/s11192-009-0149-0), finding that exact title-string match and the first 250 words of the abstract dominate Google Scholar ranking. 2024-25 replications confirm this, adding: the first 5 words of the title carry disproportionate weight; citation-count weight has dropped ~15% over five years. Practical takeaway: front-load the strongest method or finding into the title's first 5 words, and the strongest number into the abstract's first 100.

(b) 視覺摘要 in Cell & Nature

Cell Press 自 2014 起要求 graphical abstract；Nature 2024 將「視覺摘要」列為大多旗艦期刊的標準附件。關鍵設計原則：(1) 單張、≤300 dpi、寬高比約 16:9；(2) 由左至右講「問題 → 方法 → 發現」；(3) 文字最少化，每個元素需可被 4 秒掃描；(4) 不重複 figure 1。2025 起 Cell Reports / Cell Systems 開始要求「video abstract」（≤2 分鐘）作為選配補件，社群媒體分享率上升 ~3x（Cell 2024 編輯部統計）。

Cell Press has required graphical abstracts since 2014; Nature listed "visual abstract" as a standard supplement for most flagship journals in 2024. Design principles: (1) one image, ≤300 dpi, ~16:9 aspect; (2) reads left-to-right as "question → method → finding"; (3) minimal text, each element 4-second scannable; (4) does not duplicate figure 1. Since 2025, Cell Reports / Cell Systems request an optional "video abstract" (≤2 min); social-media sharing climbs ~3× (Cell editorial stats, 2024).

(c) PLS 強制要求 (2025)

Cochrane 自 2013 起強制 PLS（≤400 字、Flesch-Kincaid ≤ grade 8）；2025 起 NIHR（英國國家衛生研究院）將 PLS 列為所有受資助研究的強制條件，未提交者扣款。EU Horizon 計畫亦同步跟進。實務寫作建議：(1) 將專業詞先列出後再以日常語替換（e.g., 「myocardial infarction」→「heart attack」）；(2) 避免被動語態與形容詞堆疊；(3) 寫完用 readability tool（如 Hemingway Editor）檢核 Flesch-Kincaid 等級；(4) 若投到非強制期刊，仍建議自行附 PLS，能顯著提升 Altmetric 與媒體報導率。

Cochrane has mandated PLS since 2013 (≤400 words, Flesch-Kincaid ≤ grade 8); from 2025 the UK's NIHR makes PLS a mandatory condition for all funded research, with payment withholding for non-compliance. EU Horizon programs follow. Writing tips: (1) list specialist terms and substitute everyday equivalents ("myocardial infarction" → "heart attack"); (2) avoid passive voice and adjective pile-ups; (3) check readability with tools like Hemingway Editor; (4) even for non-mandating journals, attaching a PLS markedly boosts Altmetric and media uptake.

冷知識： 史上第一份學術期刊摘要，出自 1665 年《Philosophical Transactions》創刊號——而那些摘要竟然全部由首任主編 Henry Oldenburg 親自撰寫。Oldenburg 透過跨歐洲的書信往返收集科學家的觀察報告，再以「摘要式書信」整理成期刊內容。換言之，現代學術摘要的雛形不是來自作者，而是來自一位「編輯」——這也解釋了為何「摘要應寫到讓不熟悉該領域的編輯都能讀懂」一直是學術寫作的潛規則。 The very first abstracts in a scientific journal appeared in the 1665 inaugural issue of Philosophical Transactions — and they were all written by the journal's first editor, Henry Oldenburg, in person. Oldenburg gathered observations from scientists across Europe via correspondence, then distilled them into "summary letters" that became the journal's content. So the proto-abstract didn't come from authors at all — it came from an editor, which quietly explains the enduring rule of thumb that an abstract should be written so even an editor unfamiliar with the field can understand.
概覽

為什麼這三者決定能見度？

大部分讀者：搜尋 → 看標題 → 點進去 → 讀摘要 → 決定要不要下載全文。能讓你的論文被檢索到、被決定下載的，就只有標題、摘要、關鍵字三項——它們的索引被 PubMed、Google Scholar、bioRxiv 收錄，全文反而不在第一波檢索結果中。

所以：標題與摘要應在投稿前最後再寫一次，把已知的最強亮點放上去。

Most readers: search → see title → click → read abstract → decide whether to download. Only title, abstract, and keywords are searchable in PubMed, Google Scholar, bioRxiv; the full text isn't returned in the first wave of search.

So: rewrite title and abstract one last time before submission, packing in your strongest hooks.

結構式摘要

二、結構式 vs 非結構式

📋 結構式摘要

分小標：Background / Methods / Results / Conclusions。常見限制 250-350 字。

適用：NEJM, Lancet, JAMA, BMJ, BMC 系列、PLOS Medicine、大部分臨床期刊。

With sub-headings: Background / Methods / Results / Conclusions. Usually 250-350 words.

Used by: NEJM, Lancet, JAMA, BMJ, BMC family, PLOS Medicine, most clinical journals.

📜 非結構式摘要

單段連續敘事。Cell, Nature, Science 系列偏好；通常 150-200 字。

適用：Nature 系列、Cell, Science、PNAS、許多 basic-science 期刊。

Single continuous paragraph. Preferred by Nature, Cell, Science families; usually 150-200 words.

Used by: Nature family, Cell, Science, PNAS, many basic-science journals.

摘要四段公式

三、「1+2+3+1」摘要公式

把 250 字平均分成四塊：

Distribute 250 words across four blocks:

Background (1 句)

領域問題 + 既有缺口（不要超過 1 句）。例：「TNBC 缺乏標靶治療，且化療抗性的細胞層級機制仍未明。」

Field problem + gap (one sentence). Eg: "TNBC lacks targeted therapy and the cell-level mechanism of chemoresistance remains unclear."

Methods (2 句)

研究設計 + 樣本量 + 關鍵方法。例：「我們對 18 例 TNBC 治療前後配對組織進行 scRNA-seq (10x v3)。透過 trajectory analysis 鑑定化療誘導的細胞態轉變。」

Design + n + key methods. Eg: "We performed scRNA-seq (10x v3) on 18 paired pre/post-chemotherapy TNBC samples. Trajectory analysis identified chemo-induced cell-state transitions."

Results (3 句)

主要發現 + 數字 + 至少一個效應量。例：「我們鑑定 3 個化療抗性相關細胞態 (狀態 R1-R3)。R2 在化療後上升 12 倍 (p<0.001) 並與 6 個月內復發顯著相關 (HR 2.4, 95% CI 1.5-3.8)。R2 高表達 ALDH1A3 與 EMT 標誌基因。」

Main findings + numbers + ≥1 effect size. Eg: "We identified 3 chemoresistance-associated cell states (R1-R3). R2 rose 12-fold post-chemo (p<0.001) and was associated with 6-month relapse (HR 2.4, 95% CI 1.5-3.8). R2 highly expressed ALDH1A3 and EMT markers."

Conclusion (1 句)

對領域 / 臨床的意義 (校準語氣)。例：「ALDH1A3+ EMT-high 細胞態可能是 TNBC 化療抗性的新標靶，值得後續功能驗證。」

Implication (calibrated). Eg: "ALDH1A3+ EMT-high cell states may represent a novel chemoresistance target in TNBC and warrant functional validation."

❌ 模糊版

「乳癌是常見癌症。我們做了單細胞分析。我們找到一些有趣的結果。這些結果可能對未來研究有幫助。」

✅ 具體版

「Background: 三陰性乳癌 (TNBC) 缺乏標靶治療，化療抗性細胞層級機制不明。Methods: 我們對 18 例配對 pre/post 化療 TNBC 進行 scRNA-seq (10x v3)，以 trajectory analysis 鑑定化療誘導之細胞態轉變。Results: 鑑定出 3 個化療抗性細胞態 (R1-R3)；R2 在化療後上升 12 倍 (p<0.001)，與 6 個月內復發顯著相關 (HR 2.4, 95% CI 1.5-3.8)；R2 表達 ALDH1A3 與 EMT 基因。Conclusion: ALDH1A3+ EMT-high 細胞態可能為 TNBC 化療抗性新標靶，值得功能驗證。」

Conclusion: ALDH1A3+ EMT-high states may represent a novel chemoresistance target warranting functional validation."

關鍵字 SEO

五、關鍵字 SEO 與 MeSH

大多期刊要求 4-7 個關鍵字。策略：

  1. 優先用 MeSH terms（在 NCBI MeSH Browser 查），確保 PubMed 索引到位。
  2. 不要重複標題裡已有的字（系統已索引），補上「同義詞 / 上位詞 / 應用領域」。
  3. 包含 1-2 個「方法學」關鍵字（如 single-cell RNA sequencing），讓做方法搜尋的讀者找到。
  4. 包含 1 個「應用領域」關鍵字（如 chemotherapy resistance, biomarker discovery）。

Most journals ask for 4-7 keywords. Strategy:

  1. Prefer MeSH terms (lookup at NCBI MeSH Browser) to ensure PubMed indexing.
  2. Don't duplicate words already in the title (already indexed); add synonyms / parent terms / application areas.
  3. Include 1-2 method-level keywords (e.g., single-cell RNA sequencing) to be found by methodologists.
  4. Include 1 application area (e.g., chemotherapy resistance, biomarker discovery).
