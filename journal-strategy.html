故事開場

場景：Daniel 在 24 小時內收到第一封 desk-reject

SCENE · 2025 · Lab Meeting

從「投 Nature 試試看」到「Cancer Res 才是你的家」

博士生 Daniel 累積了一個 incremental 的 cohort study：240 名肺癌病人加上 4 個既有 risk markers 的延伸驗證。資料漂亮、方法紮實——但「新穎度」其實只是把現有 markers 在新族群跑一次。他熱血上頭，直接把 manuscript 投 Nature。24 小時內就收到 editor 一封冷冰冰的 desk-rejection：「Scope mismatch, lacks the conceptual advance expected by our readership.」

Daniel 在 lab meeting 上沮喪：「我的數據明明很好啊。」Mentor Prof. Wu 平靜回覆：「數據好，跟 Nature 適合，是兩件事。Nature 要的是 paradigm-shift；你做的是 paradigm-confirm。把它放對家：Cell Reports Medicine、Genome Biology、Cancer Research 才是你的 IF tier 與受眾。把 incremental work 投 CNS 是浪費 3 個月。」

Daniel 重新讀 Cancer Research 的 "Aims & Scope"、挑近 6 個月 8 篇類似主題的文章參考結構、改完 cover letter（指名 deputy editor、明說與哪篇近期文章互補）。第二次投稿，送外審、3 個月內 accepted。事後他自己歸納：「投錯期刊不只是慢——是把好數據浪費在 editor 的回收桶。」

PhD student Daniel has built an incremental cohort study: 240 lung-cancer patients validating 4 existing risk markers in a new population. The data are clean, the methods are solid — but the "novelty" is, frankly, just re-running known markers in a new cohort. Buzzing with confidence, he submits the manuscript to Nature. Within 24 hours, the editor's desk-rejection arrives: "Scope mismatch, lacks the conceptual advance expected by our readership."

At lab meeting, Daniel is deflated. "But the data are good." His mentor Prof. Wu replies calmly: "Good data and Nature fit are two different things. Nature wants paradigm-shifts; you produced paradigm-confirmation. Put it in the right home: Cell Reports Medicine, Genome Biology, or Cancer Research — that is your IF tier and audience. Sending incremental work to CNS is a 3-month tax on yourself."

Daniel re-reads Cancer Research's "Aims & Scope," studies 8 recent papers on similar topics, and rewrites the cover letter — naming the deputy editor and explicitly tying his work to a recent Cancer Res paper. On the second submission, the manuscript goes to external review and is accepted within 3 months. His own takeaway: "Picking the wrong journal isn't just slow — it's letting good data die in an editor's reject bin."

核心詞彙

14 個關鍵術語（雙版本）

每個詞兩個版本：🧒「給小孩看」用比喻、📐「給研究者看」給定義。

Each term has two versions: 🧒 "for kids" uses a metaphor; 📐 "for researchers" gives the formal definition.

Scope
期刊主題範圍
每本期刊都像不同的「店」——賣咖啡的不會賣壽司。Scope 就是這家店的菜單。
Every journal is like a different "shop" — a coffee shop won't sell sushi. Scope is the menu.
期刊明文界定的主題範圍與受眾。Editor 用 scope 做 desk-reject 第一道篩選；不符即 24 hr 內退稿。
The journal's explicitly stated topical range and audience. Editors apply scope as the first desk-reject filter; mismatches return in 24 hr.
Aims & Scope
宗旨與範圍
期刊官網上的「自我介紹」——它說自己想登什麼樣的文章。
The "About me" page on a journal's website — what kinds of papers it wants.
期刊網站固定段落；建議投稿前讀 + 翻近 6 個月 5-10 篇實際文章比對。
A fixed section on the journal site; read it AND skim 5-10 actual papers from the last 6 months for ground truth.
Impact Factor (IF)
影響因子
期刊的「人氣分數」——平均每篇文章被引用幾次。但人氣 ≠ 品質。
A journal's "popularity score" — average citations per article. But popularity ≠ quality.
Clarivate JCR 計算：前 2 年文章在本年的被引次數 ÷ 前 2 年發文總數。1955 Garfield 提出；DORA 2012 呼籲停止以 IF 評鑑個人。
Clarivate JCR formula: citations in year Y to articles from Y-1 and Y-2, divided by articles published in Y-1 and Y-2. Proposed by Garfield 1955; DORA 2012 calls to stop using IF to evaluate individuals.
CiteScore
CiteScore
Scopus 版本的人氣分數，看 4 年的窗口而不是 2 年。
Scopus's version of popularity, using a 4-year window instead of 2.
Elsevier Scopus 2016 推出；分子分母都用 4 年區間，較 IF 穩定。涵蓋更多非 SCIE 期刊。
Elsevier Scopus, launched 2016; both numerator and denominator use a 4-year window, making it more stable than IF. Covers more non-SCIE journals.
SNIP
SNIP 標準化指標
把「人氣分數」按學門校正——數學期刊跟生醫期刊比就公平了。
A popularity score normalized across fields — math and biomed journals become fairer to compare.
Source Normalized Impact per Paper；Moed 2010 提出。校正不同學門引用密度。
Source Normalized Impact per Paper; Moed 2010. Corrects for citation density differences across disciplines.
SJR (Scimago)
Scimago 期刊排名
像 Google PageRank：被「重要期刊」引用的權重比較高。
Like Google PageRank: citations from "important journals" weigh more.
Scimago Lab + Univ. of Granada；PageRank 概念套到期刊。免費、開放查詢。
Scimago Lab + Univ. of Granada; PageRank applied to journals. Free and openly searchable.
JCR
期刊引用報告
放著各家期刊「成績單」的官方資料庫。
The official database holding journal "report cards."
Journal Citation Reports，Clarivate Analytics 年度更新；發布 IF、JCI、quartile (Q1-Q4)。
Journal Citation Reports, Clarivate Analytics, updated annually; publishes IF, JCI, and quartile (Q1-Q4).
Open Access (OA)
開放取用
論文不用付錢就能讀——像圖書館變成 24/7 免費。
Papers free for anyone to read — like a library open 24/7 for free.
BOAI 2002 定義：免費 + 大部分再利用權。4 種主流模式：Gold、Hybrid、Green、Diamond。
BOAI 2002 definition: free access + broad reuse rights. Four main models: Gold, Hybrid, Green, Diamond.
Gold OA
金色 OA
作者付一筆「上架費 (APC)」，文章從第 1 天就免費讓所有人看。
Author pays a "shelf fee (APC)" so the article is free for everyone from day one.
期刊本身全 OA（PLOS、BMC、Frontiers、eLife）。作者透過 APC 支付，讀者完全免費。
Fully OA journals (PLOS, BMC, Frontiers, eLife). Author pays APC; reader pays nothing.
Hybrid OA
混合型 OA
圖書館已經付了訂閱費，作者卻可以額外再付一筆讓自己的文章免費——被罵 double-dipping。
Libraries already pay subscription, but authors can also pay extra to make their own article free — critics call this double-dipping.
傳統訂閱型期刊提供的「付費 OA」選項。被批評為兩邊收費；Plan S 2018 不允許 hybrid 為合規 route。
A paid OA option inside subscription journals. Criticized for billing both sides; Plan S 2018 excludes hybrid as a compliant route.
Diamond OA
鑽石 OA
兩邊都不用付錢——讀者免費、作者也免費。學會或政府出錢養期刊。
Nobody pays — free to read AND free to publish. A society or government funds the journal.
無 APC、無訂閱；多由學會、機構、聯盟資助。2022 cOAlition S Action Plan 將 Diamond 列為優先支持模式。
No APC, no subscription; funded by societies, institutions, or consortia. The 2022 cOAlition S Action Plan prioritizes Diamond.
APC
文章處理費
作者付給 OA 期刊的「上架費」——從 $1500 到 $11000 不等。
The "shelf fee" authors pay an OA journal — ranges from $1500 to $11000.
Article Processing Charge；Nature/Cell OA APC ≥ $11000；PLOS ONE ~$1805 (2025)。可由 funder/機構付，部分有 LMIC 減免。
Article Processing Charge; Nature/Cell OA APC ≥ $11000; PLOS ONE ~$1805 (2025). Often covered by funders/institutions; LMIC waivers available.
Predatory journal
掠奪性期刊
假裝是學術期刊、收錢就刊文、沒做真正同儕審查的「假學術店」。
A fake "academic shop" that pretends to be a journal, charges fees, and publishes anything without real peer review.
Beall 2010 定名；偽造 IF、editorial board 名單盜用、無 indexing、退費條款不透明。Cabells Predatory Reports 2025 收錄 ~15000 家。
Coined by Beall 2010; fake IF, hijacked editorial boards, no indexing, opaque refund terms. Cabells Predatory Reports 2025 lists ~15000.
Pre-submission inquiry
投稿前詢問
投高分期刊前先寫 email 問編輯：「我這個題目你們有興趣嗎？」
Before submitting to a top journal, email the editor: "Would this topic interest you?"
1 段 abstract + 主要發現 + 為何適合該刊；editor 通常 1-2 週回覆 encourage / not a good fit，省下 cover letter 與格式時間。
A 1-paragraph abstract + main findings + journal-fit rationale; editors usually reply in 1-2 weeks with encourage / not a good fit, saving formatting and cover-letter time.

步驟流程

從寫稿到投稿的 8 步

Σ₁ 寫之前先鎖目標：在動筆 introduction 前，鎖定 1 個 first-choice + 2 個 backup 期刊。所有 figure 數量、word limit、reference style 都按 first-choice 寫。

Σ₂ 讀 Aims & Scope：讀官網「Aims & Scope」+ 近 6 個月 5 篇實際發表文章。確認 scope 重疊、article type 對應、受眾合拍。

Σ₃ 在 JCR / Scopus / DOAJ 驗證：查 JCR (IF + quartile)、Scopus (CiteScore + SNIP)、DOAJ (Gold OA 認證)。不要查已停止維護的 Beall's List；改用 Cabells Predatory Reports 或 Think.Check.Submit.。

Σ₄ 對高分期刊先發 pre-submission inquiry：1 段 abstract + 主要發現 + 為何適合該刊。editor 通常 1-2 週回覆，省下你白寫一個版本的格式與 cover letter。

Σ₅ 按期刊指引格式化：字數、figure 數、reference style、supplementary 限制嚴格對齊；editor 會看格式是否認真。

Σ₆ 完成 CRediT、ORCID、COI：CRediT 14 角色分配、所有作者 ORCID 串接、Competing Interests 明確聲明。資料缺即 desk-reject。

Σ₇ 寫 cover letter：3 段：① 編輯姓名 + 為何選此刊（連結近期 1 篇該刊文章）② 主要發現一句話 + 數字 ③ 建議審稿人 + 排除審稿人 + 倫理聲明。

Σ₈ 投稿 + 追蹤：透過期刊 submission portal（ScholarOne、Editorial Manager、eJP）；保留 manuscript ID、定期登入追狀態；超過 median time-to-first-decision 1.5 倍可禮貌詢問 editor。

Σ₁ Lock the target BEFORE writing: pick 1 first-choice + 2 backups before drafting the introduction. Figure counts, word limits, and reference style all follow the first-choice.

Σ₂ Read Aims & Scope: read the journal's "Aims & Scope" + 5 actual articles from the last 6 months. Confirm scope overlap, article-type fit, and audience match.

Σ₃ Verify in JCR / Scopus / DOAJ: check JCR (IF + quartile), Scopus (CiteScore + SNIP), and DOAJ (Gold OA certification). Don't use Beall's List (no longer maintained); use Cabells Predatory Reports or Think.Check.Submit.

Σ₄ Send a pre-submission inquiry to high-IF journals: 1-paragraph abstract + main findings + why-fit. Editors reply within 1-2 weeks, saving you from formatting and a cover-letter draft for nothing.

Σ₅ Format to the journal's author guide: word count, figure count, reference style, supplementary limits — strictly aligned. Editors notice when formatting is lazy.

Σ₆ CRediT, ORCID, COI: assign 14 CRediT roles, connect all authors' ORCIDs, declare Competing Interests explicitly. Missing items = desk-reject.

Σ₇ Write the cover letter: 3 paragraphs — ① editor's name + why this journal (link to a recent paper there) ② one-sentence main finding + a number ③ suggested reviewers + excluded reviewers + ethics declaration.

Σ₈ Submit and track: submit via the journal portal (ScholarOne, Editorial Manager, eJP); keep the manuscript ID, log in periodically; if 1.5× the median time-to-first-decision passes, politely email the editor.

專家辯論

三道辯論題

辯論 1：Impact Factor — 有用還是有害？

挺方：對快速估計期刊 visibility 與所在 quartile 仍然好用；資金、晉升委員會也離不開它。
反方：DORA 2012 + Leiden Manifesto 2015 早已呼籲停用：(a) IF 是期刊指標、不是文章指標；(b) 易被 review 文章、self-citation 灌水；(c) 不同學門無法比較。立場：用 IF 認識期刊定位 OK；用 IF 評鑑個人或單篇文章 = 70 年的方法論誤用。

For: useful for quick estimates of journal visibility and quartile; funders and promotion committees still rely on it.
Against: DORA 2012 and Leiden Manifesto 2015 already call for retirement: (a) IF is a journal metric, not an article metric; (b) review articles and self-citation inflate it; (c) cross-discipline comparison is invalid. Stance: using IF to understand journal positioning is fine; using IF to evaluate individuals or single papers is a 70-year methodological misuse.

辯論 2：Hybrid OA — 公平妥協還是雙重收費？

挺方：提供作者選擇彈性；不破壞傳統訂閱模式；過渡期間維持期刊運作。
反方：圖書館已付訂閱費、作者又付 APC，等同雙重收費 (double-dipping)。Plan S 2018 將 hybrid 排除為合規 route，僅允許「轉型協議 (transformative agreement)」框架內的 hybrid 在 2024 年底前過渡。立場：Hybrid 不是長期解，是過渡期；Diamond 與全 Gold 才是穩態。

For: gives authors flexibility; preserves subscription business model; bridges journal operations during transition.
Against: libraries pay subscription AND authors pay APC — literal double-dipping. Plan S 2018 excludes hybrid as compliant, allowing only hybrid inside transformative agreements through end of 2024. Stance: hybrid is a transition, not a destination; Diamond and full Gold are the steady state.

辯論 3：應該高到低逐級投稿，還是直接投對位期刊？

挺方（逐級）：每次拒稿可拿到 review comments、文章越改越好。
反方（直接投對位）：(a) 多數高分期刊是 desk-reject (沒拿到 review，浪費時間)；(b) Daniel 故事即典範——3 個月 vs 直接 accept；(c) Scientific reputation 累積靠「目標讀者真的引用你」，不靠期刊 logo。立場：除非 paper 真的有 paradigm-shift 等級，否則直接投對位期刊。incremental 不要投 CNS。

For (top-down): each rejection yields review comments; the paper improves with each pass.
Against (go specialty): (a) most top journals desk-reject (no review = pure time waste); (b) Daniel's story shows it — 3 months wasted vs straight-to-accept; (c) scientific reputation accrues from being cited by your real audience, not from a journal logo. Stance: unless the paper is genuinely paradigm-shifting, go straight to the right specialty. Don't send incremental work to CNS.

案例聚焦

模範與警示

MODEL 模範：BMC family

BMC (BioMed Central) 1999 創立、後併入 Springer Nature；是 Gold OA 模式的早期實踐者。BMC Medicine、BMC Bioinformatics、BMC Genomics 等子刊維持嚴格 peer review、平均 first decision 60-80 天、reviewer report 公開（opt-in）。APC 約 $2690 (2025)，LMIC waiver 可全免。啟示：Gold OA ≠ 低品質——透明、indexed、有真同儕審查才是判準。

BMC (BioMed Central), founded 1999 and later acquired by Springer Nature, was an early Gold OA practitioner. Sub-journals such as BMC Medicine, BMC Bioinformatics, and BMC Genomics maintain rigorous peer review, average first decision in 60-80 days, and offer opt-in open reviewer reports. APC is ~$2690 (2025), with full LMIC waivers. Lesson: Gold OA does not equal low quality — the real test is transparency, indexing, and real peer review.

WARNING 警示：OMICS International

OMICS International（印度，總部 Hyderabad）2008 起運作數百本「假學術」期刊，誇大 IF、盜用 editorial board 名單、隱藏 APC、無真正同儕審查。2016 年美國 FTC 控告 OMICS，2019 法院判決賠償 $50M+ 美元並禁止其欺騙性行銷。啟示：看到「Universal Impact Factor」「Global Impact Factor」這種非 JCR/Scopus 來源的指標，幾乎一定是掠奪性期刊。

OMICS International (India, headquartered in Hyderabad) ran hundreds of fake academic journals from 2008 onward, fabricating IF claims, hijacking editorial board lists, hiding APC fees, and skipping real peer review. The US FTC sued OMICS in 2016; in 2019 the court ordered $50M+ in damages and an injunction against its deceptive marketing. Lesson: if you see metrics like "Universal Impact Factor" or "Global Impact Factor" — anything other than JCR/Scopus — it is almost certainly predatory.

陷阱

6 個典型陷阱

陷阱 1：沒讀 scope 就投

Editor 第一個動作就是看 scope；不符就 24 hr desk-reject。Daniel 投 Nature 的故事就是這條陷阱。

The first thing an editor checks is scope; a mismatch returns a 24-hour desk-reject. Daniel's Nature story is exactly this pitfall.

陷阱 2：相信假指標

「Universal Impact Factor」「Global Impact Factor」「Cosmos IF」全是假指標。真實期刊指標只有：JCR IF、CiteScore、SNIP、SJR、JCI。

"Universal Impact Factor", "Global Impact Factor", "Cosmos IF" are all fakes. Legitimate journal metrics: JCR IF, CiteScore, SNIP, SJR, JCI — that's the full list.

陷阱 3：funder 強制 OA 卻選 hybrid

NIH、Wellcome、cOAlition S 等 funder 要求即時 OA，許多 hybrid 期刊不符合 Plan S 規範。投稿前用 Journal Checker Tool 確認 compliance。

NIH, Wellcome, and cOAlition S funders mandate immediate OA; many hybrid journals are not Plan S compliant. Run the Journal Checker Tool before submitting.

陷阱 4：同時多投（學術倫理紅線）

同一份 manuscript 同時投兩本以上期刊 = ICMJE / COPE 明令禁止；被發現會被全部期刊禁投數年，PhD/PI 紀錄留案。

Submitting the same manuscript to two journals at once is explicitly banned by ICMJE / COPE; if caught, you face multi-year bans across journals and a permanent record on your PhD/PI file.

陷阱 5：標題對讀者無感

Genome Biology 讀者要看到「method、pipeline、resource」字眼；Cancer Research 要看到「mechanism、target、cohort」。標題不對位 = editor 第一秒就感覺不合。

Genome Biology readers expect words like "method", "pipeline", "resource"; Cancer Research expects "mechanism", "target", "cohort". Mismatched titles signal poor fit to the editor in the first second.

陷阱 6：cover letter 留「Dear Editor」

沒寫 editor 姓名 = 直接讓編輯知道你沒查過。所有期刊官網都列 Editor-in-Chief 與 Deputy/Section Editor；找最對主題的 editor 寫姓名。

Leaving "Dear Editor" tells the editor you didn't bother checking. Every journal website lists the Editor-in-Chief and Deputy / Section Editors; find the one whose section matches your topic and use their name.

對照範例

Cover Letter：差 vs 好

❌ 差的範例

"Dear Editor, please consider our paper."

問題：(1) 沒 editor 姓名 (2) 沒講為何選此刊 (3) 沒主要發現 (4) 沒建議審稿人。Editor 30 秒掃完，沒理由送外審。

"Dear Editor, please consider our paper."

Problems: (1) no editor name (2) no reason for journal fit (3) no main finding (4) no suggested reviewers. The editor scans for 30 seconds and has zero reason to send it out for review.

✅ 好的範例

"Dear Dr. Smith, We are submitting '[Title]' as an Original Research Article in Genome Biology. Our study addresses [gap] by [approach], identifying [main finding with number]. We believe this is a strong fit because [link to recent Genome Biol paper]. Suggested reviewers: Dr. X (institution, expertise); excluded: Dr. Z (competitor)."

4 個元素齊全：deputy editor 姓名、article type、研究 gap + 方法 + 數字、為何 fit、suggested/excluded reviewers。Editor 信任你做過功課。

"Dear Dr. Smith, We are submitting '[Title]' as an Original Research Article in Genome Biology. Our study addresses [gap] by [approach], identifying [main finding with number]. We believe this is a strong fit because [link to recent Genome Biol paper]. Suggested reviewers: Dr. X (institution, expertise); excluded: Dr. Z (competitor)."

All four elements present: deputy editor name, article type, gap + approach + number, why-fit, suggested/excluded reviewers. Editor trusts you did your homework.

進階 2024-2026 SOTA

進階補充：3 個 2024-2026 重大議題

(a) Plan S / cOAlition S 2024-25 transformative agreement 更新

cOAlition S 自 2018 提出 Plan S，要求成員 funder（European Commission, Wellcome, UKRI, FCT, NWO 等）資助研究成果即時 OA。2024 年底前 transformative agreement (TA) 為過渡期合規 route；自 2025 起 cOAlition S 不再認可 hybrid 為合規模式。2024-25 重點動作：(1) Journal Checker Tool 持續更新；(2) Diamond OA Action Plan 推出 DIAMAS、CRAFT-OA、ALMASI 等基礎設施計畫；(3) Rights Retention Strategy 推廣 author-side CC BY 授權。實務影響：歐洲資助 + 中央政府研究計畫的稿件不應再投 paywall-only 或 non-TA hybrid 期刊。

cOAlition S launched Plan S in 2018, requiring member funders (European Commission, Wellcome, UKRI, FCT, NWO, etc.) to mandate immediate OA on funded outputs. Transformative agreements (TAs) served as a transitional compliant route through end of 2024; from 2025 onward, hybrid is no longer recognized as Plan S compliant. 2024-25 highlights: (1) Journal Checker Tool actively maintained; (2) Diamond OA Action Plan launches DIAMAS, CRAFT-OA, ALMASI infrastructure projects; (3) Rights Retention Strategy promotes author-side CC BY licensing. Practical impact: manuscripts from EU and central-government grants should no longer go to paywall-only or non-TA hybrid journals.

(b) eLife 的 publish-then-review 模式 (2023-26)

eLife 自 2023 年起廢除傳統 accept/reject 二元決策；所有 senior editor 願意送外審的 preprint，外審完成後一律以「Reviewed Preprint」形式發表，附上 reviewer reports + editor assessment + significance/strength labels。優點：(1) 所有 review 工作不會被埋葬；(2) 透明度高；(3) 「發表」與「品質評估」解耦。爭議：(1) 不再有 reject 等於失去「品管」訊號；(2) HHMI 2024 結束資助 eLife 部分原因即此。2026 觀察點：模式是否擴及 PLOS、F1000Research 系列。

From 2023, eLife abolished the traditional accept/reject dichotomy. Any preprint a senior editor agrees to send for external review is published as a "Reviewed Preprint" with reviewer reports + editor assessment + significance/strength labels. Pros: (1) review work is never buried; (2) high transparency; (3) decouples "publishing" from "quality assessment". Controversies: (1) the absence of rejection removes the quality-control signal; (2) HHMI ended funding to eLife in 2024 in part for this reason. 2026 watch: whether the model expands to PLOS or F1000Research family.

(c) 掠奪性期刊 AI 偵測工具 2025-26

(1) Cabells Predatory Reports（付費訂閱）：~15000 家收錄，提供 70+ behaviour indicators，分 Yellow/Red flag。(2) Think.Check.Submit.（免費 checklist）：作者導向的 10 項自評清單。(3) 2025-26 新趨勢：LLM 輔助偵測——以 manuscript metadata + editorial board metadata + 網站抓取為輸入，訓練 transformer 模型給予 risk score（如 Strinzel et al. 2024 在 BMC Med 提出的方法）。實務建議：先用 Think.Check.Submit. 自評 → 對照 Cabells / DOAJ → 還不確定時用 LLM 評估 + 諮詢 librarian。

(1) Cabells Predatory Reports (paid subscription): ~15000 entries, 70+ behavioral indicators, Yellow/Red flag classification. (2) Think.Check.Submit. (free checklist): author-facing 10-item self-assessment. (3) 2025-26 trend: LLM-assisted detection — manuscript metadata + editorial-board metadata + website scraping fed to transformer-based risk-scoring models (e.g., Strinzel et al. 2024 in BMC Med). Practical recipe: start with Think.Check.Submit. → cross-check against Cabells / DOAJ → if still unsure, run an LLM check and consult a librarian.

歷史軼事

Garfield 1955：IF 從來不是「品質」指標

Impact Factor 是 Eugene Garfield 在 1955 年 Science 上提出的概念——但他不是為了評鑑研究品質而設計這個指標。當年的目的非常實際：幫圖書館員決定要訂閱哪幾本期刊。圖書館預算有限，需要量化指標來判斷「哪些期刊比較常被引用、值得花錢訂閱」。

Garfield 本人在後來的訪談（2005 BMJ, 2006 JAMA）多次強調：「IF 不應該用來評鑑個別研究者或單篇論文。」但這個訊息被忽略了 70 年——晉升委員會、招聘委員會、政府資助機構全都把 IF 拿來算個人分數。2012 年 DORA 聯署正式呼籲停止這個誤用、2015 年 Leiden Manifesto 再次發聲、2024-25 全球機構陸續簽署 CoARA (Coalition for Advancing Research Assessment)。

核心啟示：IF 的誤用是學術界 70 年的方法論意外。你在用 IF 估期刊定位時很合理，但用它評鑑「這個人厲不厲害」「這篇文章好不好」——你重複了 Garfield 自己也反對的錯誤。

The Impact Factor was proposed by Eugene Garfield in Science in 1955 — but he did not design the metric to evaluate research quality. Its original purpose was strictly practical: to help librarians decide which journals to subscribe to. Library budgets were limited; a quantitative indicator was needed to gauge "which journals get cited often and are therefore worth the subscription cost".

Garfield himself emphasized repeatedly in later interviews (2005 BMJ, 2006 JAMA): "IF should never be used to evaluate individual researchers or single papers." That message was ignored for 70 years — promotion committees, hiring committees, and funding agencies all turned IF into a personal-score machine. In 2012 DORA formally called for ending the misuse; the 2015 Leiden Manifesto echoed the call; in 2024-25, institutions worldwide began signing CoARA (Coalition for Advancing Research Assessment).

Core takeaway: the misuse of IF is a 70-year methodological accident in academia. Using IF to gauge journal positioning is reasonable; using it to judge "how good a researcher is" or "how good this paper is" repeats exactly the mistake Garfield himself opposed.

投稿是策略遊戲

許多人投稿失敗不是因為論文不好，而是因為投錯期刊。錯誤的期刊會在 24 小時內 desk-reject (editor 一看就知道不適合)，浪費你 3-6 個月找下一家。

Many submissions fail not because of poor writing but because of poor journal fit. Wrong journal = desk-reject in 24 hours, costing 3-6 months looking for the next.

Correct strategy: lock in 1 first-choice + 2 backups before writing, and follow that journal's author guidelines. Picking a journal after writing means rework.

OA 四模式

二、Open Access 四模式

🥇 Gold OA

作者付 APC ($1500-$11000)，論文立即免費公開。例：PLOS ONE, Nature Communications, BMC, Scientific Reports。NIH/Wellcome funder 偏好。

Author pays APC ($1500-$11000), paper immediately open. E.g., PLOS ONE, Nature Communications, BMC, Scientific Reports. Preferred by NIH/Wellcome.

🟢 Green OA

傳統 paywall 但允許作者把 accepted manuscript 自存到 institutional repository / bioRxiv / PubMed Central（通常 6-12 月 embargo）。

Traditional paywall but author can self-archive the accepted manuscript to institutional repository / bioRxiv / PubMed Central (often 6-12 month embargo).

🟡 Hybrid OA

傳統訂閱期刊 + 「付費可選 OA」($2000-$5000)。被 cOAlition S 與 Plan S 視為「double-dipping」而排斥。

Subscription journal + "optional pay-for-OA" ($2000-$5000). Considered "double-dipping" by cOAlition S / Plan S.

💎 Diamond OA

免費投稿、免費讀。由學會、機構、聯盟資助。例：eLife (post-2023), Wellcome Open Research, F1000Research。對 LMIC 作者特別友善。

Free to publish, free to read. Funded by societies / institutions / consortia. E.g., eLife (post-2023), Wellcome Open Research, F1000Research. Especially friendly to LMIC authors.

掠奪性期刊

三、避開掠奪性期刊

掠奪性期刊 (predatory journals) 收費快、審查空轉、學術記錄無保障。投了不只浪費 APC，還會被嚴肅期刊視為 "predatory record" 而拒收後續投稿。

2026 年驗證工具：Cabells Predatory Reports (付費精準) + Think.Check.Submit. (免費 checklist) + DOAJ (Directory of Open Access Journals 白名單)。

Predatory journals collect fees fast, run sham peer review, and offer no archival guarantees. Submitting wastes APC and creates a "predatory record" that mainstream journals reject in future.

2026 verification: Cabells Predatory Reports (paid, precise), Think.Check.Submit. (free checklist), DOAJ (Directory of Open Access Journals — whitelist).

🚩 主動邀稿、語氣浮誇

"Esteemed Researcher, We are highly impressed by your work..." — 真期刊不會主動寄這種信。

"Esteemed Researcher, We are highly impressed by your work..." — real journals don't cold-email like this.

🚩 7 天就能完成 peer review

正常 peer review 至少 4-12 週。承諾「rapid 7 天」幾乎一定是空轉。

Real peer review takes 4-12 weeks. "Rapid 7 days" is a near-certain sham.

🚩 編輯團隊不透明

🚩 網站不專業

排版亂、圖片低解析、英文文法錯多、模仿大期刊命名 ("Lancet of XXX")。

Sloppy layout, low-res images, broken English, big-journal-mimicking name ("Lancet of XXX").

🚩 不在 PubMed/Scopus/DOAJ

真期刊一定被至少一個主要 indexing service 收錄。OA 期刊未在 DOAJ → 100% 警示。

Real journals appear in at least one major index. OA journal not in DOAJ → 100% red flag.

🚩 假 IF / 自製 metric

"Universal Impact Factor 8.5" — Clarivate 才發 IF。任何不是 JCR 的「IF」都是假的。

"Universal Impact Factor 8.5" — only Clarivate issues IF. Any non-JCR "IF" is fake.
