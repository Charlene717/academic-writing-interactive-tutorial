故事開場

場景：一句話救活 18 個月的數據

SCENE · 2024 · NTUH

從「我想做 TNBC」到「測量什麼？比較誰？多久？」

博士生 Mei 兩年累積了 18 對三陰性乳癌 (TNBC) 治療前後配對組織，準備寫第一篇 paper。她把資料寄給 PI 劉教授，附信標題寫「TNBC 化療反應研究」。回信只有一行：「你的研究問題是什麼？」

Mei 一開始覺得這問題太抽象——「就是研究 TNBC 怎麼對化療反應啊」。但當她真的坐下來寫，發現：「反應」是指什麼？臨床反應（RECIST）？腫瘤縮小百分比？分子層面的基因表達變化？細胞層面的群體比例變化？要比較誰跟誰？反應好的 vs 反應差的？治療前 vs 治療後？每一條路線答案都不一樣，研究設計也不一樣。

第三天她交回一行：「在 III-IV 期 TNBC 病人中 (P)，pembrolizumab + chemo (I) 相對於 chemo only (C)，在 6 個月內的腫瘤微環境細胞態 (O) 有什麼差異？」劉教授回：「這就能寫了。從這句話倒推 introduction、methods、figure 1。」

三個月後，Mei 的 draft 被劉教授改了 11 版才送 reviewer。但 Methods 的核心 cohort 與 outcome 設計沒動過——因為那一句研究問題在她寫第一個字之前就鎖死了。

PhD student Mei has spent two years collecting 18 paired pre/post-chemotherapy biopsies from triple-negative breast cancer (TNBC) patients. Ready to write her first paper, she emails her PI Prof. Liu with the subject "TNBC chemotherapy response study." The reply is one line: "What is your research question?"

Mei thinks it's an abstract question — "I'm studying how TNBC responds to chemo." But sitting down to write, she stalls: what does "respond" mean? Clinical response (RECIST)? Tumor shrinkage percentage? Molecular gene-expression changes? Cell-population shifts? Compared between whom? Good vs poor responders? Pre vs post? Each route leads to a different study design.

Day three she sends back one sentence: "In patients with stage III-IV TNBC (P), how does pembrolizumab + chemo (I) versus chemo alone (C) alter the tumor-microenvironment cell-state composition (O) within 6 months?" Liu replies: "Now you can write. Back-engineer Introduction, Methods, Figure 1 from this sentence."

Three months later the draft is rewritten 11 times before going to reviewers — but the core cohort and outcome definition never changes. They were locked the moment that single sentence was right.

核心詞彙

12 個關鍵術語（雙版本）

每個詞有兩個版本：🧒「給小孩看」用比喻、📐「給研究者看」給定義。

Each term has two versions: 🧒 "for kids" uses a metaphor; 📐 "for researchers" gives the formal definition.

PICO
PICO 框架
把研究寫成一齣戲的卡司表：角色 (P)、做了什麼 (I)、跟誰比 (C)、看到什麼結果 (O)。
A "cast list" for your study: Person, Intervention, Comparator, Outcome — like writing the cast of a play.
Richardson 1995 在 EBM 中提出；P=Population、I=Intervention、C=Comparator、O=Outcome。延伸 PICOS (+Study)、PICOTS (+Time, Setting)。
Richardson 1995 in EBM; P/I/C/O. Extensions: PICOS (+Study design), PICOTS (+Time, Setting).
FINER
FINER 判準
5 個 yes/no 題：做得到嗎？有趣嗎？新嗎？合倫理嗎？有用嗎？全部 yes 才值得做。
Five yes/no checks: doable, interesting, novel, ethical, relevant — all must be yes.
Hulley 等 (Designing Clinical Research, 5e) 提出；Feasible/Interesting/Novel/Ethical/Relevant。任一 No → 換問題。
From Hulley et al. (Designing Clinical Research, 5e); Feasible / Interesting / Novel / Ethical / Relevant. Any No → revise the question.
Aim
研究目標
「整本書要講什麼」——一句很廣的話。
"What the whole book is about" — one broad sentence.
最寬層次的目的陳述；動詞為 investigate / explore / characterize。1 個 study 1 個 aim。
Broadest level of intent; verbs: investigate / explore / characterize. One aim per study.
Objective
研究細目
把「整本書」拆成「3 章」——每章具體可量化。
Splits the "book" into "3 chapters" — each concrete and measurable.
Aim 之下的可達成步驟，通常 2-4 個；動詞用 quantify / compare / identify。
Concrete measurable steps under the Aim; typically 2-4; verbs: quantify / compare / identify.
Hypothesis
假設
「我猜會發生這件事」——而且有可能被證明猜錯。
"I predict X will happen" — and the prediction could be proven wrong.
可被否證 (falsifiable) 的具體預測 (Popper 1934)。方向性 = 預測上升/下降；非方向性 = 預測有差異。對應 H₀ 與 H₁。
A falsifiable specific prediction (Popper 1934). Directional = predicts up/down; non-directional = predicts difference. Paired with H₀ (null) and H₁ (alternative).
Population (P)
研究對象
你要研究「哪些人 / 哪些細胞 / 哪些動物」——越具體越好。
Who you study — patients, cells, animals? More specific = better.
納入 / 排除準則需明確：年齡、性別、疾病分期、共病、種族、地理。Omics 研究中 P = 樣本集 (cell type、tissue、batch)。
Inclusion / exclusion criteria must be explicit: age, sex, disease stage, comorbidities, ethnicity, geography. In omics, P = sample set (cell type, tissue, batch).
Intervention (I)
介入或暴露
你要「做什麼事」給 P——吃藥、加基因、觀察。
What you "do to" P — give a drug, knock out a gene, simply observe.
介入性 (interventional) = 主動操控；觀察性 (observational) = 被動 exposure。需列劑量、時程、給藥途徑或暴露定義。
Interventional = active manipulation; observational = passive exposure. Specify dose, schedule, route, or exposure definition.
Comparator (C)
對照組
「跟誰比？」沒對照組就像考試沒及格線——分數無意義。
"Compared to whom?" Without a comparator, scores are meaningless — like an exam with no pass line.
Placebo、active control (現行標準治療)、wild-type、healthy control。N-of-1 設計可用個案前後當對照。
Placebo, active control (current standard), wild-type, healthy control. N-of-1 designs use within-patient pre/post as comparator.
Outcome (O)
結果指標
「你怎麼知道有沒有效？」事先決定要測量什麼。
"How will you know it worked?" Pre-specify what you'll measure.
Primary outcome 必須事先註冊 (PROSPERO / ClinicalTrials.gov)；secondary 列出但不可變主結局。生資為 quantifiable readout (#DEGs, AUC)。
Primary outcome must be pre-registered (PROSPERO / ClinicalTrials.gov); secondary listed but cannot become primary. In bioinformatics: quantifiable readout (#DEGs, AUC).
HARKing
事後假設
先看結果再「假裝」自己一開始就猜中——學術界的最大禁忌之一。
Looking at results then pretending you predicted them all along — one of academia's biggest taboos.
Hypothesizing After the Results are Known (Kerr 1998 Pers Soc Psychol Rev)。導致 false positive 暴增；preregistration 是解方。
Hypothesizing After the Results are Known (Kerr 1998). Inflates false positives; pre-registration is the antidote.
Pre-registration
研究預先註冊
在做實驗之前就把「我要做什麼、預測什麼」公開貼出來——之後不能改。
Publicly post "what I'll do and predict" BEFORE the experiment — you can't change it later.
PROSPERO (systematic review)、OSF Registry、Clinical Trials.gov、AsPredicted；對應 Registered Report (期刊先審計畫再做)。
PROSPERO (systematic reviews), OSF Registry, ClinicalTrials.gov, AsPredicted. Related: Registered Reports (journal reviews plan first).
Falsifiability
可否證性
一個好猜想必須「有可能猜錯」——不能保證自己一定對。
A good guess must be capable of being wrong — it can't be guaranteed to be right.
Popper 1934 科學陳述的劃界判準。「Gene X 跟病有關」不可否證；「Gene X 高表達者 5 年存活高 ≥10%」可否證。
Popper 1934: demarcation criterion for science. "Gene X is related to disease" is unfalsifiable; "5-yr survival ≥10% higher in Gene X-high vs -low" is falsifiable.

一個比喻
🛰️
比喻 · GPS

好的研究問題就像 GPS：給你 P / I / C / O 四個座標，否則整篇論文會在野外迷路。少一個座標，定位就會偏；少兩個，連自己在哪都不知道。FINER 是把座標經緯度換算成「值不值得走」——可行嗎？新嗎？合倫理嗎？相關嗎？重要嗎？沒有 GPS 的旅行叫探險，沒有 GPS 的論文叫流水帳。

A strong research question is like a GPS: it gives you four coordinates — P / I / C / O — without which the whole paper wanders off-trail. Drop one coordinate and the bearing drifts; drop two and you no longer know where you are. FINER converts those coordinates into "is this trip worth taking?" — Feasible? Interesting? Novel? Ethical? Relevant? Travel without GPS is called adventure; a paper without it is called rambling.

Σ 推導

從 idea 到 PICO 鎖死研究問題的 8 步

Σ PICO Lock-In Pipeline
Σ₁

找出大方向 (broad area)。從你最近讀過、最想吵的 3–5 篇論文出發；不要一開始就 narrow，先承認自己對哪個 disease/system/method 真的有熱情。

Identify the broad area. Start from the 3–5 papers you most want to argue with; don't narrow yet — first admit which disease/system/method you genuinely care about.

Σ₂

列出該領域的 pain points。用 bullet 寫 5–10 個「為什麼這件事還沒解決？」沒寫滿 5 條表示讀得還不夠。

List the field's pain points. Bullet 5–10 "why hasn't this been solved?" items. Fewer than 5 means you haven't read enough.

Σ₃

挑 P (Population)。具體到「分期 / 細胞型別 / 物種 / 年齡層」。「癌症病人」不算 P；「stage III–IV TNBC, female adults」才算。

Pick P (Population). Specify "stage / cell type / species / age group". "Cancer patients" is not a P; "stage III–IV TNBC, female adults" is.

Σ₄

挑 I (Intervention / Exposure)。劑量、頻次、處理時長都要寫上；biology 研究則寫擾動類型 (KO / KD / OE / drug dose)。

Pick I (Intervention/Exposure). Include dose, frequency, duration; for biology, specify perturbation type (KO / KD / OE / drug dose).

Σ₅

挑 C (Comparator)。沒有 comparator 的研究不是研究是個案報告。最低標準：placebo / wild-type / baseline 至少要有一個。

Pick C (Comparator). A study without a comparator is a case report, not research. Minimum bar: placebo / wild-type / baseline — at least one.

Σ₆

挑 O (可量化 Outcome)。「改善」不算；「24-month PFS」「log2FC ≥1 的基因數」「scRNA-seq UMAP cluster shift」才算。可量化 = 能寫出單位。

Pick O (quantifiable outcome). "Improvement" doesn't count; "24-month PFS", "#genes with log2FC ≥1", "UMAP cluster shift" do. Quantifiable = a unit you can write down.

Σ₇

對 FINER 五項過篩。Feasible / Interesting / Novel / Ethical / Relevant 一項打勾不過就回 Σ₃ 重來。最常掛掉的是 Feasible（樣本拿不到）與 Novel（早被做過）。

Screen against the 5 FINER criteria. Feasible / Interesting / Novel / Ethical / Relevant — fail any and return to Σ₃. Most common failures: Feasible (samples unobtainable) and Novel (already done).

Σ₈

30 秒同事測試。對 3 位同領域同事念出你的問題；30 秒內若無法複述或追問具體實驗，回 Σ₁ 重寫。通過 = 研究問題可以鎖死。

30-second colleague test. Read the question aloud to 3 same-field colleagues; if none can paraphrase or pose a sharp follow-up within 30 s, return to Σ₁. Pass = PICO is locked.

思辨

三個值得吵的問題

爭點 1

Novel = 從未做過 vs 重大延伸?

純粹論者：Novel 必須是「世界上第一次」；replication 只是 confirmatory，不該被當成 novelty。
Purist: Novel must be "world-first"; replication is confirmatory and shouldn't count as novelty.
實用派：「相同問題在不同人群 / 不同條件下被重做」也算 novel，特別是在再現危機 (replication crisis) 之後。
Pragmatist: Re-doing the same question in a new population / condition counts as novel — especially post-replication-crisis.
編輯視角：Top journal 看「能改變教科書嗎？」；領域期刊看「能改變該領域共識嗎？」。Novelty 永遠是相對 audience 的。
Editor view: Top journals ask "does this change a textbook?"; specialty journals ask "does this shift field consensus?" Novelty is always relative to the audience.
爭點 2

Hypothesis-free 研究是否值得?

Popper 派：不可否證 ≠ 科學；hypothesis-free 只是 data fishing 的化名。
Popperians: Non-falsifiable ≠ science; "hypothesis-free" is data-fishing in disguise.
Atlas 派：沒有 Human Cell Atlas、ENCODE、UK Biobank 這些 hypothesis-generating 資源，下一代假說根本生不出來。
Atlas builders: Without HCA, ENCODE, UK Biobank — hypothesis-generating infrastructures — the next generation of hypotheses can't even be formulated.
折衷：Hypothesis-free 適合 discovery phase，但 validation phase 必須回到 pre-registered hypothesis。
Middle ground: Hypothesis-free suits the discovery phase, but validation must return to a pre-registered hypothesis.
爭點 3

Stat-significance vs 臨床/生物意義

統計嚴格派：樣本夠大時任何噪音都會顯著；effect size + CI 才是真結論。
Statistical hardliners: With large n, anything becomes significant; effect size + CI is the actual conclusion.
臨床派：需要 MCID (minimum clinically important difference)；統計顯著但低於 MCID = 對病人無意義。
Clinicians: Need MCID (minimum clinically important difference); significant but below MCID = meaningless to patients.
ASA 2016 立場：p-value 不該被當成 thresholding tool；報告 effect size、CI、precision 是 minimum standard。
ASA 2016 stance: p-values shouldn't be used as thresholds; reporting effect size, CI, and precision is the minimum standard.
個案聚焦

一個典範、一個警訊

🏆
典範 · 1948 Streptomycin RCT

Bradford Hill 在 MRC 主導的 1948 鏈黴素治療肺結核試驗是史上第一個現代 RCT，也是 proto-PICO 的具體實踐：P=15–25 歲新確診結核病人；I=streptomycin + 臥床；C=只臥床；O=6 個月放射學改善。 Hill 用 random number table 做 allocation，並 blind 評估放射片——這些方法後來成為 CONSORT 的雛形。

Bradford Hill's 1948 MRC streptomycin trial for tuberculosis is the first modern RCT and a tangible proto-PICO: P=newly diagnosed TB patients aged 15–25; I=streptomycin + bed-rest; C=bed-rest only; O=6-month radiological improvement. Hill used random-number-table allocation and blinded radiograph review — methods that later became the seed of CONSORT.

⚠️
警訊 · WHI 2002

2002 Women's Health Initiative (WHI) hormone-replacement therapy 試驗推翻了 1990 年代以來「HRT 可預防心血管疾病」的廣泛觀察性結論。一個 N=16,608 的 pre-registered RCT，在 effect size 不大、但 PICO 鎖死、outcome 預先定義的設計下，逆轉了整代臨床建議。教訓：含糊的觀察性問題（"HRT 對女性健康有什麼好處？"）無法守住推論；鎖死 PICO 的 RCT 才是金標準。

The 2002 Women's Health Initiative (WHI) HRT trial overturned a generation of observational claims that HRT prevented cardiovascular disease. An N=16,608 pre-registered RCT — modest effect sizes but locked PICO and pre-specified outcomes — reversed a generation of clinical guidance. Lesson: vague observational questions ("what's good about HRT?") can't sustain inference; only PICO-locked RCTs are the gold standard.

常見陷阱

六個會毀掉研究問題的錯誤

① 含糊的「我想研究 X」

「我想研究 TNBC」「我想了解免疫」——這些是主題不是問題。寫不出 PICO 表示還沒進入研究階段。

"I want to study TNBC" / "I want to understand immunity" — these are topics, not questions. If you can't write a PICO, you haven't entered research yet.

② 沒有 comparator

「Drug X 在 stage IV 病人中的反應」——對照在哪？沒 C 的研究只能寫 case series，不能寫 hypothesis-driven 論文。

"Drug X response in stage IV patients" — where's the control? Without C, you can only write a case series, not a hypothesis-driven paper.

③ 無法測量的 outcome

「病人狀況改善」「腫瘤微環境改變」——這些不可量化。Outcome 必須有單位（PFS 月數、log2FC、AUC）。

"Patient improvement" / "TME alteration" — not quantifiable. Outcomes need a unit (PFS months, log2FC, AUC).

④ Sample 拿不到 (FINER-F 失敗)

「N=2000 罕病患者 longitudinal scRNA-seq」聽起來很棒；但你的 IRB / 經費 / 時程拿得到嗎？拿不到 = 不可行。

"N=2000 rare-disease longitudinal scRNA-seq" sounds great — but can your IRB / budget / timeline deliver? If no, it's infeasible.

⑤ HARKing (事後假說)

看完 data 才寫 hypothesis，再說「我預測這個結果」——這違反 Popper 與 ICMJE。用 pre-registration 鎖死才公允。

Writing the hypothesis after seeing the data and claiming "I predicted this" violates both Popper and ICMJE. Pre-register to lock it in.

⑥ 答案明顯「是」的瑣碎問題

「吸菸是否與肺癌有關？」——已被證實 60 年；做這題等於浪費 IRB。FINER-I (interesting) + Novel 是抵擋瑣碎的兩道防線。

"Is smoking associated with lung cancer?" — settled 60 years ago. Pursuing it wastes IRB time. FINER-I (interesting) + Novel are your two guardrails.

壞例 vs 好例

同一個構想，兩種寫法

❌ 壞範例

「我想研究 TNBC。」

"I want to research TNBC."

  • 沒有 P（哪一期？哪一群病人？）
  • No P (which stage? which patients?)
  • 沒有 I（要做什麼？藥物？基因？）
  • No I (what intervention? drug? gene?)
  • 沒有 C（與誰比較？）
  • No C (compared to what?)
  • 沒有可量化 O（測量什麼？）
  • No quantifiable O (measure what?)
  • 沒有時間範圍（什麼時候測？）
  • No time frame (when measured?)

✅ 好範例

「在 stage III–IV 的 TNBC 病人 (P)，pembrolizumab + chemo (I) 相較於 chemo only (C)，在 6 個月內如何改變 tumor microenvironment 的 cell-state composition (O)？」

"In stage III–IV TNBC patients (P), how does pembrolizumab + chemo (I) vs chemo only (C) alter tumor microenvironment cell-state composition (O) over 6 months?"

  • P 明確：stage III–IV TNBC
  • P explicit: stage III–IV TNBC
  • I 明確：pembro + chemo
  • I explicit: pembro + chemo
  • C 明確：chemo only
  • C explicit: chemo only
  • O 可量化：scRNA-seq cell-state proportion
  • O quantifiable: scRNA-seq cell-state proportion
  • 時間範圍：6 個月
  • Time frame: 6 months

六道反思題

Q1. 你能用一個句子把研究問題寫完嗎？寫不完表示還沒收斂。

Q2. Is each PICO element specific (with unit / cohort / dose)? If any is vague, rewrite.
Q3. FINER 五項（Feasible / Interesting / Novel / Ethical / Relevant）能否全部打勾？
Q3. Do all five FINER criteria (Feasible / Interesting / Novel / Ethical / Relevant) check yes?
Q4. 30 秒同事測試是否通過？3 位同事能否複述你的問題？
Q4. Does the 30-second colleague test pass? Can 3 peers paraphrase the question?
Q5. 是否有 HARKing 疑慮？hypothesis 是看 data 之前還是之後寫下來的？
Q5. Any HARKing risk? Was the hypothesis written before or after seeing the data?
Q6. Sample 真的拿得到嗎？IRB、經費、時程、cohort access 全部就位嗎？
Q6. Is the sample actually accessible? Are IRB, funding, timeline, and cohort access all in place?

前沿議題（可摺疊）

SOTA
(a) AI 輔助 PICO 拆解 — Claude / GPT-5 工作流

2024–2026 大型語言模型（Claude Sonnet 4.6 / GPT-5 / Gemini 3）已能可靠地從一段 free-text idea 拆出候選 P / I / C / O，並用 prompting 對 FINER 自動評分。實務 workflow：

  1. 給 LLM 你的 200-word free text + 3 篇 seminal paper abstract。
  2. 要求輸出「3 組候選 PICO」+「每組對應的 FINER 5×3 評分矩陣」。
  3. 讓 LLM 為每組 PICO 寫 3 句版「30-sec colleague pitch」。
  4. 把 3 組都拿給真人同事看 — LLM 解決發散問題，人類解決收斂判斷。

⚠️ 警告：LLM 會「順著 prompt 寫出像樣的 PICO」即使主題不可行；falsifiability 與 ethics 判斷仍須人類。建議搭配 2025 Cochrane LLM-evidence-synthesis guidance。

By 2024–2026, large language models (Claude Sonnet 4.6 / GPT-5 / Gemini 3) reliably decompose free-text ideas into candidate P / I / C / O and auto-score FINER. Practical workflow:

  1. Feed the LLM your 200-word free text + 3 seminal abstracts.
  2. Ask for "3 candidate PICOs" + "a 5×3 FINER scoring matrix per PICO".
  3. Ask the LLM to draft a 3-sentence "30-sec colleague pitch" per PICO.
  4. Show all three to human colleagues — LLMs handle divergence, humans handle convergent judgment.

⚠️ Caveat: LLMs will produce "plausible-looking" PICOs even for infeasible topics; falsifiability and ethics judgments remain human. Pair with 2025 Cochrane LLM-evidence-synthesis guidance.

(b) AsPredicted + Registered Reports 增長 2024–26

Pre-registration 工具 AsPredicted.org（9 題簡版）與 OSF Registries（完整版）已成為心理學、行為經濟、神經科學的事實標準；2024 年起 Nature Human Behaviour、eLife、PNAS Nexus 大量採用 Registered Reports（先審 protocol 再做實驗）。Stage 1 通過 = in-principle acceptance，無論結果正負都會刊登。對研究問題的影響：HARKing 幾乎不可能，但要求 PICO 必須在資料蒐集前就鎖死且可公開驗證。

Pre-registration platforms — AsPredicted.org (9-question lite) and OSF Registries (full) — are now de-facto standard in psychology, behavioral econ, neuroscience. Since 2024 Nature Human Behaviour, eLife, PNAS Nexus have heavily adopted Registered Reports (protocol-first peer review). Stage 1 acceptance = in-principle publication regardless of result direction. Implication for research questions: HARKing becomes nearly impossible, but PICO must be locked and publicly verifiable before data collection.

(c) Open Hypothesis Initiative

2025 年由多家 funders（Wellcome、HHMI、ZonMw）發起的 Open Hypothesis Initiative，鼓勵在 grant application 階段就 publicly post 候選 hypothesis，並允許他人 fork / refine。觀念類比於 GitHub 對 open source 的影響——hypothesis 不再是 PI 私有財產而是 community asset。對 PICO 的影響：早期 community feedback 可以在 Σ₈ 之前就提供 30-sec colleague test 的放大版（30-day community test）。

The 2025 Open Hypothesis Initiative — backed by funders (Wellcome, HHMI, ZonMw) — encourages publicly posting candidate hypotheses at the grant-application stage and lets others fork / refine them. The analogy is GitHub-to-open-source: a hypothesis is no longer a PI's private asset but a community resource. Implication for PICO: early community feedback effectively becomes a scaled-up "30-day colleague test" before Σ₈.

概覽

為什麼一切從「好問題」開始？

論文寫不好，多數情況不是寫作技巧的問題，而是研究問題本身就含糊。一個含糊的問題會讓緒論說不出 gap、方法選不對工具、結果不知道該回答什麼、討論變成感想文。

好的研究問題具備三個特徵：可被檢驗 (testable)、有意義 (significant)、可行 (feasible)。三者缺一，論文都會出問題。

Most weak papers don't fail because of writing — they fail because the research question itself was vague. A vague question means the introduction has no clear gap, the methods don't fit, the results have nothing specific to answer, and the discussion becomes commentary.

A good research question is testable, significant, and feasible. Lacking any one of these breaks the paper.

💡
核心原則：能在一個句子裡寫完、且能用「是 / 否 / 多少」回答的研究問題，才是寫得出論文的問題。寫不出來，多半是還沒想清楚。 Core principle: A research question you can write in one sentence and answer with "yes / no / how much" is one you can actually write a paper about. If you can't write it cleanly, you haven't thought it through.
PICO 框架

一、PICO：把問題結構化

PICO 是循證醫學裡用來把問題結構化的工具，但它對任何「比較」型的生醫研究都適用。也常擴充為 PICOS（加 Study design）或 PICOTS（加 Time、Setting）。

PICO originated in evidence-based medicine for structuring questions, but it works for any comparative biomedical study. It often extends to PICOS (+ Study design) or PICOTS (+ Time, Setting).

👥

P — Population / Problem

研究對象是誰？年齡、性別、診斷、細胞型別、物種…越具體越好。
❌「癌症病人」
✅「III–IV 期非小細胞肺癌、未接受過免疫治療的成人病人」

Who are your subjects? Age, sex, diagnosis, cell type, species — the more specific, the better.
❌ "cancer patients"
✅ "treatment-naïve adults with stage III–IV non-small-cell lung cancer"

💊

I — Intervention / Exposure

你「介入」或「觀察」的因子是什麼？藥物、治療、暴露、基因敲除、scRNA-seq 處理流程…
❌「免疫治療」
✅「Pembrolizumab 200 mg 每 3 週一次，共 4 週期」

The factor you intervene with or observe — drug, treatment, exposure, gene knockout, scRNA-seq pipeline.
❌ "immunotherapy"
✅ "Pembrolizumab 200 mg every 3 weeks for 4 cycles"

⚖️

C — Comparator

你拿來比較的對照組是什麼？安慰劑、標準療法、wild-type、健康對照…
❌「沒有比較」
✅「化療 (carboplatin + paclitaxel) 標準療法」

What you compare against — placebo, standard care, wild-type, healthy controls.
❌ "no comparison"
✅ "standard chemotherapy (carboplatin + paclitaxel)"

🎯

O — Outcome

你想測量什麼結果？必須事先定義並可量化。
❌「病人狀況改善」
✅「24 個月 progression-free survival (PFS) 比例」

What outcome you measure — must be pre-specified and quantifiable.
❌ "patient improvement"
✅ "24-month progression-free survival (PFS) rate"

⚠️
生資/Omics 研究的 PICO 變體：P=細胞群/樣本群、I=處理或基因擾動、C=對照樣本或 baseline、O=可量化讀出 (差異基因數、UMAP 距離、AUC)。例：「在 PBMC 中，IFN-γ 刺激相對於未刺激對照，會引起多少 ISG (interferon-stimulated genes) 的差異表達？」
PICO for omics studies: P = cell/sample population, I = perturbation, C = control/baseline, O = quantifiable readout (#DEGs, UMAP distance, AUC). E.g.: "In PBMCs, how many ISGs are differentially expressed under IFN-γ stimulation vs unstimulated control?"
FINER 評估

二、FINER：你的問題值得做嗎？

PICO 幫你把問題寫清楚；FINER 幫你判斷該不該做這個問題。Hulley 等人在《Designing Clinical Research》中提出此 5 項判準。

PICO clarifies the question; FINER tells you whether it's worth doing. Hulley et al. (Designing Clinical Research) proposed these 5 criteria.

FINER自問紅燈
Feasible 樣本量、預算、時間、技術都到位嗎？Sample size, budget, time, expertise — all available? 需要 1000 例稀有疾病樣本，但你只有 50 例Needs 1000 rare-disease samples; you have 50
Interesting 研究者、領域、社會關心嗎？Do you, the field, and society care? 「我有空所以做這個」"I'm doing this because I have free time"
Novel 能在既有文獻上加些什麼？確認、推翻、延伸？Does it add to existing literature — confirm, refute, extend? 主題已有 3 篇 Cell 論文、結論一致Topic has 3 Cell papers with the same conclusion already
Ethical 能通過 IRB / IACUC？符合 Helsinki / Belmont？Will it pass IRB / IACUC? Follow Helsinki / Belmont? 無法獲得受試者知情同意Cannot obtain informed consent
Relevant 能影響臨床實踐、政策、後續研究？Will it affect clinical practice, policy, or follow-up research? 「結果不會改變任何決策」"The result wouldn't change any decision"
關鍵區辨

三、Aim / Objective / Hypothesis

三者經常被混用，導致緒論最後一段交代不清。記住層級：Aim（最廣）→ Objective（具體可達成步驟）→ Hypothesis（可被否證的預測）。

These three are often muddled, leaving the last paragraph of the introduction unclear. Hierarchy: Aim (broadest) → Objective (concrete steps) → Hypothesis (falsifiable prediction).

Aim (目標)Objective (細目)Hypothesis (假設)
性質 寬泛意圖Broad intent 具體可量化步驟Concrete measurable steps 可被否證的預測Falsifiable prediction
動詞 investigate / explore / understand quantify / compare / identify will / increases / decreases
例 了解 IFN-γ 對 PBMC 的免疫調控Understand IFN-γ immune regulation in PBMCs (1) 量化 ISG 表達；(2) 鑑定上調超過 2× 的轉錄因子(1) Quantify ISG expression; (2) identify TFs upregulated >2× IFN-γ 處理會使 ISG 表達在 24 小時內顯著上升 (≥4×, p<0.05)IFN-γ will significantly upregulate ISG expression within 24 h (≥4×, p<0.05)
💡
探索性研究怎麼辦？不是每篇論文都要有 hypothesis。Discovery / hypothesis-generating 研究可以只列 aim 與 objectives，但要明確說「This is a hypothesis-generating study」，不要假裝你有先驗假設然後再 cherry-pick 結果。 What about exploratory work? Not every paper needs a hypothesis. Discovery / hypothesis-generating studies can list only aim and objectives — just declare "This is a hypothesis-generating study" upfront. Don't pretend you had a prior hypothesis then cherry-pick.
範例對照

四、好壞研究問題對照

❌ 模糊版

「我想研究腸道菌與肥胖的關係。」

"I want to study the relationship between gut microbiota and obesity."

✅ 可檢驗版

「在 18–45 歲的台灣成人中 (P)，Akkermansia muciniphila 相對豐度 (I) 與身體質量指數 (BMI) (O) 是否呈負相關？並比較體重正常 (BMI 18.5–24) 與肥胖 (BMI ≥27) 兩組 (C)。」

"In Taiwanese adults aged 18–45 (P), is relative Akkermansia muciniphila abundance (I) negatively correlated with body mass index (O), comparing normal-weight (BMI 18.5–24) and obese (BMI ≥27) groups (C)?"

❌ 無法檢驗

「Single-cell 技術在癌症研究中很重要。」
(這是觀點，不是問題。沒有變數、沒有對照、無法用數據回答。)

"Single-cell technology is important in cancer research."
(This is an opinion, not a question. No variables, no comparison, no data can answer it.)

✅ 可量化

「相較於 bulk RNA-seq，scRNA-seq (10x v3) 是否能在三陰性乳癌樣本中多偵測出至少 30% 的稀有 (<1%) 細胞亞群？」

"Does scRNA-seq (10x v3) detect at least 30% more rare (<1%) cell subpopulations than bulk RNA-seq in triple-negative breast cancer samples?"

決策引導

五、研究問題定型決策樹

🌳 一週內鎖定研究問題的流程

Q1:
能寫成一句話的可量化問題嗎？→ 否 → 回去做 PICO，先把 P/I/C/O 一個個寫具體。
Q2:
過去 5 年內有相同問題的論文嗎？→ 是 → 找他們的 limitation 段落、或在不同 population / setting / outcome 重做（仍可投較低 IF 期刊或重複性研究）。
Q3:
樣本/數據可拿到嗎？→ 否 → 改做 secondary analysis（GEO、TCGA、UK Biobank、GTEx）或 systematic review / meta-analysis。
Q4:
結果有任何「臨床/生物學/方法學」實質意義嗎？→ 否 → 重新換問題。
Q5:
找 3 位同領域同事可以 30 秒內聽懂嗎？→ 否 → 表達還沒成熟，繼續打磨。
Q1:
Can you write it as one quantifiable sentence? → No → Go back to PICO; specify each P/I/C/O.
Q2:
Has the same question been answered in the last 5 years? → Yes → Mine their limitations section, or replicate in a different population / setting / outcome (still publishable in mid-IF journals or replication studies).
Q3:
Can you get the samples/data? → No → Switch to secondary analysis (GEO, TCGA, UK Biobank, GTEx) or systematic review / meta-analysis.
Q4:
Will the result have any real clinical / biological / methodological meaning? → No → Change the question.
Q5:
Can 3 colleagues understand it in 30 seconds? → No → Keep refining.
範本

六、可直接套用的問題與假設範本

# 比較型 / Comparative
In [POPULATION], does [INTERVENTION] compared with [COMPARATOR]
result in [OUTCOME] over [TIMEFRAME]?

# 關聯型 / Associative
Among [POPULATION], is [EXPOSURE] associated with
[OUTCOME], after adjusting for [CONFOUNDERS]?

# 預測型 / Prognostic
In [POPULATION], can [BIOMARKER / MODEL] predict
[OUTCOME] with AUC > 0.75 in an external validation cohort?

# 方法學 / Methodological
Compared with [CURRENT METHOD], does [NEW METHOD]
improve [METRIC] by at least [THRESHOLD] on [BENCHMARK]?
# 方向性假設 / Directional
H1: [Variable A] will increase / decrease [Variable B]
    by at least [effect size] in [population].

# 非方向性假設 / Non-directional
H1: There is a significant difference in [outcome]
    between [group 1] and [group 2].

# 虛無假設 / Null
H0: There is no difference in [outcome]
    between [group 1] and [group 2].
# Aim (one sentence, broad)
The aim of this study is to [verb: investigate / characterize / compare]
[topic] in [population/system].

# Objectives (numbered, measurable)
Objective 1: To [verb: quantify / identify / compare] [X]
             using [method].
Objective 2: To [verb] [Y] using [method].
Objective 3: To [verb] [Z] using [method].
⛔
千萬別寫的句型：「The purpose of this study is to study…」「This thesis aims to discuss…」——動詞太弱、沒有方向。改用 quantify、compare、test、identify、characterize、predict 等具體動詞。 Avoid these: "The purpose of this study is to study…" "This thesis aims to discuss…" — verbs are weak and directionless. Use quantify, compare, test, identify, characterize, predict instead.
專家辯論

哪些問題才算 novel？

DISCUSSION · DEBATE

爭點：novelty 應該怎麼定義？

Reviewer 常寫「nothing novel」就 reject。但 novelty 的標準從來不一致——不同期刊、不同領域、甚至不同 reviewer 都有不同定義。以下是 4 種代表性觀點。

Reviewers love writing "nothing novel" to justify rejection. But the novelty bar is inconsistent — varies by journal, field, even reviewer. Four representative views:

① 主編觀點 (NEJM, Cell)：必須「改變既有理解」——能讓教科書改一頁。incremental confirmation 不足。
① Editor view (NEJM, Cell): must "change existing understanding" — enough to rewrite a textbook page. Incremental confirmation doesn't cut it.
② Bayesian 觀點：novelty = posterior 與 prior 的距離。複製研究在「reduces uncertainty」也是 novel——複製成功 = 你的 posterior 變窄。
② Bayesian view: novelty = posterior–prior distance. Replications reduce uncertainty, which IS novel — your posterior narrows.
③ Open Science 觀點 (Munafò 2017 Nat Hum Behav)：過度追求 novelty 是 reproducibility crisis 的元兇之一。應該獎勵 replication 與 negative results。
③ Open Science view (Munafò 2017 Nat Hum Behav): over-prizing novelty drives the reproducibility crisis. Reward replication and negative results.
④ 實用主義 (PI、advisor)：能讓你畢業、能拿到 fund、能讓 lab 站穩——就是足夠 novel。
④ Pragmatist (PIs / advisors): if it gets you a degree, a grant, or moves the lab forward — it's novel enough.
DISCUSSION · CASE

案例：3 篇相同問題、3 種命運

2024 年三組獨立團隊同樣回答「PD-L1 高表達 TNBC 病人 pembrolizumab 是否能延長 PFS？」結果：

In 2024, three independent groups asked the same question: "Does pembrolizumab extend PFS in PD-L1-high TNBC?" Outcomes:

A 組 (NEJM)：n=850 多中心 RCT，找到顯著 HR 0.62 → 上 NEJM。
Group A (NEJM): n=850 multicenter RCT, significant HR 0.62 → NEJM.
B 組 (Cancer Res)：n=120 單中心 retrospective cohort，趨勢 HR 0.78 但 95% CI 跨 1 → mid-IF specialty journal。
Group B (Cancer Res): n=120 single-center retrospective, HR 0.78 but 95% CI crosses 1 → mid-IF specialty journal.
C 組：同樣 n=120 retrospective cohort 但結果 null → desk-rejected x 6，最終發在 Sci Rep 因為「沒有 novel finding」。
Group C: same n=120 retrospective, null result → desk-rejected ×6, eventually in Sci Rep — "no novel finding."
結論：相同問題、不同 sample size + study design → 完全不同命運。Novel ≠ 第一個做；novel = 在當時的 evidence base 中能改變 decision。
Lesson: same question, different sample size + design → very different fates. Novel ≠ first to ask; novel = changes a decision given the current evidence base.
陷阱

6 個典型陷阱

多重問題擠一句

❌「我想了解 IFN-γ 對 PBMC、肝細胞、神經元的影響」 → 三個問題用同一份 cohort 不可行。修法：挑 1 個主問題，其他列為 secondary objectives。

❌ "I want to study IFN-γ on PBMCs, hepatocytes, and neurons" — 3 questions, 1 cohort, impossible. Fix: pick one primary; list others as secondary objectives.

動詞太弱

❌「study / discuss / investigate」太空泛。修法：改用 quantify / compare / characterize / predict / classify 等具體動詞。

❌ "study / discuss / investigate" — too vague. Fix: use quantify / compare / characterize / predict / classify.

缺對照組

❌「描述 TNBC 細胞態」沒有 C → 不知道你的發現是 TNBC 特有還是所有 cancer 共通。修法：至少加 healthy control 或 historical cohort。

❌ "Describe TNBC cell states" — no C → can't tell if findings are TNBC-specific or cancer-general. Fix: add healthy controls or historical cohort.

Outcome 沒事先定義

❌做完才決定 primary outcome → HARKing → reviewer 一眼識破，且違反 ICMJE。修法：送 IRB 同時就 pre-register primary outcome。

❌ Deciding primary outcome after seeing data → HARKing → caught instantly, violates ICMJE. Fix: pre-register primary outcome alongside IRB submission.

樣本對不上問題

❌「問題」需要 n=500 但你只有 n=20 → 從 FINER 的「Feasible」直接失敗。修法：改 PICO 縮小 P；或改成 secondary analysis 公共資料庫 (TCGA, UK Biobank)。

❌ Question demands n=500 but you have 20 → fails FINER's Feasible. Fix: narrow P; or pivot to secondary analysis of public data (TCGA, UK Biobank).

HARKing：事後改 hypothesis

❌ 跑完分析發現 Gene Y 顯著、就改寫 introduction 假裝早預測 → reviewer 比對 pre-registration 立刻發現。修法：誠實標記為 exploratory finding。

❌ After analysis, Gene Y is significant; rewrite Intro as if you predicted it → reviewer cross-checks pre-registration and catches you. Fix: honestly label as exploratory.

反思題

  1. 把你目前最關心的研究主題寫成 1 句 PICO 格式的問題。哪一個元素 (P/I/C/O) 最難明確？為什麼？
  2. Write your current research interest as a one-sentence PICO question. Which element (P/I/C/O) is hardest to nail down? Why?
  3. 你的研究在 FINER 5 條中，最弱的是哪一條？是「不可行」「沒人感興趣」「不夠 novel」「倫理問題」還是「不夠 relevant」？該如何補強？
  4. Of FINER's 5 criteria, which is weakest for your study? Is it infeasibility, low interest, lack of novelty, ethics, or low relevance? How will you address it?
  5. 想像你 3 個月後做出與假設「相反」的結果——你的研究問題還站得住嗎？如果不行，那原本問題可能只是「希望成真」而非真正的科學問題。
  6. Imagine 3 months later you get the opposite result of your hypothesis — does your research question still stand? If not, your original "question" was wishful thinking, not science.
⭐ ADVANCED · 2024-2026 SOTA

AI 輔助研究問題生成 (2024-2026)

2025 起，Anthropic / OpenAI / DeepMind 多項研究顯示 LLM 已能將模糊 idea 轉成 PICO 格式 (e.g., Lievin et al. 2024 NEJM AI doi:10.1056/AIoa2300010)。但有 3 個業界共識：

  • (1) Hypothesis falsifiability 仍須人類判斷。LLM 容易產生「看似 hypothesis 但其實 unfalsifiable」的陳述 (e.g., "Gene X plays a role in cancer")。
  • (1) Falsifiability still needs human judgment. LLMs often produce "hypothesis-shaped but unfalsifiable" statements (e.g., "Gene X plays a role in cancer").
  • (2) Pre-registration 不能交給 LLM。它會在你迭代問題時悄悄調整，造成 unintended HARKing。OSF 與 PROSPERO 2025 後明確禁止「LLM-generated registration without human review」。
  • (2) Pre-registration can't be outsourced to LLMs. They quietly modify your question across iterations, causing unintended HARKing. OSF and PROSPERO (2025+) explicitly forbid "LLM-generated registration without human review."
  • (3) Multi-agent debate (Liu et al. 2025 Nat Mach Intell) 顯示，讓 2-3 個 LLM 從不同 POV 辯論你的研究問題，比單一 LLM 更能揪出 novelty 缺陷與 ethics 盲點。Anthropic Constitutional Critic 框架是常見實作。
  • (3) Multi-agent debate (Liu et al. 2025 Nat Mach Intell) shows 2-3 LLMs debating your question from different POVs uncover novelty gaps and ethics blind spots better than a single LLM. Anthropic's Constitutional Critic is a common implementation.

歷史軼事 · James Lind 與第一個原型 PICO (1747)。蘇格蘭海軍外科醫師 James Lind 在 HMS Salisbury 上面對船員大量罹患壞血病的災難，做了史上第一個對照試驗：他選了 12 名症狀「相似」的水手 (P)，分成 6 組各 2 人，給予 6 種不同處方 (I 與 C：蘋果酒、稀硫酸、醋、海水、藥膏、柑橘類)，在 6 天內觀察症狀變化 (O)。柑橘組兩人 6 天就回到崗位。Lind 1753 出版《A Treatise of the Scurvy》，但英國皇家海軍直到 1795 年才正式採行——47 年的延遲。教訓：好的研究問題如果說服不了 institution，再強的證據也會躺在書裡 47 年。

Historical anecdote · James Lind and the first proto-PICO (1747). Scottish naval surgeon James Lind, facing a scurvy catastrophe aboard HMS Salisbury, ran the first controlled trial in history: he chose 12 sailors with "similar" symptoms (P), split them into 6 pairs, gave 6 different remedies (I and C: cider, dilute sulphuric acid, vinegar, seawater, an electuary paste, and citrus fruits), and observed symptoms over 6 days (O). The citrus pair returned to duty in 6 days. Lind published A Treatise of the Scurvy in 1753 — but the Royal Navy didn't formally adopt the protocol until 1795: a 47-year lag. Lesson: a great research question that fails to persuade an institution can sit on the shelf for half a century.
