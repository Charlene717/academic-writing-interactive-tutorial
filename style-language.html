故事開場

場景：博士生 Wei 的翻譯腔

SCENE · 2025 · TAIPEI

從「Please have a native speaker edit」到 minor revision

博士生 Wei 把研究 RNA-seq 兩年的成果寫成英文初稿投到一本 IF 8 期刊。內容紮實，但他每寫一句都先用中文想再翻成英文。三週後 reviewer 1 回信：「The science is interesting, but the language requires significant improvement. Please have a native English speaker edit before resubmission.」（科學有趣，但語言需大幅改善，請找母語編輯。）

Wei 一開始很挫折，但他的 PI 給了他一本 Joseph Williams 的《Style: Toward Clarity and Grace》。讀完前三章他發現自己每句都有同樣的毛病：被動語態、把動詞藏進名詞裡（"the conduction of an analysis"）、缺冠詞（"data was analyzed by R"）、句子又長又散。

他花兩週重寫整篇，每段檢查：(1) 主動 vs 被動比例；(2) 把 "made / performed / conducted" 換掉；(3) "in order to" → "to"；(4) 老資訊放句首、新資訊放句尾。改完後請一位母語同事念出 abstract，能順暢念完就過關。重投後 minor revision，三個月接受。Wei 後來說：「不是英文不夠好，是不知道學術風格有這麼具體的規則。」

PhD student Wei writes up two years of RNA-seq work as his first English manuscript and submits to a journal with IF 8. The science is solid, but he drafts every sentence in Chinese first and translates. Three weeks later Reviewer 1 writes: "The science is interesting, but the language requires significant improvement. Please have a native English speaker edit before resubmission."

Wei is discouraged, but his PI hands him Joseph Williams's Style: Toward Clarity and Grace. The first three chapters show him his own habits: passive voice everywhere, actions hidden in nouns ("the conduction of an analysis"), missing articles ("data was analyzed by R"), and sentences that sprawl.

He spends two weeks rewriting. Each paragraph gets checked: (1) active-to-passive ratio; (2) replace "made / performed / conducted" with strong verbs; (3) "in order to" → "to"; (4) put old information at the start and new information at the end. When finished, a native-speaker colleague reads the abstract aloud — fluent through = pass. Resubmission earns minor revision and the paper is accepted in three months. Wei reflects: "It wasn't that my English was bad — I didn't know academic style had such concrete rules."

核心詞彙

14 個風格術語（雙版本）

每個詞兩個版本：🧒「給小孩看」用比喻、📐「給研究者看」給定義。

Each term has two versions: 🧒 "for kids" uses a metaphor; 📐 "for researchers" gives the formal definition.

Active voice
主動語態
「誰做了什麼」直接講——A 打 B，主詞在前。
"Who did what" up front — A hits B, subject first.
主詞 = 動作執行者；句型 S-V-O。Nature/Cell/NEJM 2020 後普遍鼓勵。例：「We measured...」
Subject = agent of the action; S-V-O. Encouraged by Nature/Cell/NEJM since ~2020. Eg: "We measured…"
Passive voice
被動語態
「B 被 A 打」——主詞變成受詞，做動作的人退場。
"B was hit by A" — subject becomes recipient, actor steps offstage.
be + past participle；主詞 = 受動者。可保留 (methods 強調程序) 或避免 (results 強調發現)。
be + past participle; subject = patient. Acceptable in methods (procedure focus); avoid in results (finding focus).
Nominalization
名詞化
把動詞做成名詞，再用一個假動詞拉出來——「做了一個分析」而不是「分析」。
Turn the verb into a noun, then drag it back with a fake verb — "made an analysis" instead of "analyzed."
verb→noun 衍生 (analyze→analysis)。Williams 1981 視為最大風格病源。例：performance of comparison → compared.
Verb→noun derivation (analyze→analysis). Williams 1981 calls it the single worst style ailment. Eg: performance of comparison → compared.
Hedging
保留語
不要把話講死——用「可能」「或許」留退路。
Don't claim too hard — use "may / suggests / appears" to leave room.
Hyland 1998 提出；標誌「主張的確信度」。常見：may, might, suggest, appear, seem, likely, possibly。
Hyland 1998; markers of claim certainty. Common: may, might, suggest, appear, seem, likely, possibly.
Cohesion (old-to-new)
銜接（舊→新）
每句開頭講上一句已說過的事，結尾才丟新東西——像接力跑棒交給下一句。
Each sentence starts with what came before, ends with something new — like passing a baton.
Halliday-Hasan 1976 提出；Williams 強調 known-information-first。段落內 ≥80% 句子符合舊→新較易讀。
Halliday-Hasan 1976; Williams emphasizes known-information-first. Paragraphs with ≥80% old→new sentences read more smoothly.
Collocation
搭配語
英文「習慣搭」——"strong tea" 對，"powerful tea" 怪，但你說不出原因。
Words that "go together" in English — "strong tea" right, "powerful tea" odd, but you can't say why.
高頻共現詞對；L2 作者最大盲點。學術範例：conduct a study (✓) vs make a study (✗)。可查 SkELL / Linggle。
High-frequency co-occurring word pairs; biggest L2 blind spot. Academic: conduct a study (✓) vs make a study (✗). Check via SkELL / Linggle.
Article (a/an/the)
冠詞
英文每個可數名詞前面都要有「招牌」——「一個」「那個」或不戴。中文沒有，所以漢語母語者最容易漏。
Every English countable noun needs a "sign" — a/an/the/none. Chinese has none, so Chinese L1 writers drop them most.
a/an=非特定可數單數；the=特定或已提；零冠詞=不可數/泛指複數。漢語、日語、韓語、俄語 L1 高錯誤率。
a/an=indefinite singular; the=definite/anaphoric; zero=mass/generic plural. High error rate among Chinese, Japanese, Korean, Russian L1 writers.
Hedge verb
保留動詞
「動詞版」的保留語——「我們發現」太硬，「我們建議」軟一點。
The verb form of hedging — "we found" is hard; "we suggest" is softer.
suggest, indicate, imply, appear, seem, propose。Results 用 found/observed；Discussion 用 suggest/imply。
suggest, indicate, imply, appear, seem, propose. Use found/observed in Results; suggest/imply in Discussion.
Hedge adverb
保留副詞
「副詞版」的保留語——「絕對是」變「可能是」就靠它。
The adverb form of hedging — turns "is" into "may possibly be."
possibly, likely, probably, perhaps, somewhat, relatively。避免堆疊 (may possibly = 1 個就夠)。
possibly, likely, probably, perhaps, somewhat, relatively. Don't stack (may possibly = one is enough).
Run-on sentence
連寫句
兩個獨立句中間只用逗號連——像兩台車碰碰相黏。
Two independent clauses joined only by a comma — two cars bumper-bumped.
comma splice 子類；解法：句號、分號、或加連接詞 (and/but/because)。L2 作者高頻誤用。
A type of comma splice; fix with a period, semicolon, or coordinating conjunction (and/but/because). Frequent L2 error.
Subject-verb agreement
主謂一致
主詞單數動詞單數、主詞複數動詞複數——別被中間插的句子搞亂。
Singular subject takes singular verb — don't be tricked by phrases in between.
"The set of samples were…" (✗) → "The set of samples was…" (✓)。長介系詞片語常造成 attractive error。
"The set of samples were…" (✗) → "was…" (✓). Long prepositional phrases cause attraction errors.
that vs which
限定 vs 非限定
that = 沒有它整句變不完整；which = 加註用，前面要有逗號。
that = essential, no comma; which = extra info, with comma.
Restrictive (that, no comma) vs non-restrictive (which, with commas)。AMA/APA 都採此區分；英式英語較寬鬆。
Restrictive (that, no comma) vs non-restrictive (which, with commas). AMA/APA enforce; British English looser.
Topic sentence
主題句
每段第一句先講「這段要講什麼」，後面才填細節——別讓讀者猜。
First sentence of each paragraph says what the paragraph is about — don't make the reader guess.
段落控制句；通常 sentence 1 或 sentence 2 (若 sentence 1 為過渡)。Strunk-White 經典原則。
Paragraph-controlling sentence; usually sentence 1 (or 2 if 1 is a transition). Classic Strunk-White principle.
Plain language
平實語言
用最短、最常見的字寫複雜的概念——能用 use 就不要用 utilize。
Use the shortest, most common word for complex ideas — pick "use" over "utilize."
Plain Language Movement 1999；強調 active、短句、常見字。US Plain Writing Act 2010 立法要求政府文件採用。
Plain Language Movement 1999; emphasizes active voice, short sentences, common words. US Plain Writing Act 2010 mandates it for government documents.

關鍵指標

風格的數字感

~70%現代論文主動語態目標
17-25句子平均字數
1句一個概念
2:1強動詞 : 名詞化
≤7主詞需在前 7 字內
80%段內舊→新銜接句佔比
這些不是硬性規則，而是「健康指標」。如果某篇你的初稿主動語態 <30%、句子平均 >30 字，那基本上需要重寫。
These aren't hard rules — they're health checks. If a draft scores active voice <30% and avg sentence >30 words, it needs a rewrite.
類比：學術風格就像水管——讀者不應該注意到水管，而是注意到水（你的想法）順暢地流過。如果讀者每讀兩句就要停下來解碼，水管漏了。 Analogy: Academic style is plumbing — the reader shouldn't notice the pipes; they should notice your idea flowing through. If they stop every two sentences to decode, the pipes are leaking.
操作演練

潤稿 8 步

把這 8 步當「checklist」跑過你的 draft 至少一次。每步只看一件事，不要混合處理。

Run these 8 steps as a checklist over your draft at least once. Each pass looks at one thing — don't multitask.

Σ₁
搜尋「in order to」→「to」

Ctrl-F 搜「in order to」，幾乎所有都可改為「to」。同類：「due to the fact that」→「because」、「at this point in time」→「now」。

Ctrl-F for "in order to" — nearly all reduce to "to." Same family: "due to the fact that" → "because"; "at this point in time" → "now."

Σ₂
搜尋「It was」「There were」→ 主動句

這兩個句首是 expletive construction (虛主詞)，幾乎都能改寫得更短更主動。例：「It was found that X increased.」→「X increased.」

Both are expletive constructions — nearly always rewritable as shorter active sentences. Eg: "It was found that X increased." → "X increased."

Σ₃
搜尋 make / perform / conduct → 強動詞

「performed an analysis of」→「analyzed」；「conducted a comparison」→「compared」；「made an investigation」→「investigated」。Williams 1981 的核心練習。

"performed an analysis of" → "analyzed"; "conducted a comparison" → "compared"; "made an investigation" → "investigated." Williams 1981's core drill.

Σ₄
動詞-證據強度校準

Results 用「found / observed / showed」(現在式或過去式)；Discussion 用「suggest / indicate / imply」。如果你只有 n=3 不要寫「demonstrates」；如果你有 RCT 不要弱化成「may suggest」。

Results: "found / observed / showed." Discussion: "suggest / indicate / imply." Don't write "demonstrates" if you have n=3, and don't water down an RCT to "may suggest."

Σ₅
念出來——切斷長句

把整段念出聲。如果有句子讓你需要「半路偷氣」，那就是太長。30 字以上的句子強烈建議拆成兩句。

Read the paragraph aloud. Any sentence where you sneak a breath mid-way is too long. Strongly split anything >30 words into two.

Σ₆
檢查段內舊→新銜接

每句的開頭應該講「上一句末尾或前面已說的事」，結尾才放新資訊。如果連續兩句都用新主詞開頭，讀者會卡住。

Each sentence should open with something the previous one (or earlier text) already said, and end with the new information. Two new subjects in a row will stall the reader.

Σ₇
跑 Grammarly / DeepL 抓 L2 錯誤

冠詞、單複數、主謂一致、prepositions — 這些 L2 作者高頻錯誤，AI 工具 95% 抓得到。但「風格」與「強動詞」AI 抓不準，仍需人工。

Articles, singular/plural, subject-verb agreement, prepositions — high-frequency L2 errors that AI catches ~95% of the time. Style and strong-verb choices remain a human call.

Σ₈
母語同事念 abstract

最終測試：請一位英文母語的同事「念出 abstract」並 30 秒內復述你的研究問題與發現。如果他卡住或誤解，abstract 還沒過關。

Final test: ask a native-English colleague to read the abstract aloud and paraphrase the question and finding in 30 seconds. If they stumble or misread, the abstract isn't ready.

專家辯論

三個風格爭議

(a) 主動語態的強制

正方：Nature 2020 後審稿明確鼓勵主動；責任、可讀性、簡潔三贏。反方：Methods 強調「過程」而非「人」；被動才能凸顯重現性。共識：Results & Discussion 大量主動 (≥60%)、Methods 可保留被動 (~50/50)。

For: Nature since 2020 explicitly encourages active in reviews — gains in accountability, readability, brevity. Against: Methods emphasizes "process," not "actor"; passive highlights reproducibility. Consensus: Results & Discussion mostly active (≥60%); Methods can stay ~50/50 passive.

(b) L2 作者用 AI 編修

正方：把「英文門檻」拉低，讓 L2 作者用內容競爭而非語言；Salim 2025 BMJ 證實 AI 編修改善被引用率。反方：過度 AI 化讓所有 paper 風格雷同；冒抄襲、作者性消失之險。共識：AI 用來抓文法錯誤 (Σ₇) 可，整篇 rewrite 不可；揭露 AI 使用 (Nature 2023 政策)。

For: Lowers the English barrier so L2 authors compete on content; Salim 2025 BMJ shows AI editing improves citation rates. Against: Over-AI-ed prose homogenizes style; raises plagiarism and authorial-voice concerns. Consensus: Fine for grammar (Σ₇), not for full rewriting; disclose AI use (Nature 2023 policy).

(c) Hedge 太多 vs claim 太硬

Hedge 太多：「may possibly suggest a potential trend」聽起來像沒信心；reviewer 會問「那你到底發現什麼？」Claim 太硬：「proves / demonstrates conclusively」與 n=12 不匹配；reviewer 會直接 reject。原則：動詞強度 = 證據強度。RCT 用 demonstrate；觀察性研究用 suggest；探索性 omics 用 propose。

Over-hedged: "may possibly suggest a potential trend" sounds spineless; reviewers ask "so what did you find?" Over-claimed: "proves / demonstrates conclusively" with n=12 invites desk reject. Rule: verb strength = evidence strength. RCT → demonstrate; observational → suggest; exploratory omics → propose.

兩個典範

範本與警鐘

範本：Watson & Crick 1953 Nature

DNA 雙螺旋論文全文僅 900 字、13 段，每段一個概念，平實語言、強動詞、沒有任何 nominalization 鏈。開頭即名句：「We wish to suggest a structure for the salt of deoxyribose nucleic acid.」短句、主動、保留語精準——同時拿諾貝爾獎與寫作獎。Sword 2020 將它列為「stylish academic writing」首例。

The DNA double-helix paper is only 900 words across 13 paragraphs — one idea per paragraph, plain language, strong verbs, no nominalization chains. The famous opener: "We wish to suggest a structure for the salt of deoxyribose nucleic acid." Short, active, precisely hedged — Nobel and writing prize in one. Sword 2020 lists it as the first model of "stylish academic writing."

警鐘：Sokal 1996 hoax

物理學家 Alan Sokal 1996 投稿一篇全是「後現代行話 + 物理術語亂組」的偽論文〈Transgressing the Boundaries〉到 Social Text 期刊。期刊接受發表，Sokal 隨即在 Lingua Franca 揭露：沒人看得懂，所以也沒人能反駁。這成為「過度行話 / 無意義抽象 / 無 falsifiability」三項風格警報的經典案例。今日寫作教科書 (Pinker 2014) 將其列為「curse of knowledge」極端版。

Physicist Alan Sokal submitted a parody article — postmodern jargon randomly fused with physics terminology — to Social Text in 1996. It was accepted; Sokal then exposed the hoax in Lingua Franca: no one could decode it, so no one could refute it. The case became the canonical warning against jargon-overload, meaningless abstraction, and non-falsifiable prose. Pinker 2014 lists it as an extreme "curse of knowledge."

6 個典型風格陷阱

L2 作者最常踩的 6 個地雷

① 被動氾濫

「The experiments were performed by us」「Samples were collected by the technicians」全篇被動。讀者讀不到「誰做的」，也讓問責模糊。對策：抓出每個 "was/were + done" 句，至少一半改主動。

"The experiments were performed by us"; "Samples were collected by the technicians" — passive everywhere. The reader can't tell who acted, and accountability blurs. Fix: find every "was/were + done" sentence; rewrite at least half as active.

② 名詞化鏈

「The conduction of an analysis of the comparison of expression levels」——一個動作 (compare) 被裹了三層名詞。對策：找到核心動作 (compare)，重寫成「We compared expression levels」。

"The conduction of an analysis of the comparison of expression levels" — one action (compare) buried under three noun layers. Fix: identify the core verb (compare) and rewrite as "We compared expression levels."

③ 冠詞錯誤

「Data was analyzed by R」(漏 the)、「We used a R package」(冠詞錯誤 a→an)、「The proteins are important」(泛指應零冠詞)。對策：Grammarly / DeepL Write 抓 ≥90%；剩下請母語同事看一次。

"Data was analyzed by R" (missing "the"); "We used a R package" (a→an); "The proteins are important" (generic should be zero article). Fix: Grammarly / DeepL Write catch ≥90%; ask a native colleague to spot-check the rest.

④ 連寫句

「Gene X was upregulated, this confirms our hypothesis」——兩個獨立子句只用逗號連。對策：(1) 改句號；(2) 改分號；(3) 加 "and / because / which"。例：「..., which confirms our hypothesis.」

"Gene X was upregulated, this confirms our hypothesis" — two independent clauses joined by only a comma. Fix: (1) period; (2) semicolon; (3) add "and/because/which." Eg: "..., which confirms our hypothesis."

⑤ 主謂不一致

「The expression of these genes were upregulated」——主詞是 expression (單數)，不是 genes (複數)。長介系詞片語經常造成 attraction error。對策：把中間介系詞片語遮住，再對主詞與動詞。

"The expression of these genes were upregulated" — subject is "expression" (singular), not "genes." Long prepositional phrases cause attraction errors. Fix: mentally cover the prepositional phrase, then match subject-verb.

⑥ 翻譯腔搭配

「make a research」(✗ → conduct/do a study)、「open the computer」(✗ → turn on)、「receive a treatment」(✓ 但中文式語序「a treatment was received by」✗)。對策：用 SkELL / Linggle / Just-the-Word 查英文真實搭配。

"make a research" (✗ → conduct/do a study); "open the computer" (✗ → turn on); "receive a treatment" (✓, but Chinese-word-order "a treatment was received by" ✗). Fix: verify with SkELL / Linggle / Just-the-Word.

❌ 全壞範例

"It was observed by us that an upregulation of Gene X was found via the conduction of a comparison analysis between the treated group and the control group."

(27 字、被動、雙重名詞化、冗餘介系詞鏈、無數值)

(27 words, passive, double nominalization, redundant prepositional chain, no numbers)

✅ 修好範例

"We found Gene X upregulated in treated vs control samples (log2FC=2.3, p<0.001)."

(13 字、主動、強動詞、含關鍵數值與檢定)

(13 words, active, strong verb, with effect size and p-value)

自我反思

6 個自問問題

  1. 每段是否以舊資訊開頭、以新資訊結尾？
  2. 你的主動 vs 被動比例是多少？(Results 應 ≥60% 主動)
  3. 你的動詞強度是否匹配證據強度？(n=12 不該用 demonstrate)
  4. 每段是否有清楚的 topic sentence？
  5. 你是否堆疊了 hedge？(may possibly suggest a potential)
  6. 念出 abstract 時，你需要幾次喘氣？(>2 表示句子太長)
進階補充

進階：三條風格的學術深化

(a) Pinker：classic vs plain prose

Pinker (2014, The Sense of Style) 將寫作風格分為兩大類型：Classic prose（「看見世界」型）假設作者與讀者並肩看世界，作者只是指出值得注意的事——這是學術寫作該追求的；Plain prose（「告訴你」型）平鋪直敘陳述事實。兩者皆比後現代「self-reflexive prose」（「告訴你我如何告訴你」型）更受歡迎。Pinker 強調學術作者最大的敵人是 curse of knowledge：你已經懂太多，無法想像讀者不懂的痛苦。

Pinker (2014, The Sense of Style) groups writing into two main modes: classic prose ("seeing the world") imagines author and reader gazing together — the author just points out what's worth noticing; this is what academic writing should aim for. Plain prose ("telling you") narrates facts directly. Both beat postmodern self-reflexive prose ("telling you how I'm telling you"). The academic writer's chief enemy is the curse of knowledge: you know too much to imagine what readers don't.

(b) Swales' CARS 2024 更新

Swales 1990 原始 CARS (Create-A-Research-Space) 三步：(1) Establishing a territory; (2) Establishing a niche; (3) Occupying the niche。2024 更新 (Swales & Feak 4th ed)：增加「Move 0: Establishing relevance to society」回應 SDG / impact 趨勢；Move 2 (niche) 從「gap」擴大為「gap + counter-claiming + question-raising」。AI 寫作工具普及後，CARS 仍是 introduction 結構的黃金標準，但 narrative 與 storytelling 元素 (Sword 2020) 開始與 CARS 結合。

Swales 1990's original CARS (Create-A-Research-Space) has three moves: (1) Establish a territory; (2) Establish a niche; (3) Occupy the niche. 2024 update (Swales & Feak 4th ed) adds "Move 0: Establishing relevance to society" to reflect SDG / impact trends; Move 2 (niche) expands from "gap" to "gap + counter-claiming + question-raising." CARS remains the gold standard for introduction structure even with AI tools, but narrative and storytelling elements (Sword 2020) increasingly blend in.

(c) AI 英文編修工具 2025-26

DeepL Write：強項在 collocation 與母語化句構 (歐語 L1 開發)；學術語境較弱。Claude / GPT-5：能讀懂上下文，整段重寫品質高；風險是「過度 AI 化」風格雷同。Grammarly Premium：抓文法錯誤 / 冠詞 / agreement 最快；但不擅長 nominalization 與強動詞建議。建議組合：Grammarly 跑 Σ₇ 文法層 → DeepL Write 跑 collocation → Claude / GPT 跑「結構與強動詞」最終 polish。記得 Nature 2023、Cell 2024 政策要求揭露 AI 使用。

DeepL Write: excels at collocations and native-sounding clauses (European L1 development); weaker on academic register. Claude / GPT-5: understand context, produce high-quality paragraph-level rewrites; risk of homogenized "AI-ed" style. Grammarly Premium: fastest at grammar, articles, agreement; weak on nominalization and strong-verb suggestions. Recommended stack: Grammarly for Σ₇ grammar → DeepL Write for collocation → Claude / GPT for structure and strong-verb polish. Disclose AI use per Nature 2023 / Cell 2024 policies.

歷史小記

Einstein 的 14 字句

Einstein 名言：「If you can't explain it simply, you don't understand it well enough.」(如果你無法簡單地解釋它，你就還沒真正理解它。) 他 1905 年的「奇蹟年」四篇論文 (光電效應、布朗運動、狹義相對論、質能等價)，平均每句僅 14 字——遠短於今日同領域論文的 22-25 字。當年沒有 LLM 編修、沒有 Grammarly，只有清晰的思考。下次你寫長句卡住時，問自己：「Einstein 會怎麼寫？」

Einstein famously said, "If you can't explain it simply, you don't understand it well enough." His four annus mirabilis papers of 1905 (photoelectric effect, Brownian motion, special relativity, mass-energy equivalence) average just 14 words per sentence — far shorter than today's 22-25 in the same fields. No LLM, no Grammarly — just clear thinking. Next time a long sentence won't move, ask: "How would Einstein write this?"

概覽

風格不是裝飾

學術寫作的目標不是炫技。一個句子讀起來吃力，讀者會懷疑作者是否真的清楚自己在說什麼。風格的核心是：讓讀者花最少的認知成本理解你最複雜的結果。

三個原則：(1) 主詞越具體越好；(2) 動詞越強越好；(3) 句子越短越好（除非真的需要長）。

Academic writing isn't about showing off. A laborious sentence makes readers doubt the author understands their own work. The point of style is: let the reader spend minimum cognitive effort understanding your most complex result.

Three principles: (1) concrete subjects; (2) strong verbs; (3) short sentences unless length is justified.

主動 vs 被動

一、主動 vs 被動的共識

1990 年代以前，科學寫作偏好被動語態（"It was found that..."）。2026 年的主流期刊（含 Nature, Cell, NEJM）已普遍鼓勵主動語態，因為主詞清楚、句子更短、責任歸屬明確。

例外：當主詞是「方法 / 程序」而非「人」時，被動仍合適。例：「Cells were stained with..." — 因為強調動作而非執行者。

Before the 1990s, science writing preferred passive voice ("It was found that..."). By 2026, mainstream journals — including Nature, Cell, NEJM — encourage active voice because subjects are clear, sentences shorter, and accountability explicit.

Exception: when the subject is the method/procedure (not a person), passive remains fine. Eg: "Cells were stained with..." — emphasis on action, not actor.

❌ 被動 / 累贅

"It was observed by us that the expression of Gene X was significantly upregulated in the treated group as compared to the control group." (24 字)

"It was observed by us that the expression of Gene X was significantly upregulated in the treated group as compared to the control group." (24 words)

✅ 主動 / 精煉

"Gene X was upregulated in treated vs control samples (log2FC=2.3, p<0.001)." (10 字)

"Gene X was upregulated in treated vs control samples (log2FC=2.3, p<0.001)." (10 words)

強動詞

二、強動詞 vs nominalization

L2 作者最常被改的：動詞名詞化 (nominalization)。把動作藏進名詞裡，再用一個弱動詞（make / perform / conduct）拉出來。

The most common L2-author rewrite: nominalization — burying the action in a noun and using a weak verb (make / perform / conduct) to drag it back out.

三、Hedging 強度

學術寫作的 verb 強度 = 證據強度。用錯一級就會變 overclaim 或退縮。

Verb strength = evidence strength. Off by one level = overclaim or retreat.

段落連貫

四、過渡詞 / 連貫

段落內句子之間需要邏輯連接。亂跳的句子讓讀者覺得「這作者跟不上自己」。常用過渡詞分類：

  • 承接：moreover, furthermore, in addition, similarly
  • 對比：however, in contrast, nevertheless, conversely
  • 因果：therefore, thus, consequently, as a result
  • 例證：for example, for instance, specifically, notably
  • 讓步：although, despite, while, even though
  • 總結：in summary, taken together, overall, collectively

Sentences within a paragraph need logical glue. Jumpy sentences make a reader feel the author can't keep up with themselves. Common transitions:

  • Addition: moreover, furthermore, in addition, similarly
  • Contrast: however, in contrast, nevertheless, conversely
  • Causation: therefore, thus, consequently, as a result
  • Example: for example, for instance, specifically, notably
  • Concession: although, despite, while, even though
  • Summary: in summary, taken together, overall, collectively
💡
「Old to new」原則：每句的開頭應接續上一句的結尾。例：上一句結尾是 "...chemoresistance"，下一句開頭可以是 "This resistance is driven by..."，讀者就能順著走。 "Old to new" rule: each sentence opens with what the previous one ended on. E.g., last sentence ends "...chemoresistance," next opens "This resistance is driven by..." — readers follow naturally.
L2 常見錯誤

五、L2 作者 10 大錯誤

① 冠詞

中文沒有冠詞，最常缺漏。規則：第一次提及用 a/an；第二次提及或唯一指稱用 the；複數泛稱用 Ø。例：「The mechanism remains unclear」(已提過)；「Cells were treated」(複數泛稱)。

Chinese lacks articles — most often dropped. Rule: first mention → a/an; subsequent or unique reference → the; plural generic → Ø. E.g., "The mechanism remains unclear" (mentioned); "Cells were treated" (plural generic).

② 單複數

"data is" → 改 "data are" (data 為複數)；analyses, criteria, phenomena 同理。但 species, series 單複數同形。

"data is" → "data are" (data is plural); analyses, criteria, phenomena likewise. species, series have same singular/plural form.

③ 時態

Methods 與 Results 用過去式 (we performed, we found)；既有事實用現在式 (BRCA1 is a tumor suppressor)；Discussion 混用。

Methods/Results in past tense (we performed, we found); established facts in present tense (BRCA1 is a tumor suppressor); Discussion mixes both.

④ 主謂一致

"The level of Gene X are..." → "The level (單數) of Gene X is..."。被中間片語打斷後容易失準。

"The level of Gene X are..." → "The level (singular) of Gene X is..." Easy to slip when an intervening phrase intrudes.

⑤ 搭配 (Collocation)

"do an experiment" ✅；"make an experiment" ❌。"strong evidence" ✅；"powerful evidence" ❌。被認為是「翻譯腔」最大來源。

"do an experiment" ✅; "make an experiment" ❌. "strong evidence" ✅; "powerful evidence" ❌. The single biggest source of "translationese."

⑥ 逗號黏連

"Gene X was upregulated, this suggests..." → 改 "Gene X was upregulated. This suggests..." 或 "Gene X was upregulated, suggesting..."。

"Gene X was upregulated, this suggests..." → "Gene X was upregulated. This suggests..." or "Gene X was upregulated, suggesting..."

⑦ That vs Which

限定子句用 that，無逗號；補述子句用 which，有逗號。"The genes that were upregulated" vs "the genes, which were upregulated, ..."

Restrictive: "that" without comma. Non-restrictive: "which" with comma. "The genes that were upregulated" vs "the genes, which were upregulated, ..."

⑧ 代名詞模糊

"This shows..." 一句中有 3 個 candidate，"this" 指誰？改 "This upregulation shows..."、"This finding shows..."。

"This shows..." after a sentence with 3 candidates — "this" refers to which? Use "This upregulation shows..." or "This finding shows..."

⑨ 冗詞

"in order to" → "to"；"a number of" → "several"；"due to the fact that" → "because"；"at this point in time" → "now"。

"in order to" → "to"; "a number of" → "several"; "due to the fact that" → "because"; "at this point in time" → "now."

⑩ 句尾介系詞

傳統規則：避免句尾用介系詞 (in, with, of)。2026 已不嚴格，但正式論文中仍建議改寫。

Traditional rule: avoid ending with prepositions (in, with, of). By 2026 this is relaxed, but in formal manuscripts rewriting is still advised.
