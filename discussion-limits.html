故事開場

場景：被 PI 退回的第一稿 Discussion

SCENE · 2025 · NTUH

從「press release」到「被接受的 Discussion」

博士後 Hsuan 第一次獨立寫完一篇 Discussion，把 draft 交給 PI 隔天就收到回信，只有一行紅字：「你寫的是 press release，不是 Discussion。」她翻開自己的稿：第一段寫「本研究證實 Gene X 是 TNBC 化療抗性的關鍵驅動因子」——一個 24 例的觀察性 cohort，沒有 functional validation。整篇沒有一條 limitations。

PI 把她叫進辦公室，講了 2010 年代的阿茲海默症 amyloid hypothesis：上百篇 papers 在 Discussion overclaim「amyloid 是因」，藥廠投入超過 400 億美元，臨床試驗一個接一個 fail。「不是資料造假，是 Discussion 寫太滿。一句『may』、一句『however, observational design』，就能救一條科學路線。」

Hsuan 重寫：把「proves」改成「suggests」「may」「is associated with」；補上 3 條具體的 limitations（不是「樣本太少」這種廢話）；明確跟 Smith 2023、Chen 2024 的既有發現對話，標出哪裡一致、哪裡延伸。三週後，manuscript accept；reviewer 2 在 comments 裡寫了一句：「Refreshingly calibrated discussion. Authors clearly know what their data can and cannot say.」

Postdoc Hsuan finishes her first solo-written Discussion and emails it to her PI. The next morning the reply contains one red line: "You wrote a press release, not a Discussion." She reopens her draft: paragraph one declares "This study proves Gene X is the key driver of chemo-resistance in TNBC" — from a 24-sample observational cohort with no functional validation. Not a single limitation is listed.

The PI sits her down and tells the story of the 2010s amyloid hypothesis in Alzheimer's: hundreds of papers overclaimed in their Discussions that amyloid was causal; pharma sank over US$40 billion into trials that failed one after another. "It wasn't fraud. It was Discussions written too tightly. One 'may', one 'however, observational design' — that's all it takes to save a scientific direction."

Hsuan rewrites. "Proves" becomes "suggests", "may", "is associated with". She adds three specific limitations (not the vague "small sample" cop-out). She explicitly converses with Smith 2023 and Chen 2024 — where her data agree, where they extend. Three weeks later: accepted. Reviewer 2 leaves one line in the comments: "Refreshingly calibrated discussion. Authors clearly know what their data can and cannot say."

核心詞彙

14 個關鍵術語（雙版本）

每個詞兩個版本：🧒「給小孩看」用比喻、📐「給研究者看」給定義。

Each term has two versions: 🧒 "for kids" uses a metaphor; 📐 "for researchers" gives the formal definition.

Discussion section
討論章
論文的最後一場辯論——你拿你的證據去跟整個領域對話。
The paper's final debate — where you take your evidence and converse with the whole field.
IMRaD 結構的 D 段；任務 = 解釋意義、放入文獻脈絡、列出限制、提供未來方向；通常 5-7 段。
The D in IMRaD; tasks = interpret meaning, contextualize within literature, list limitations, propose future directions; usually 5-7 paragraphs.
Inverted funnel
倒漏斗
從一個小水滴開始，慢慢張開到整個海洋——Discussion 從具體結果出發、漸漸擴大到領域意義。
Start with a droplet, open out to the ocean — Discussion goes from specific finding to broad field implication.
Discussion 的結構模式：第一段最窄（重述主結論），最後一段最廣（領域意義/未來方向）；與 Introduction 的「漏斗」(broad→narrow) 互為鏡像。
Discussion's structural mode: first paragraph narrowest (restate main finding), last paragraph broadest (field implications, future directions); mirrors Introduction's funnel (broad→narrow).
Hedging
校準語氣
不要說「絕對」，也不要說「完全不知道」——找剛剛好的「可能」「也許」。
Don't say "definitely", don't say "no idea" — find the right "maybe" / "likely" in between.
校準的不確定性表達；動詞分四級 (demonstrates / indicates / suggests / may)；證據力越弱，hedging 越深。
Calibrated expression of uncertainty; verbs span 4 tiers (demonstrates / indicates / suggests / may); the weaker the evidence, the deeper the hedge.
Limitation
研究限制
老實說你的實驗有哪裡不夠好——不是缺點，是邊界。
Honestly admit where your experiment falls short — not a flaw, a boundary.
三層 honest 寫法：① 內部 (內部 validity)、② 外部 (generalizability)、③ 方法學 (technique)。每條須有 mitigation + future fix。
3-layer honest format: ① internal (internal validity), ② external (generalizability), ③ methodological (technique). Each needs mitigation + future fix.
Confounding
干擾因子
兩件事看起來有關，其實是第三件事在背後操控——像冰淇淋銷量和溺水率（夏天才是真兇）。
Two things look linked but a third hidden factor is pulling the strings — like ice-cream sales and drowning rates (summer is the real culprit).
同時影響 exposure 與 outcome 的第三變項；觀察性研究的核心威脅；解方 = stratification、matching、regression adjustment、DAG (Pearl 2009)。
A third variable affecting both exposure and outcome; the core threat of observational studies; solutions = stratification, matching, regression adjustment, DAGs (Pearl 2009).
Generalizability
外推性
你在台北市的小學發現的事，能不能套到屏東漁村？這就是 generalizability。
What you found in a Taipei elementary school — does it apply to a Pingtung fishing village? That's generalizability.
= External validity；研究結論可推廣到其他人群/設定/時代的程度。Single-center cohort 通常 generalizability 較弱。
= External validity; extent to which findings extend to other populations / settings / eras. Single-center cohorts typically have weaker generalizability.
Overclaim
過度宣稱
你的證據只能說「可能」，你卻寫「絕對」——這就是 overclaim。
Your evidence can only say "maybe" but you wrote "definitely" — that's overclaim.
超出證據能支持的強度的陳述；常見：correlation 寫成 causation、cohort n=20 寫成「will revolutionize」、in silico 寫成「demonstrates clinically」。
Claims stronger than evidence supports; common forms: correlation written as causation, n=20 cohort written as "will revolutionize", in-silico written as "demonstrates clinically".
Calibrated language
校準語言
你的詞要剛好搭配你手上的證據——不多一分、不少一分。
Your words must match your evidence — no more, no less.
動詞、副詞與證據強度對齊：RCT → "demonstrates"；observational → "associated"；in silico → "may"。Reviewer 第一個檢查的就是這個一致性。
Verbs/adverbs aligned to evidence strength: RCT → "demonstrates"; observational → "associated"; in silico → "may". This alignment is the first thing reviewers check.
Causal vs association language
因果 vs 關聯語言
「A 跟 B 一起出現」≠「A 造成 B」——這兩種說法絕對不能混用。
"A and B appear together" ≠ "A causes B" — never mix these two.
關聯：correlated / associated / linked；因果：causes / drives / induces。只有 RCT、Mendelian randomization、或 functional perturbation 才允許因果語言。Bradford Hill 1965 為經典判準。
Association: correlated / associated / linked; Causal: causes / drives / induces. Only RCTs, Mendelian randomization, or functional perturbation permit causal language. Bradford Hill 1965 remains the classic framework.
Negative result
陰性結果
你猜會發生的事沒發生——這也是有用的資訊，不是失敗。
What you predicted didn't happen — that's still useful information, not a failure.
未達顯著的結果；不等於「不存在效應」(absence of evidence ≠ evidence of absence)。Discussion 須說明 power、effect size、可能 type II 等。Registered Reports 鼓勵發表。
A non-significant result; ≠ "no effect" (absence of evidence ≠ evidence of absence). Discussion must address power, effect size, possible type II. Registered Reports encourage publishing them.
Internal validity
內部效度
「在這個實驗裡，A 真的造成 B 嗎？」——只看研究裡面有沒有 bug。
"Within this experiment, did A truly cause B?" — about whether the study has bugs inside.
設計上排除替代解釋（confounding、selection bias、measurement error）的程度；RCT + blinding + randomization 內部效度最強。
Extent to which alternative explanations (confounding, selection bias, measurement error) are ruled out by design; RCT + blinding + randomization yields strongest internal validity.
External validity
外部效度
「這個結果到外面真實世界還能用嗎？」——別人能不能套用你的結論。
"Will this result still work in the real world outside?" — whether others can apply your conclusion.
= Generalizability；推廣到不同人群、設定、時代的能力。內部與外部效度常成反比 (efficacy vs effectiveness)。
= Generalizability; ability to extend to other populations, settings, eras. Internal and external validity often trade off (efficacy vs effectiveness).
Mechanism speculation
機制推測
你猜為什麼會這樣——但要明確說這只是「猜」，不是「證明」。
You guess why it happens — but explicitly mark it as a guess, not proof.
Discussion 中提出 plausible mechanism 時須明確標 speculative；可引用既知 pathway / 細胞類型佐證，但不可呈現為已證實。
When proposing plausible mechanism in Discussion, flag it as speculative explicitly; can cite known pathways / cell types as support but never present as proven.
Future direction
未來方向
下一步要做什麼具體實驗——不是「需要更多研究」這種廢話。
What specific experiment is next — not the lame "more research is needed".
2-3 個 actionable next steps；具體到「在 PDX models 中做 Gene X knockdown 驗證」這種程度；避免 boilerplate「需要更多研究」。
2-3 actionable next steps; specific to the level of "validate Gene X knockdown in PDX models"; avoid boilerplate "more research is needed".

比喻
Discussion 就像審判庭上的結辯：你不是再背一遍證據（那是 Results 的事），你是把證據織成一個陪審團能夠捍衛的判決。每一句話都要禁得起 reviewer（對方律師）的交叉詰問。
Discussion is the closing argument of a trial: you don't restate the evidence (that's Results' job), you weave it into a verdict the jury can defend. Every sentence must survive the reviewer's (opposing counsel's) cross-examination.
Σ-走查

Σ-8 步寫完整 Discussion

Σ₁

蒸餾主結論為 1-3 句

第一段不要重貼 Results 的數字，直接寫「本研究在 X 樣本中發現 Y，並建立 Z 的新觀察」三句以內。如果寫不出來，回去再讀 Results。

Paragraph 1 doesn't repaste Results numbers — write "We found Y in X samples and established the new observation Z" in ≤3 sentences. If you can't, go reread Results.

Σ₂

與既有文獻對話

明確標出與哪幾篇 paper 的關係：「與 Smith 2023 一致」「延伸 Chen 2024 的 cell-line 觀察至 in vivo」「顛覆 Zhang 2022 的結論——可能因 cohort 差異」。引用具體文獻，不要籠統 "many studies"。

Specify the relationship paper by paper: "consistent with Smith 2023"; "extends Chen 2024's cell-line observation to in vivo"; "contradicts Zhang 2022 — possibly cohort-driven". Cite specifically; avoid vague "many studies".

Σ₃

提出機制 (標示 speculative)

可引用已知 pathway / cell type / 蛋白互動，但每一句都要有 hedge：「One plausible mechanism is…」「This may reflect…」。不要把推測呈現為已證實。

Cite known pathways / cell types / protein interactions but every sentence must hedge: "One plausible mechanism is…", "This may reflect…". Don't present speculation as proven.

Σ₄

校準動詞與證據強度

觀察性 → "associated"；介入性單中心 → "indicates"；多中心 RCT → "demonstrates"；in silico → "may"。Reviewer 第一件事就是檢查動詞 vs design 的一致性。

Observational → "associated"; single-center interventional → "indicates"; multicenter RCT → "demonstrates"; in silico → "may". Verb-design consistency is the first thing reviewers check.

Σ₅

寫臨床 / 生物意義

對醫師、病患、政策、後續研究有什麼意義？避免 overclaim：「these findings may inform」「warrant further investigation」比「will revolutionize」好得多。

Implications for clinicians, patients, policy, follow-up research. Avoid overclaim: "these findings may inform" or "warrant further investigation" beats "will revolutionize".

Σ₆

寫 3-5 條誠實 limitations

三層 honest：① 內部 (sample size、selection bias)、② 外部 (generalizability)、③ 方法學 (技術限制)。每條格式：「限制 + 為何重要 + 我們做了什麼緩解 + 未來該怎麼補」。

Three layers: ① internal (sample size, selection bias), ② external (generalizability), ③ methodological (technique). Each: "limitation + why it matters + mitigation we did + future fix".

Σ₇

未來方向 = 2-3 個具體下一步

不要寫「需要更多研究確認」（廢話），要寫「在 PDX models 進行 Gene X knockdown 驗證」、「擴展至 TCGA-BRCA 多中心 cohort 驗證」這種具體 actionable 句。

Don't write "more research is needed" (worthless), write "validate via Gene X knockdown in PDX models", "extend validation to multicenter TCGA-BRCA cohort" — concrete actionable items.

Σ₈

結論呼應 Intro aim

2-3 句結論，必須能與 Introduction 的 aim/hypothesis 1:1 呼應。如果 Intro 問「Gene X 是否與化療抗性相關？」，結論就要回「Yes/No，並且…」。錯位 = reviewer 立刻發現結構問題。

2-3-sentence conclusion that must 1:1 echo the Introduction's aim/hypothesis. If Intro asked "Is Gene X linked to chemoresistance?", the conclusion answers "Yes/No, and…". Mismatch = reviewer immediately spots structural issue.

專家辯論

Discussion 寫作的三個爭議

① Limitations 寫的是「誠實」還是「表演」？

Ioannidis 2007 JAMA 系統性審查發現，大部分 limitations 段落是 boilerplate 樣板（「樣本小、未來會做更多」）。是真誠實還是儀式性表演？立場一：誠實寫 = 削弱論文競爭力；立場二：表演式 limitations 反而傷信任。共識：3-5 條具體 + mitigation + future fix，不寫廢話。

Ioannidis 2007 JAMA's systematic review found most limitations sections are boilerplate ("small sample, will do more"). Genuine honesty or ritual performance? Position 1: honest writing weakens competitiveness; Position 2: performative limitations damage trust more. Consensus: 3-5 specific + mitigation + future fix, no boilerplate.

② Revision 時要不要引用 reviewer 自己的論文？

「Citation coercion」爭議：reviewer 暗示 / 明示作者引用其論文。COPE 2020 指引明確禁止 coercive citation。但作者經常猜「revision 引用 reviewer 一下會不會比較順」。共識：只有當該文獻 本來就應該 被引用時才補；若不相關，禮貌拒絕並向 editor 反映。

"Citation coercion" debate: reviewers hint / openly ask authors to cite their papers. COPE 2020 guidelines explicitly forbid coercive citation. But authors often wonder "would citing reviewer help my revision?". Consensus: add the citation only if the paper genuinely belongs there; if irrelevant, politely decline and flag the editor.

③ Future directions = 灌水還是真有價值？

部分編輯認為 future-directions 段是 paper 的「結尾儀式」，並不該佔太多篇幅。但好的 future directions 段可以替後來研究者鋪路、被高度引用。共識：2-3 個 具體 actionable 下一步，不要寫「needs more research」這種 cliché。

Some editors view future-directions as a closing ritual, not deserving much space. But strong future-directions paragraphs can pave the road for follow-up researchers and become highly cited. Consensus: 2-3 specific actionable next steps; never write "needs more research" — that's a cliché.

案例聚焦

兩個對照案例

🚨 WARNING：2020 Surgisphere 撤稿事件

Mehra 等 2020 在 Lancet 發表的 COVID-19 hydroxychloroquine 觀察性研究 Discussion 寫「provides decisive evidence against hydroxychloroquine」——一個觀察性 cohort + 來源可疑的資料庫。WHO 因此暫停全球 trial。13 天後 Lancet 撤稿。教訓：Discussion 的 overclaim 可以引發政策層級災難。動詞校準不是文字遊戲，是公衛責任。

Mehra et al. 2020 published a COVID-19 hydroxychloroquine observational study in Lancet whose Discussion stated "provides decisive evidence against hydroxychloroquine" — from an observational cohort using a database of dubious origin. WHO paused trials globally. 13 days later Lancet retracted it. Lesson: Discussion overclaim can trigger policy-level disasters. Verb calibration is not a word game; it's a public-health responsibility.

✨ MODEL：Sabeti Lab 2018 Lassa 論文

Siddle 等 2018 Nature；Discussion 明確區分 ecological 觀察（齧齒類為 reservoir 的證據強）與 human transmission 觀察（樣本有限、需更多 cohort）。每一段限制都標明 mitigation 與 future direction。被多本教科書引用為「Discussion 寫作教學範本」。語氣校準 + 三層 limitations + 與 prior literature 對話皆到位。

Siddle et al. 2018 Nature; the Discussion explicitly separates ecological observations (strong evidence for rodent reservoirs) from human-transmission observations (limited samples, needs more cohorts). Every limitation is paired with mitigation and future direction. Cited in multiple textbooks as a teaching exemplar of Discussion writing. Calibrated language + three-layer limitations + literature dialogue all done right.

常見陷阱

6 個 Discussion 典型錯誤

① 重述所有 Results

把 Results 的數字、表格、p 值再貼一遍——這不是 Discussion，是 Results 2.0。Discussion 只在第一段濃縮 1-3 句主結論，後續是「對話」。

Re-pasting Results' numbers, tables, p-values — this isn't Discussion, it's Results 2.0. Discussion distills to 1-3 sentences in paragraph 1; the rest is "dialogue".

② 觀察性研究寫「proves」/「demonstrates」

Reviewer 第一個畫紅的地方。觀察性 cohort 只能說 "associated with" / "suggests"；用 "proves" 表示作者不懂 study design 的證據力等級。

First red-line a reviewer makes. Observational cohorts only permit "associated with" / "suggests"; using "proves" signals the authors don't grasp evidence-tier hierarchy.

③ 模糊 limitations

「樣本太少」「未來會做更多」——廢話。Reviewer 一秒看穿。要寫：n=18 對某分析夠用、對 subgroup 不夠；我們用 bootstrap 估計 stability；未來擴展到 n≥100。

"Small sample", "will do more later" — boilerplate. Reviewers spot it in a second. Write instead: n=18 adequate for X analysis, underpowered for subgroup; we used bootstrap for stability; future cohort to n≥100.

④ 只引用自己 group 的論文

Discussion 引用 12 篇 paper，其中 8 篇來自同一個 PI——reviewer 立刻起疑（self-citation 偏見 / 缺乏領域對話）。應該引用各方主要 group 的代表作。

Discussion cites 12 papers, 8 from the same PI — reviewers immediately suspect self-citation bias / lack of field dialogue. Should cite key papers from various groups.

⑤ 沒提出任何機制

Discussion 全部在描述「什麼」(what)，沒有討論「為什麼」(why) 與「怎麼會這樣」(how)。即使是 speculative 機制也應提，並明確標示 speculative。

Discussion only describes "what" — no "why" or "how". Even speculative mechanisms should be proposed, explicitly flagged as speculative.

⑥ 結論與 Intro 矛盾

Intro aim：「探討 Gene X 與化療抗性的關係」，Conclusion 卻寫：「Gene X 可作為 prognostic biomarker」——結構錯位。結論必須 1:1 呼應 aim。

Intro aim: "investigate the relationship between Gene X and chemoresistance"; Conclusion: "Gene X can serve as a prognostic biomarker" — structural mismatch. Conclusion must 1:1 echo the aim.

對照範例

Discussion 句子對照

❌ Overclaim

「Our results prove Gene X causes resistance and will revolutionize TNBC therapy.」

"Our results prove Gene X causes resistance and will revolutionize TNBC therapy."

問題：(1) prove + causes = 因果語言但只是觀察性 cohort；(2) will revolutionize = 過度宣稱；(3) 沒有 limitations；(4) 沒有 mitigation。

Problems: (1) prove + causes = causal language from an observational cohort; (2) "will revolutionize" = overclaim; (3) no limitations; (4) no mitigation.

✅ Calibrated

「Our data show Gene X expression is significantly associated with chemoresistance (HR 1.8, 95% CI 1.2–2.7), consistent with Smith et al. 2023. However, observational design cannot establish causality; functional validation in PDX models remains necessary.」

"Our data show Gene X expression is significantly associated with chemoresistance (HR 1.8, 95% CI 1.2–2.7), consistent with Smith et al. 2023. However, observational design cannot establish causality; functional validation in PDX models remains necessary."

優點：(1) "associated" 符合觀察性設計；(2) 引用具體文獻 (Smith 2023)；(3) 明確指出 limitation (observational design)；(4) 列出 future fix (PDX validation)。

Strengths: (1) "associated" matches observational design; (2) cites specific paper (Smith 2023); (3) names a limitation explicitly (observational design); (4) lists a future fix (PDX validation).

反思題

送審前的 6 個自我提問

  1. 你的動詞跟你的 study design 對齊嗎？（觀察性別寫 proves！）Does your verb match your study design? (Observational ≠ "proves"!)
  2. 你寫了 3 條具體 limitations（不是樣板）嗎？每條都有 mitigation 與 future fix？Have you written 3 specific limitations (not boilerplate)? Each with mitigation and future fix?
  3. 結論與 Introduction 的 aim 是 1:1 呼應嗎？拿出 Intro 比一下。Is the conclusion 1:1 echo of your Intro's aim? Pull up the Intro and compare.
  4. 機制段落是否明確標示 "speculative" / "may" / "plausible"？Does the mechanism paragraph clearly flag "speculative" / "may" / "plausible"?
  5. 引用了至少 5 篇不同 group 的相關文獻，並標出 agreement / disagreement？Cited ≥5 papers from different groups, marked as agreement / disagreement?
  6. Future directions 是 2-3 個 actionable 句，不是「needs more research」？Are future directions 2-3 actionable sentences, not "needs more research"?
進階

進階補充

(a) Bradford Hill 因果準則 (1965 → 2025)

Hill 1965 Proc R Soc Med 9 條準則：strength、consistency、specificity、temporality、biological gradient (dose-response)、plausibility、coherence、experiment、analogy。使用 "causes" / "drives" 動詞前的 Discussion 內部檢查表：你的 cohort 滿足幾條？臨床流行病學論文應在 Discussion 段顯式逐條呈現（Fedak 2015 Emerg Themes Epidemiol 提出 Hill criteria 在現代資料的擴充版）。

Hill 1965 Proc R Soc Med 9 criteria: strength, consistency, specificity, temporality, biological gradient (dose-response), plausibility, coherence, experiment, analogy. Internal checklist before using "causes" / "drives" in Discussion: how many does your cohort satisfy? Clinical-epidemiology papers should walk through each in their Discussion (Fedak 2015 Emerg Themes Epidemiol expanded the framework for modern data).

(b) 陰性結果與 Registered Reports

傳統期刊偏好「正向結果」，導致 publication bias 與 file-drawer 問題。Registered Reports 機制：作者投稿研究計畫，期刊在實驗開始前承諾接受（無論結果正負）。Cortex (2013 啟動)、Royal Society Open Science、PLOS Biology、Nature Human Behaviour 等已有 RR 軌道。Discussion 中寫陰性結果應強調 power 計算、effect size 估計、預先 hypothesis (避免 HARKing 嫌疑)。

Traditional journals favor positive results, causing publication bias and file-drawer problems. Registered Reports: authors submit study plans, journal commits to publish before data collection regardless of outcome. Cortex (launched 2013), Royal Society Open Science, PLOS Biology, Nature Human Behaviour have RR tracks. For negative results, Discussion should emphasize power calculation, effect-size estimation, pre-specified hypothesis (avoiding HARKing suspicion).

(c) AI-輔助 hedging 2024-2026

2024-26 時代 Claude Sonnet 4.6 / GPT-5 / Gemini 2.5 等 LLM 可協助：① 校準動詞（"prove" → "suggest"）；② 平衡 hedging 與信心；③ 對照證據強度給建議。但無法取代研究者對 study design 證據力的判斷——AI 不知道你的 cohort 是觀察性還是 RCT、是否有 confounding。實務做法：寫完 draft → AI flag 過強動詞 → 研究者最終決定。NEJM 2024 政策：AI 不能列為作者；使用 LLM 須在 Methods 揭露。

In the 2024-26 era Claude Sonnet 4.6 / GPT-5 / Gemini 2.5 can assist with: ① verb calibration ("prove" → "suggest"); ② balancing hedging with confidence; ③ suggestions matched to evidence strength. But cannot replace researcher judgment about study-design evidence tier — AI doesn't know whether your cohort is observational or RCT, whether confounding is present. Practical workflow: write draft → AI flags overclaim verbs → researcher makes final call. NEJM 2024 policy: AI cannot be listed as author; LLM use must be disclosed in Methods.

歷史小註

Newton 與「Hypotheses non fingo」

Isaac Newton 1687 出版 Philosophiae Naturalis Principia Mathematica（《自然哲學的數學原理》），在每一段論證的結尾常加上「Hypotheses non fingo」——「我不臆造假設」。在 17 世紀，這是 Newton 對哲學圈批評他「沒解釋重力為什麼存在」的回應：他坦承他的數學定律無法解釋「機制」，只能描述「現象」。這是科學史上最早的 limitations 段落雛形——在 limitations 段這個寫作慣例還不存在的時代，Newton 已經主動為自己的理論畫出邊界。三百多年後，現代 Discussion 段裡的「this study cannot establish causality」其實是這句話的回聲。

Isaac Newton's 1687 Philosophiae Naturalis Principia Mathematica often ended an argument with "Hypotheses non fingo" — "I feign no hypotheses". In the 17th century this was Newton's reply to philosophers attacking him for "not explaining why gravity exists": he openly admitted his mathematical laws could describe phenomena but not mechanism. This is the earliest proto-limitations statement in scientific history — drawing a boundary around his own theory in an era before there was even such a writing convention. Over 300 years later, every "this study cannot establish causality" in a modern Discussion is an echo of that Latin phrase.

概覽

Discussion 是「對話」，不是「重述」

新手最常見的錯誤：把 Discussion 寫成 Results 的擴大版。Discussion 的真正任務是「把你的結果放回領域既有知識的脈絡裡」，回答四個問題：

  1. 主要發現是什麼？(1 段，不要超過)
  2. 與既有文獻一致嗎？不一致的話為什麼？
  3. 可能機制與意義？
  4. 限制與未來方向？

Beginner's most common mistake: writing Discussion as an expanded Results. The real job of Discussion is to put your results into the field's existing context, answering four questions:

  1. What is the main finding? (1 paragraph max)
  2. Is it consistent with prior literature? If not, why?
  3. Possible mechanism & significance?
  4. Limitations & future directions?
💡
結構口訣：Discussion 是倒漏斗——窄到寬。第一段最具體（重述主結論），最後一段最廣（對領域的意義、未來方向）。Introduction 與 Discussion 互為鏡像。 Structure mnemonic: Discussion is an inverted funnel — narrow to broad. First paragraph most specific (restate main finding), last paragraph broadest (implications for field, future directions). Introduction and Discussion are mirror images.
標準六段結構

一、Discussion 的標準六段

主要發現摘要

1-3 句話濃縮核心結果，不要重貼數字。例：「本研究在 18 例 TNBC 中首次描繪 chemotherapy 後的轉錄組重塑，鑑定 3 個化療抗性相關細胞態。」

1-3 sentences distilling core findings — no number copy-paste. Eg: "We profiled the post-chemo transcriptional remodeling of 18 TNBC samples and identified 3 chemo-resistance-associated cell states."

與既有文獻對話

「我們的結果與 X 一致 (Smith 2023)」「我們延伸了 Y 的觀察」「我們的數據顛覆了 Z 的結論——可能是因為 cohort/method 差異」。具體引用，不要籠統說 "many studies"。

"Our results agree with X (Smith 2023)"; "We extend Y's observation"; "Our data contradicts Z — possibly due to cohort / method differences." Cite specifically; don't say "many studies."

可能機制與生物意義

提出可能的解釋機制，明確標示「speculative」。可引用既知的 pathway / 細胞類型，但不要當成已證實。

Propose plausible mechanisms — flag them as speculative. You may cite known pathways / cell types but don't present them as proven.

臨床/應用意義

對醫師、患者、政策、後續研究有什麼意義？避免 overclaim：「these findings may inform」「warrant further investigation」比「will revolutionize」好得多。

Implications for clinicians, patients, policy, follow-up research. Avoid overclaim: "these findings may inform" or "warrant further investigation" beats "will revolutionize."

限制 (Limitations)

誠實列出 3-5 個真正的限制（不是套話）。寫法：「Limitation + 為何重要 + 我們做了什麼緩解 + 未來該怎麼補」。

Honestly list 3-5 real limitations (not boilerplate). Format: "Limitation + why it matters + what we did to mitigate + future fix."

結論與未來方向

2-3 句結論 + 2-3 個具體 next steps。結論要與 Introduction 的 aim 呼應。

2-3 sentence conclusion + 2-3 concrete next steps. Conclusion must echo the Introduction's aim.

Limitations

二、Limitations 三層寫法

新手把 limitations 寫成「樣本太少、未來會做更多」，審稿人秒看穿。專業作法分三層：

Beginners write "small sample, will do more next time" — reviewers see through it instantly. Professional version has three layers:

層級範圍範例
① 內部 研究設計本身的限制：sample size、selection bias、measurement errorStudy design limits: sample size, selection bias, measurement error 「樣本來自單一醫學中心，可能限制推論至其他族群」"Single-center cohort may limit generalizability"
② 外部 推廣/應用上的限制：generalizability, real-world translationGeneralizability and translation limits 「我們僅納入治療前樣本，無法評估治療反應的縱向變化」"We only included pre-treatment samples, so longitudinal treatment response cannot be assessed"
③ 方法學 技術/演算法/工具的限制Technique / algorithm / tool limits 「scRNA-seq dropouts 可能低估低表達基因；CITE-seq 蛋白驗證有助降低此風險」"scRNA-seq dropouts may underestimate low-expression genes; CITE-seq protein validation could mitigate"
⚠️
3 個被 reviewer 立刻盯住的「假 limitations」：
① 「樣本不夠」（為什麼不夠？n 多少才夠？）
② 「未來會做 in vivo 驗證」（沒講為什麼這次不做）
③ 「結果還需要更多研究確認」（廢話，所有研究都需要）
Three "fake limitations" reviewers spot instantly:
① "Sample size is small" (why is it small? what n is needed?)
② "Future work will include in vivo validation" (no rationale for omitting now)
③ "Findings need further confirmation" (useless — every study needs that)
範例對照

三、Discussion 段落對照

❌ Overclaim

「我們的結果證明 Gene X 是 TNBC 化療抗性的核心驅動因子，必將成為新藥標靶。」
(從相關性跳到因果，從一個 cohort 跳到「必將」——審稿人會用紅筆畫滿。)

"Our results prove Gene X is the central driver of chemo resistance in TNBC and will become a new drug target."
(Jumps from correlation to causation, from one cohort to "will" — reviewer will redline this.)

✅ 校準語氣

「我們的數據顯示 Gene X 表達與化療抗性顯著相關 (HR 1.8, 95% CI 1.2-2.7)，與 Smith 等 (2023) 的細胞株發現一致。然而，本研究為觀察性設計，無法確立因果關係；功能性驗證 (e.g. Gene X knockdown in PDX models) 仍是必要的下一步。」

"Our data show Gene X expression is significantly associated with chemoresistance (HR 1.8, 95% CI 1.2–2.7), consistent with cell-line findings by Smith et al. (2023). However, this observational design cannot establish causality; functional validation (e.g., Gene X knockdown in PDX models) remains a necessary next step."

❌ 假 limitations

「本研究有一些限制：樣本數較少、結果還需更多研究確認、未來會做更多分析。」

"This study has limitations: small sample size, results need confirmation, future work will do more analysis."

✅ 真 limitations

「本研究有三個主要限制。第一，n=18 對於 cell-state 分群已足夠，但對 subgroup 分析 (如 PD-L1+ vs PD-L1−) power 不足；我們透過 bootstrap (n=1000) 估計 stability。第二，所有樣本來自台大醫院 2024-2025 cohort，可能不代表其他種族與化療方案族群；驗證 cohort (TCGA-BRCA, METABRIC) 顯示一致趨勢。第三，scRNA-seq 對低表達 transcription factor 的偵測有 dropout 限制，未來可結合 ATAC-seq 或 CITE-seq 提升解析度。」

"This study has three main limitations. First, n=18 is adequate for cell-state clustering but underpowered for subgroup analysis (e.g., PD-L1+ vs PD-L1−); we estimated stability via bootstrap (n=1000). Second, samples are from a single 2024-2025 NTUH cohort and may not generalize across ethnicities or chemotherapy regimens; validation in TCGA-BRCA and METABRIC showed consistent trends. Third, scRNA-seq dropout limits detection of low-expression TFs; future work could integrate ATAC-seq or CITE-seq for finer resolution."

校準語氣 (Hedging)

四、Hedging：不過度也不退縮

學術寫作講究「校準的不確定性」。Hedging 過度→沒立場、沒說服力；Hedging 不足→overclaim、被審稿人抓。

Academic writing requires "calibrated uncertainty." Over-hedge → no stance, no persuasion. Under-hedge → overclaim, reviewer catches it.

證據強度動詞副詞 / 形容詞
非常強demonstrate, establish, showconclusively, robustly, clearly
強indicate, support, revealstrongly, consistently
中等suggest, imply, proposelikely, plausibly, often
弱may, might, couldpossibly, potentially, in principle
💡
校準準則：觀察性 = "associated with"；介入性單中心 = "indicates"；多中心 RCT = "demonstrates"；in silico 預測 = "may"。動詞要與 study design 的證據力相稱。 Calibration rule: observational = "associated with"; single-center interventional = "indicates"; multicenter RCT = "demonstrates"; in silico = "may." Match the verb to the evidence level of your study design.
